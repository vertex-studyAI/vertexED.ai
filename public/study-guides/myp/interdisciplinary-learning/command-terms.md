# MYP Interdisciplinary (IDL / IDU) Command Terms Reference

A consolidated guide to how every command term used in the public IDL mark schemes (N16, M17, M19, N19, N20, M21, N21, M22, M23, M24) is graded. Built from the verbatim command-term glossaries each MS prints, the rubrics that show how each verb is banded, and the "Key MS notes" that name caps and reward criteria. The IDL papers are not point-marked the way Sciences MYP papers are — every extended task is judged holistically against a markband. This document explains what each verb buys you, what gates entry to the top band, and what the MS systematically penalises.

---

## 1. Best-fit Approach — How IDL Marking Works

IDL papers are marked using a **"best fit" levelled markband** model, not point-marking. The MS instructs examiners (verbatim, repeated across sessions):

> "The examiner or teacher applying an assessment criterion must choose the achievement level that overall best matches the piece of work being marked. It is not necessary for every detailed aspect of an achievement level to be satisfied for that level to be awarded but it must reflect the balance of student achievement against the markband. For example, if student work matches two of the three requirements within a markband but one is seriously lacking, the student should be awarded for the strands that have been met well, but the mark awarded should be at the lower end of the markband to compensate for what is lacking in one strand. If the level of student work spans multiple markbands, compensation depends on the performance in the higher order skills." (M17 MS p.3 / M23 MS p.3 verbatim)

### What this means in practice

| Difference vs Sciences MYP | Implication for IDL answers |
|---|---|
| Bands are 0 / 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6 / 7–8 (8-mark) or 0 / 1–3 / 4–6 / 7–9 / 10–12 (12-mark) | You cannot scrape marks by listing facts — depth of treatment within each named bullet is what moves you up bands |
| The whole response is judged together | A weak strand drags the band; one strong strand alone cannot pull you to the top |
| "Best fit" allows compensation | If you nail two of three strands at a higher band, the examiner places you in the lower part of that higher band rather than dropping you a full band |
| Higher-order skills weighted at boundary | When the response sits between two bands, performance on the higher-order strand (synthesis, evaluation, reflection) decides which way it goes |
| "Highest level does not represent perfection" | You do not need to be flawless to earn 10–12 / 12 — you need to be operating at the explanation/synthesis level across the named strands |

### Communication tiebreaker (intra-band)

Most extended-task rubrics from N16 onward append the line: *"Within this markband, answers that communicate the ideas clearly and coherently will score higher."* (N16 Q3a, Q3b, Q4b, Q5, Q6 verbatim; N20 Q5; M22 throughout). Once the band is fixed, write-quality moves you up *within* the band.

## 2. The Verb Ladder Common to IDL

Every banded IDL question — across every session — uses some version of the same vertical ladder:

### Standard 4-band verb progression

| Band | Verb | What the answer looks like |
|---|---|---|
| Lowest | **State / Identify** | A bare claim with no reasoning. "Mathematics is used to monetize disinformation." |
| Lower-mid | **Outline** | A brief account naming the link. "Mathematics is used because brands pay per click, so user-count data is monetised." |
| Upper-mid | **Describe** | A detailed account with named features. "GDI estimates US$236.4 million per year flows to disinformation outlets — the figures are derived from a run-rate model based on monitored sites." |
| Top | **Explain / Justify / Evaluate** | Cause-and-effect with explicit reasoning, evidence linkage, and a conclusion that weighs the case. |

### Where the ladder shows up

| Session | Question(s) using the ladder explicitly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| M17 | Q1b, Q2a, Q2b, Q2c, Q4a, Q4b, Q6b, Q7 | states → describes → explains → justifies/evaluates |
| M19 | Q1c, Q2e, Q3, Q4, Q6, Q7 | states → outlines → describes → explains |
| N19 | All extended tasks (Q1b, Q2c, Q3, Q4, Q5, Q6, Q7) | identifies/states → outlines → describes → explains |
| N20 | Q1b, Q2b, Q3a, Q3b, Q4, Q5, Q6, Q7 | states → outlines → describes → explains/compares-and-contrasts |
| M21 | Q1b, Q2b, Q3, Q4, Q5a, Q5b, Q6, Q7 | states → outlines → describes → explains/develops/justifies |
| N21 | Q1b, Q1d, Q2b, Q3a, Q3b, Q4, Q5, Q6, Q7 | states → outlines → describes → explains |
| M22 | Q1b, Q2c, Q3, Q5, Q6 | outlines → describes → explains → explains in detail |
| M23 | Q1, Q2, Q3 (all 12-mark) | states → outlines → describes → explains |
| M24 | Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 | states → outlines → describes → explains |

### Practical translation

| If the rubric says... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "States" (band 1–3) | One sentence per bullet, no causation |
| "Outlines" (band 4–6) | One sentence per bullet plus a "because..." clause |
| "Describes" (band 7–9) | A short paragraph per bullet with named features and at least one piece of evidence |
| "Explains" (band 10–12) | A full paragraph per bullet with cause-and-effect, named subject concepts, evidence cited from the sources/PRM, and a justified/evaluative conclusion |

## 3. Per-Term Sections

### Identify

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Provide an answer from a number of possibilities. Recognize and state briefly a distinguishing fact or feature."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M21 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark allocation |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q4a | Identify four examples of disciplinary knowledge in a sustainable-living video, two from sciences and two from I&S | 1 mark per item; cap of 2 marks per discipline (forces 2 + 2 split) |
| M17 Q1a | Identify three different maps and their classification (Cartogram, Physical, Political, Satellite, Climate, Satirical, Exploration & land-claim) | 1 mark per correct identification, max 3 |
| M19 Q1a | Identify one source making the case for limited and one for unlimited freedom of speech (drag-and-drop) | 1 mark per correct placement; over-selection forfeits the mark for that perspective |
| N20 Q2a | Identify the mode of persuasion most evident in two protest posters (drag-and-drop) | 1 mark per correctly placed poster (Poster 1 = logos, Poster 2 = pathos) |
| M21 Q2a | Identify the author's purpose for two text selections (Entertain / Persuade / Describe / Inform) | 1 mark per correct match; Selection 1 = persuade, Selection 2 = inform |

**Top band:** All items correctly named or placed.

**Caps the answer:**
- Over-selection (placing more than one item where one is asked) forfeits the mark for that slot (M19 Q1a explicit).
- Discipline-balance caps (N16 Q4a: max 2 from each of Sciences and I&S — listing 4 from one discipline forfeits 2 marks).

**Common traps:**
- Describing the item instead of naming it.
- Adding explanation when only identification is asked (no penalty but wastes time).
- Missing the cap and putting all answers in one category.

### State

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M21, M22, M23 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark scheme |
|---|---|---|
| M19 Q1b | State the origin and the purpose of Source A and Source C | 1 mark each for Origin and 1 mark each for Purpose. Direct lifts from the source AND paraphrases both accepted (OWTTE) |
| M19 Q2b | State the relationship between % target achieved and average donation | "As one increases the other increases (positive correlation or directly proportional)" — 1 mark |

**At the lowest band of every banded rubric**, "states" denotes the floor — a bare claim with no reasoning. M21 Q5a band 1–3: *"states one argument supporting the drug company's point of view"* is the entry-level descriptor.

**Top band:** Direct, specific, no extras.

**Common traps:**
- Writing a paragraph for a 1-mark "state" question — wastes time and can introduce contradictions (CON).
- Being vague when a specific term is required.
- For "state the relationship" questions, omitting both directions (M19 Q2b uses "as one increases the other increases" — a single-direction phrasing also scores by ORA).

---

### Outline

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give a brief account or summary."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M22, M23 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | What earns the mark |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q5 | Outline social guidelines for the ReGen community council (15 marks) | Banded; format-following gated at 8 marks. Top band requires synthesis showing environmental + human-systems interconnectedness |
| N19 Q2a | Outline how the key concept *global interactions* supports your understanding of the alternative-milks quotation, using an example | Band 1 = outline the choice; Band 2 = outline the choice with a valid example |
| N19 Q2b | Outline how alternative milk has led to a change in dairy milk consumption | Same ladder: outline → outline + valid example |
| M17 Q6a | Outline why innovations match imagination or reason | 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 marks for outlining 1 / 2 / 3 / all 4 innovations. Placement is NOT credited — only the reasoning |
| M22 Q2a | Outline a way that the surveyor protected the Baobab using mathematical logic | Band 1 = outline; Band 2 = outline with an example. Reciting the trig method (given in the video) earns 0 |

**Top band:** Brief account with a named example.

**Caps the answer:**
- N16 Q5: format-following gated at 8/15 — guidelines that don't follow the *script/document format* of social guidelines are capped in the bottom two bands.
- M22 Q2a: descriptions of the method/workings that are already given in the source earn 0.
- M17 Q6a: only the *reasoning* for the placement scores, not the placement itself.

**Common traps:**
- Reciting source content rather than producing your own outline (M22 Q2a explicit zero).
- Listing without a worked example.
- Overshooting into "explain" territory when only "outline" is asked (no extra credit).

### Describe

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M22, M23 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark progression |
|---|---|---|
| N20 Q2b | Describe how the mode of persuasion in poster 2 affects the audience | Band 1 = states without references; Band 2 = outlines with 1 reference; Band 3 = describes with 2 references; Band 4 = describes with 3+ references |
| N21 Q1b | Describe two reasons why the cyclone poster is a primary source | Bands: 1 (1 stated) → 2 (2 stated OR 1 described) → 3 (1 stated + 1 described) → 4 (2 described) |

**Where it sits in the verb ladder:** Almost always the second-from-top rung of the band ladder. Bands typically read *states (1–2 / 1–3) → outlines (3–4 / 4–6) → describes (5–6 / 7–9) → explains (7–8 / 10–12)*.

**Top band:** Detailed account naming features, evidence and context — but not yet causal reasoning ("because").

**Caps the answer:**
- One-sided descriptions when both sides required (N19 Q2c positive AND negative effects of dairy industry; N20 Q2b explicit references count).
- Generic descriptions without source linkage (e.g. M24 Q1 — describing "tone" without naming *Style* / *Audience imperatives* is read down).

**Common traps:**
- Confusing "describe" with "explain" — describe says WHAT, explain says WHY.
- For multi-reference questions (N20 Q2b), stopping at 1 or 2 references when the rubric requires 3+ for top band.

---

### Explain

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give a detailed account including reasons or causes."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M21, M22, M23, M24 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Top-band requirement |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q3b | Explain how synthesis contributed to understanding of the key concept "systems" (10 marks) | Top band 8–10: explains use of both disciplines AND contribution to key concept; **single-discipline cap = 5 marks** |
| M17 Q1b | Explain two ways in which maps are changing in the 21st century (4 marks) | Top mark 4: describes two changes AND explains two implications |
| M17 Q4b | Explain how preparing the route combines mathematics and individuals & societies (10 marks) | **Cap of 2 marks if only one discipline is addressed** |
| N19 Q1a | Explain three ways the lab-grown experiment could be improved (6 marks; 2 per box) | Per box: mark 1 for identifying improvement, mark 2 only when *benefit of that specific improvement* is explained |
| N19 Q1b | Explain one strength and one weakness of using lab-grown foods to reduce world hunger (4 marks) | Mark 4 requires reason for BOTH strength AND weakness |
| N21 Q1d | Explain how *global interactions* is demonstrated in the cyclone poster (3 marks) | 1 = outline; 2 = describe; 3 = explain |
| N21 Q5 | Explain how skills from L&L and I&S could be synthesised to produce an effective podcast (8 marks) | Top band 7–8: explains both disciplines AND provides conclusion explicitly referring to related concepts |
| M22 Q1b | Explain how *global interactions* helps us understand the cultural impact of removing Baobab trees (6 marks) | Example from Reference 1 mandatory at every band |
| M24 Q2 | Explain how funding, technology and language contribute to spread of disinformation (12 marks) | Top band 10–12: extensive PRM use AND explicit + accurate use of subject concepts |

**Top band:** Cause-and-effect reasoning with named subject concepts AND explicit evidence linkage. The single most important discriminator between *describe* (band 7–9) and *explain* (band 10–12) is whether the response moves from "what happens" to "why it happens, mechanically, with named concepts and evidence."

**Caps the answer:**
- **Single-discipline cap** (recurring): N16 Q3b max 5/10; M17 Q4b max 2/10; M22 Q3 (synthesis required) caps mid-band.
- **Missing benefit / missing reason** (N19 Q1a, Q1b): identifying without explaining the benefit/reason holds you at half-marks.
- **Missing example** (M22 Q1b — example from Reference 1 mandatory at every band; without it, response cannot enter that band).

**Common traps:**
- Listing improvements/causes without saying why each *works*.
- One-discipline answers — explain questions with synthesis demands punish single-discipline rigour heavily.
- Skipping the named concept — top band requires explicit naming of subject concepts (e.g. *Patterns*, *Quantity*, *Audience imperatives*, *Point of view*) per M24 MS.

### Compare

**MS definition (in IDL papers, "Compare" appears alongside "Compare and contrast"):** *"Give an account of the similarities and differences between two (or more) items or situations, referring to both (all) of them throughout."* (Note: IDL MS treats Compare as the same construct as Compare and Contrast — see next section.)

**How IDL applies it (standalone Compare):**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark allocation |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q1a | Compare the graphs generated by three CO2 trials (4 marks) | Bands 1–3 (max 3) award point-by-point claims; **mark 4 requires the explicit comparative statement** linking atmospheric vs above-atmospheric CO2 levels |

**Top band:** Mark 4 entered when the comparative statement is made explicit — not just two parallel descriptions.

**Common traps:**
- Treating compare as a list of features per item without explicit comparative language ("more than", "compared with", "whereas").
- The implicit-vs-explicit distinction matters — N16 Q1a allows the comparison to be implicit in wording but rewards it being explicit.

---

### Compare and Contrast

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give an account of the similarities and differences between two (or more) items or situations, referring to both (all) of them throughout."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, N21 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Banding gate |
|---|---|---|
| M17 Q3a | Compare and contrast Mercator and Gall-Peters projections (4 marks) | Cap of 2 each for similarities and differences. Repeated similar features count once; converse statements count once |
| M21 Q1a | Compare and contrast the scientific method used in two diabetes trials (6 marks) | 1 mark per similarity (max 3) + 1 mark per difference (max 3); referring to "use of insulin" or "treatment of diabetes" disallowed (given in stem) |
| N20 Q4 | Compare and contrast two references on Olympic protest (12 marks) | Top band 10–12 requires the *command term itself* to be enacted — side-by-side comparison referring to both sources throughout, not sequential descriptions |
| N21 Q4 | Compare and contrast two Chernobyl articles (12 marks) | **Max 3 marks if only comparisons OR only contrasts**; **max 2 marks if comparisons/contrasts not linked to both articles** (i.e. two separate lists). Top band requires 4 elements (language, style, origin, purpose) |
| M23 Q1 | Compare and contrast synthesis of mathematics and science in two car brochures (12 marks) | **Auto-zero on first two strands** if response compares the cars themselves rather than how the brochures synthesise maths + science. **Capped at 1–3 for comparison strands** if response is two separate lists |

**Top band:** The student actually compares and contrasts (referring to both items throughout) — this is the band 10–12 entry criterion. Bands below describe each item in turn.

**Caps the answer:**
- **One-sided** (only similarities OR only differences): N21 Q4 max 3/12; M21 Q1a halved (max 3 of 6).
- **Two separate lists not linked**: N21 Q4 max 2/12; M23 Q1 capped at 1–3 for the comparison strands.
- **Off-task** (M23 Q1: comparing the cars instead of how the brochures use maths + science): auto-zero on first two strands.

**Common traps:**
- Writing a paragraph on item A then a paragraph on item B without linking sentences — this is "two separate lists" and caps below the top band.
- Repeating the same point with slight variation (M17 Q3a explicit: "They both have green. They both have blue." counts once).
- Counting converse statements as two (M17 Q3a: "South America larger on Gall-Peters / North America smaller on Gall-Peters" = one mark, not two).

### Construct

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Display information in a diagrammatic or logical form."* (M19, N19, N20 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark scheme |
|---|---|---|
| M19 Q2a | Construct a scatter diagram for % target achieved vs average donation (4 marks) | 1 mark each for: x-axis label "% of target achieved"; y-axis label "Average donation in $"; three points plotted correctly; equal intervals from (0,0). Axes can be labelled either way around |

**Top band:** All four scoring elements present — labels with units, accurate plotting, equal-interval scaling.

**Common traps:**
- Missing units on axis labels.
- Uneven interval scaling.
- "(0,0)" requirement is about equal-interval scaling, not data plotting — students sometimes try to plot a fictional origin point.

### Estimate

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Obtain an approximate value."* (M19, N19 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark scheme |
|---|---|---|
| M19 Q2c | Estimate the average size of donation for 50% of the target achieved (1 mark) | Accept answers in the strict open interval $30 < Donation < $35 (boundaries excluded). The answer must include the $ sign or "dollars" |

**Top band:** Single value within the named interval, with units.

**Common traps:**
- Missing the unit (M19 Q2c — strict requirement).
- Boundary values excluded — $30 or $35 are wrong.
- Reading off the line of best fit incorrectly.

### Justify

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. (See also "Explain".)"* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M21 glossaries; N21 explicitly cross-references Explain)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Banding gate |
|---|---|---|
| M17 Q1c | Justify your opinion on the objectivity of maps (8 marks) | Source-count gate: <1 source = max 2; ≥1 = max 4; ≥2 = max 6; ≥3 + outside info = up to 8. **Top band rewards material from beyond the supplied sources** |
| M17 Q2a | Justify one mathematical concept required in navigation (3 marks) | Band 3 (top): justifies with a clear example of why the concept is required. *Just selecting a concept does not merit a mark* |
| M19 Q1c | Justify one limitation of Source C (4 marks) | Band 1–2: describes + attempts evidence; Band 3–4: explains + uses evidence directly. Limitation must be anchored in Source C's own evidence |
| M17 Q4a | Prepare and justify your documentary route (20 marks) | Verb ladder *states → describes → explains → justifies*. Top band 16–20 requires evidence from Source 7 for **every** justification |
| N19 Q3 | Justify your dish choice to a restaurant manager (8 marks) | Top band 7–8: explains health AND ethical reasons in comparison to other dishes, with PRM evidence. *In comparison to the other dishes* is a band-3+ requirement |
| N20 Q1a | Mathematically justify the claim that males/females will run the same 100m time (2 marks) | Band 1 (1 mark) = "general evidence from the data"; Band 2 (2 marks) = **"explicit evidence from the data"** — specific values, trends, or model outputs |
| N20 Q5 | Justify which city is the best Olympic host applicant (12 marks) | **Cap of 6 marks** if only one city referenced; **cap of 3 marks** if no city chosen. All three implication axes (financial, legacy, inclusivity) required at 7+ |
| M21 Q3 | Justify which country has the best healthcare system (8 marks) | Each indicator worth 2 marks. Top band 7–8: explains all 4 indicators justifying choice |
| M23 Q2 | Justify the best transport-electrification scenario (12 marks) | Top band 10–12 requires mathematical AND scientific reasoning for BOTH choice and counter-argument. Mathematical calculations themselves are not credited — only the *understanding* of mathematical concepts |

**Top band:** Reasoning supported by **explicit, named evidence** plus a clear committed position (which city, which scenario, which side).

**Caps the answer:**
- No commitment to a position (N20 Q5 max 3/12 if no city chosen).
- One-sided coverage when both sides required (N20 Q5 max 6/12 if one city only).
- Source-count thresholds (M17 Q1c — fewer than 3 sources caps at 6/8).
- "General" rather than "explicit" evidence (N20 Q1a — discriminator between mark 1 and mark 2).
- Bald selection without reasoning (M17 Q2a explicit zero for selection only).

**Common traps:**
- Repeating the question stem instead of giving evidence.
- Discussing pros/cons when the question asks whether something *should be* the case (M21 Q6 explicit off-task warning).
- Writing about the evidence in general terms instead of citing specific values, trends, quotations.

### Suggest

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Propose a solution, hypothesis or other possible answer."* (M19, M22 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Mark scheme |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q1c | Suggest two changes to the experiment to study animal life ethically (2 marks) | 1 mark each from a list of 6 acceptable bullets. Rewards transfer of design from plants to animals with ethical/welfare adaptations |
| M19 Q2d | Suggest why you would not predict donations at 80% target (1 mark) | "You do not know if data points will continue in the same pattern. Extrapolation is unreliable." WTTE accepted |

**Top band:** A reasonable, scientifically/disciplinarily sound proposal that transfers logic from the source to a new context.

**Common traps:**
- Generic answers (e.g. "it would help" — too vague).
- Re-stating information already given in the question.

### Evaluate

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Make an appraisal by weighing up the strengths and limitations."* (M17, N20, M21, N21 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Top-band requirement |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q1b | Evaluate the experimental method (8 marks) | "More than 4 marks requires improvements." Top band 7–8: 4 issues + 2 improvements. Identifying issues without suggesting fixes caps at 4 |
| N16 Q3a | Evaluate impact of farmed bluefin tuna on sustainability — academic response (15 marks) | All 4 sub-bullets must be addressed AND anchored in source examples for top band. Band 1–3 explicitly reserved for responses with **no source evidence** |
| N16 Q6 | Evaluate how sciences and I&S help understand sustainability (16 marks) | Two axes: discipline coverage (science / I&S / interdisciplinary) AND number of features of sustainability (1 → 2 → 3 → 4 across bands) |
| M17 Q2c | Evaluate the Sun stick method of navigation (8 marks) | Two parallel strands separated by a dotted line: method strand AND mathematical-accuracy/real-life strand. Top band requires explicit evaluative conclusion on BOTH strands |
| M17 Q6b | Evaluate the role of imagination and reason in exploration (16 marks) | Top band uniquely requires BOTH benefits AND limitations across BOTH imagination AND reason — all four cells, plus an evaluative conclusion |
| M19 Q7 | Evaluate how freedom of speech is important to facilitate social change (8 marks) | Coverage banding (1 → 2 → 3 → 4 of: strengths, limitations, service-as-action example, evaluative conclusion) |
| N19 Q7 | Evaluate your current eating habits (8 marks) | Top band 7–8: explains all four (strengths, weaknesses, evidence, evaluative conclusion). Evidence is from the *examination* (not just PRM) |
| N20 Q3b | Evaluate how your poster synthesises maths and L&L (8 marks) | Critique-depth banding: "makes a statement → attempts to critique → partially critiques → fully critiques", tied to count of bullets covered (1/2/3/4) |
| N20 Q6 | "All sports must be popular" — evaluate (10 marks) | All three bullets (reasons, synthesis difference, conclusion); only top band requires *justified* conclusion |
| N21 Q2b | Evaluate the impact of the cyclone poster on the audience (8 marks) | **Max 2 marks if only strengths OR only limitations**. Evidence-count gate: ≥1 / ≥2 / ≥3 pieces required across the bands |
| N21 Q6 | Evaluate the synthesis of L&L and I&S to produce an effective podcast (8 marks) | Top band 7–8: explains both + evaluative conclusion |

**Top band:** Both strengths AND limitations covered with specific evidence, plus an **evaluative conclusion** that weighs the case rather than restating.

**Caps the answer:**
- **One-sided** (only strengths or only limitations): N21 Q2b max 2/8; recurring across N20, M21.
- **No improvements / no conclusion**: N16 Q1b cap at 4 if no improvements; N20 Q1b 1–2 band if no conclusion.
- **No source/PRM evidence**: N16 Q3a band 1–3 reserved for evidence-free responses.
- **Evaluative conclusion missing**: top bands across N19, N20, N21 reserved for responses with evaluative (not just stated) conclusions.

**Common traps:**
- Restating evidence instead of weighing it.
- Listing benefits and limitations without drawing them together.
- Treating "evaluate" as a synonym of "discuss" — IDL specifically requires *appraisal* with a final committed judgement.

### Analyse

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Break down in order to bring out the essential elements or structure. To identify parts and relationships, and to interpret information to reach conclusions."* (N19, N20, M21 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Banding gate |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q2b | Analyse the impact of fishing on sustainability of marine life (12 marks) | SWOT-style: strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats + conclusion. Coverage banding: 1 → 2 → 3 → all 4. Evidence-count gate: ≥1 quotation → ≥2 → ≥3 across bands |
| N21 Q3b | Analyse how you have synthesised point of view and culture (8 marks) | Top band 7–8: explains both + conclusion explicitly referring to related concepts |
| M21 Q5b | Analyse your synthesis of L&L and sciences in 5a (8 marks) | Analysis must relate to candidate's *own* synthesis in 5a — generic content is ignored. Top band: conclusion explicitly refers to the synthesis |

**Top band:** Breakdown into named parts/relationships AND interpretation drawing them together to a conclusion. Reflective Analyse questions (N21 Q3b, M21 Q5b) require the analysis to be of the candidate's *own* prior work.

**Caps the answer:**
- One-sided coverage in SWOT-style analyses (N16 Q2b — top band requires all 4 SWOT elements).
- Generic analysis not anchored to the candidate's own work (M21 Q5b explicit: anything not relating to it is ignored).
- Evidence-count thresholds (N16 Q2b — fewer than 3 quotations caps below top band).

**Common traps:**
- Treating Analyse as describe-with-extra-words.
- For reflective Analyse, drifting into general theory instead of evaluating the specific synthesis the student produced.

---

### Discuss

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Offer a considered and balanced review that includes a range of arguments, factors or hypotheses. Opinions or conclusions should be presented clearly and supported by appropriate evidence."* (M17, M22, M23 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Top-band requirement |
|---|---|---|
| M17 Q3b | Discuss how the Gall-Peters projection represents scientific and technical innovation (16 marks) | **Cap of 8 marks if response only discusses why or why not it is innovative** (must do both). Both disciplines required for top two bands. Evidence count: 0/1/≥2/≥3 extensive pieces |
| N19 Q2c | Discuss the effects of the dairy industry on society — positive and negative (6 marks) | Both sides required at every active band. *States → describes → explains* ladder |
| M22 Q2c | Discuss strengths and weaknesses of mathematical representation of the protected zone (6 marks) | Both strengths AND weaknesses at every band; ladder *outlines → describes → explains* |
| M22 Q2b | Discuss the rounding and significant figures (2 marks) | Mark 1 for noting 11.9 m is half of 23.8 m (rounded to 1 s.f.); mark 2 for noting the model excludes the surveyor's own height |
| M23 Q3 | Discuss challenges and solutions for the self-employed delivery driver switching to electric (12 marks) | "Best fit each strand separately and then best fit all three strands." **Top band:** Balanced consideration of multiple positions with evidence for each, plus a clear conclusion or position.

**Caps the answer:**
- **One-sidedness** (M17 Q3b max 8/16 if only one side; N19 Q2c — both sides required at every active band).
- **No PRM/source evidence** for explicit-evidence questions.
- **Generic reference** to the in-question stimulus where PRM citation is required (M23 Q3 explicit: "Reference to the delivery driver does not gain credit for the third strand as reference to the pre-release material").

**Common traps:**
- Treating Discuss as a one-sided argument.
- Drifting into Evaluate (committed judgement) when Discuss only requires balanced review.
- Using only the in-question stimulus when PRM citation is the criterion.

### Synthesise / Synthesize

(IDL papers do not always print a Synthesise glossary entry — but the term appears as a *task verb* in question stems, especially in M22 Q3, M24 Q3.)

**Working definition (drawn from rubric language):** Bring together knowledge and methods from two disciplines to produce a coherent interdisciplinary account or product. Synthesis is more than mentioning both disciplines — the disciplines must operate together to do the explanatory work.

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Top-band requirement |
|---|---|---|
| M22 Q3 | Synthesise the cultural impact of removing Baobab trees on the larger human community (12 marks) | Top band 10–12: "explains in detail" how disciplinary knowledge of Sciences AND I&S helps us understand the cultural impact, with example from Reference 1. Single-discipline response caps mid-band |
| M24 Q1 | Discuss how mathematics and L&L can be used to monetize disinformation (12 marks) | Three strands: maths contribution, L&L contribution, AND synthesis impact. Top band 10–12 requires explicit and accurate naming of subject concepts (e.g. *Quantity*, *Models*, *Audience imperatives*, *Purpose*) |
| M24 Q3 | Synthesise mathematics and L&L in an article on the spread of news (12 marks) | Top band 10–12: explains using BOTH disciplines AND extensive PRM use. Article structure NOT credited — credit is for synthesis + explicit point of view + use of references |

**Top band:** Both disciplines named, both doing real explanatory work, with named subject concepts (*Quantity*, *Patterns*, *Models* for maths; *Audience imperatives*, *Purpose*, *Style* for L&L) plus extensive use of multiple references.

**Caps the answer:**
- **Single-discipline** (M22 Q3 mid-band cap; M24 Q3 — band 1–3 cap if only one discipline used to build point of view).
- **Concepts not named** (M24 top band requires explicit and accurate use of subject concepts; implicit reasoning is read down).
- **Single-reference** ("extensive" PRM use in M24 means *multiple* references woven through, not just one).

**Common traps:**
- Listing facts from each discipline side-by-side without showing how they combine.
- Using disciplinary vocabulary loosely (saying "trends" rather than naming *Patterns*).
- Treating PRM as a checklist (one reference per strand) instead of weaving multiple references into single arguments.

### To what extent

**MS definition (verbatim):** *"Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument."* (M17, M19, N19, N20, M21, N21 glossaries)

**How IDL applies it:**

| Session / Q | Worked example | Top-band requirement |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q4b | To what extent does the video synthesise sciences and I&S to inform about sustainable living (16 marks) | Top band 13–16 requires ALL of: benefits of synthesis, limitations of synthesis, evidence from source, evaluative conclusion. Most perceptive answers comment on how the video could be improved |
| M17 Q7 | To what extent does an innovation demonstrate progress in exploration (10 marks) | "Adaptation OR ingenuity" → "adaptation AND ingenuity" verb-coverage gate. Top band requires evidence from beyond the PRM, plus a thorough conclusion |
| M19 Q6 | To what extent can someone know the truth from one source (12 marks) | Verb ladder *state → outline → describe → explain*. Top band 10–12: evidence **explicitly linked** to PRM on both sides; conclusion **justified** (not merely present) |
| N19 Q6 | To what extent should scientists who develop technologies with destructive potential be celebrated (12 marks) | Bands 1–3 and 4–6 require *"a conclusion"*; bands 7–9 and 10–12 require an **evaluative conclusion**. Both sides required at every active band |
| N20 Q1b | To what extent can mathematical models predict future winning times (8 marks) | Discriminator 1–2 → 3–4 = appearance of any conclusion attempt; 3–4 → 5–6 = integration of evidence; 5–6 → 7–8 = evidence **explicitly linked** AND conclusion **justified** |
| N20 Q7 | To what extent can an Olympic legacy endure (8 marks) | Same structure as N20 Q1b. Both sustainable and not-sustainable sides required throughout |
| M21 Q6 | To what extent is healthcare a right (12 marks) | **Cap of 6 marks if only one side discussed**. Off-task warning: must address whether healthcare *should* be a right, not pros/cons of it being a right. Top band 10–12: evaluative conclusion |
| N21 Q7 | To what extent do you support the construction of the nuclear power plant in your local town (12 marks) | Top band 10–12: evidence **explicitly linked**, conclusion **justified** |

**Top band:** Both sides argued with explicitly linked evidence, plus a *justified* / *evaluative* conclusion that weighs the case rather than restating it.

**Caps the answer:**
- **One-sided** (M21 Q6 max 6/12; N19 Q6 — both sides required at every active band).
- **Off-task framing** (M21 Q6 explicit: discussing pros/cons of healthcare as a right is off-task — the question asks whether it *should be* a right).
- **No conclusion** at lower bands; **stated rather than justified/evaluative** conclusion at top bands.
- **General rather than explicit evidence linkage** (N20 Q1b/Q7 explicit discriminator).

**Common traps:**
- Treating "to what extent" as "explain" — answers must commit to a position on the spectrum (fully / partly / not at all).
- Listing pros/cons without interpreting where the balance falls.
- Generic conclusions ("there are arguments on both sides") — top band requires a justified verdict.

## 4. Synthesis-Specific IDL Verbs

IDL has compound demands that no single MYP subject paper has — these deserve their own treatment.

### "Synthesise the disciplines"

The core IDL skill. The MS rewards this when **both disciplines are doing explanatory work** in the same argument, not when they appear in adjacent paragraphs.

**What it looks like across sessions:**

| Session / Q | Required synthesis | What earns top band |
|---|---|---|
| N16 Q3b | Sciences + I&S synthesised in academic response on bluefin tuna; understanding of key concept *systems* | "A maximum of 5 marks can be given if only one of the disciplines from science and I&S has been mentioned." Balance + explicit reflection on the key concept |
| N16 Q5 | Sciences + I&S in social guidelines for ReGen community | Synthesis must show environmental + human-systems interconnectedness |
| M17 Q4b | Mathematics + I&S in documentary route preparation | "Maximum of 2 marks can be awarded if only one discipline is addressed" |
| N20 Q3a | Mathematics + L&L (modes of persuasion) in Olympic protest poster | All four areas (modes of persuasion, L&L, mathematics, PRM evidence) used effectively |
| M22 Q3 | Sciences + I&S on cultural impact of Baobab removal | "Explains in detail" how disciplinary knowledge of BOTH disciplines helps understand the cultural impact |
| M23 Q1 | Mathematics + Sciences in synthesis of two car brochures | Direct compare-and-contrast across both brochures, NOT separate paragraphs |
| M23 Q2 | Mathematics AND Sciences for both choice and counter-argument | Top band requires AND for both strands |
| M24 Q1, Q2, Q3 | Mathematics + L&L throughout | Subject concepts must be explicitly named (e.g. *Quantity*, *Audience imperatives*) |

**Caps that recur:**
- **Single-discipline cap** at half-marks or mid-band — every session uses some version of this.
- **AND vs OR**: in upper bands the rubric says "mathematical AND scientific reasoning"; lower bands accept OR. Reaching the top requires both disciplines doing the work simultaneously.

### "Demonstrate interdisciplinary understanding"

A reflective verb appearing in N16 Q7 and implicitly in M22 Q6, M24 Q4. The student must show that their understanding goes beyond what either discipline could produce alone.

**Top-band cues:**
- Naming **how** the disciplines complement / contradict / extend each other.
- Identifying gaps that one discipline reveals in the other.
- Articulating insights that depend on the *combination*.

### "Reflect on the development of interdisciplinary understanding"

The metacognitive task — N16 Q7, M22 Q6, M24 Q4. The MS gates these on:

| Element | What the rubric requires |
|---|---|
| Source/PRM reference | At least one source explicitly cited (N16 Q7) |
| Perspectives / arguments / evidence | Considered with regard to the integrating context (sustainability, online world, etc.) |
| Examination questions | Specifically named (not just "the questions") |
| Classroom preparation | Direct reference to discussions / preparation (N16 Q7) |
| Own work | Example from the candidate's own response in Q3, Q4 or Q5 (M22 Q6) |
| Audience adaptation | Style adjusted to named audience (M24 Q4 — younger students) |

**Top band entry:** Coverage of all required bullets, addressed in a "thorough and nuanced manner" (N16 Q7) or with extensive PRM use (M24 Q4) — and **specifically directed to development of interdisciplinary understanding**, not generic reflection on exam content.

## 7. Final Tips for IDL Exam Success

1. **Read the rubric, not just the question.** Bullet points in the question stem are the band gates — addressing all of them is non-negotiable for the top band.

2. **Always cover both sides.** "Both strengths AND limitations", "both for AND against", "both sides", "comparisons AND contrasts". One-sidedness is the single most common cap.

3. **Name both disciplines explicitly.** Single-discipline caps recur in every session. If the question is interdisciplinary, both disciplines must be named *and* doing explanatory work.

4. **Cite evidence specifically.** "General evidence" caps mid-band; "explicit evidence from the data" / "explicitly linked evidence" reaches top band. Use figures, quotations, named references.

5. **Name subject concepts.** Especially in M24, the top band requires "explicit and accurate use of subject concepts" — *Quantity*, *Patterns*, *Models* for maths; *Audience imperatives*, *Purpose*, *Style*, *Point of view* for L&L; key concepts (*systems*, *global interactions*) where flagged.

6. **Always conclude — and justify the conclusion.** The discriminator between bands 7–9 and 10–12 across most sessions is whether the conclusion is *evaluative* / *justified* (weighs the case) rather than merely stated.

7. **Stay on-task.** "To what extent should X" is not the same as "pros and cons of X" (M21 Q6 explicit). Reflective questions are about *your* development, not the topic in general.

8. **Sustain the role-play frame.** When given a role (drug-company representative, ReGen council member, child narrator, government official), drift out of voice = lose marks.

9. **Use both response modes when both are available.** Canvas + text-box questions cap at 3 if only one mode is used (M19 Q5a).

10. **Reference the named PRM, not just the in-question stimulus.** M23 Q3 explicit: "Reference to the delivery driver does not gain credit for the third strand as reference to the pre-release material."

11. **For Compare and contrast, do both throughout.** Two separate paragraphs (one per item) cap at the lower bands — link comparators throughout the response.

12. **For "Identify" with caps, respect the cap.** N16 Q4a forces 2 from each discipline; M19 Q1a forfeits the mark if you place more than one source per perspective.

13. **The verb in your answer should match the verb in the rubric.** If the band asks for "explains", make sure your answer actually explains (with cause-and-effect language: because, therefore, so that, which leads to). If it asks for "describes", give detailed features without necessarily reasoning about why.

14. **Do not penalise yourself by writing too much for low-mark questions.** A 1-mark "state" question only needs one sentence — long answers risk contradictions (CON) and waste time.

---

**Remember:** IDL marking is *holistic best fit*. The examiner reads your response as a whole and matches it to the band that best describes its overall character. Your job is to make that overall character unambiguous: cover every bullet, name both disciplines, cite specific evidence, and end with a conclusion that takes a position. Do that and you exit the lower bands automatically.
