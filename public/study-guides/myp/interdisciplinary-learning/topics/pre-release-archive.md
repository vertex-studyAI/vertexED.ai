### May 2026 — Identity: nature vs. nurture

- Source URL: https://idprm.ibo.org/index.html#/English
- Theme: Whether identity is shaped more by genetics or by environment, with sub-strands on diet and body typology. Anchored by twin-study evidence and the question of whether names (nominative determinism) or food choices (ultra-processed foods) measurably alter life outcomes.
- Reference list:
  1. Nature and nurture video — prose transcript (1:28)
  2. Nominative determinism video — prose transcript (1:40)
  3. Twin study summary: genetic similarities for specific human traits — static image (meta-analysis chart)
  4. Blog about twins separated at birth — static image
  5. Impact of ultra-processed food (UPF) on health — prose + 3 graphs (NOVA classification, Statistics Canada CCHS 2015 data)
  6. Newsweek, *I ate ultra-processed foods, my twin didn't* by Aimee Kingston (27 Jun 2023) — full prose article
  7. Somatotyping — static images (diagram + table)
  8. Profiles of aspiring athletes — interactive widget
- Highlights:
  - "Winner vs Loser" Chicago Tribune anecdote — two brothers whose life paths inverted their names
  - NOVA 4-tier food classification (unprocessed → ultra-processed)
  - Twins UK / ZOE Predict / Prof. Tim Spector at King's College London (UPF study)
  - Statistics Canada CCHS 2015 — n=13,608 (ages 19+) and n=20,080 (ages 2+)
  - Somatotypes (ectomorph/mesomorph/endomorph) link to athlete profiles widget

- Reference list:

  3. Digital footprint: What is it and why should I care? — video transcript (1:30)

## Patterns across the archive

**Reference-type distribution (across 7 sessions, 45 references total):**

| Type | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Interactive widget | 22 | Adobe Animate HTML5 — dominant medium from M24 onwards |
| Video transcript (prose) | 9 | Always Ross Titshall on Vimeo; 1:15–3:04 typical length |
| Static image | 10 | Infographics, posters, perspectives diagrams |
| Long-form prose article | 4 | M23 Ref 2, N24 Ref 4 (Joe McCarthy), N25 Ref 3 (Nurman), M26 Ref 6 (Kingston) |

**Discipline pairings observed:**
- Only M24 PRM names its disciplines explicitly (Ref 8 = Mathematics + Language & Literature)
- M23 mark scheme retroactively labels the pair Mathematics + Sciences
- Other PRMs leave the pair to be inferred from data-rich Refs (graphs, statistics) vs prose/literature Refs

**Recurring "shapes":**
- Every PRM has at least one **perspectives / lenses** reference (M23 Ref 5, N23 Refs 3 & 6, M24 Ref 7, M25 Ref 3, N24 Ref 3, N25 Refs 3-5, M26 Refs 4 & 8)
- Every PRM since N23 contains at least one **video transcript** — usually Reference 1 or Reference 2; always a Ross Titshall production
- M24 onwards: every session has a **named long-form article** (Joe McCarthy / Aimee Kingston / Kadir Nurman biography)
- Every PRM has 5–8 references; the median is 6
- Glossaries appear inline from N24 onwards — a convention that helps EAL students

**Recency / evolution of PRM design:**
- **M23** is the sparsest (5 refs, only one video, no glossary, no named article) — closest to a traditional "stimulus pack"
- **N23** introduces an explicit *lenses* widget (Ref 3) and pairs it with a *perspectives* image (Ref 6) — the first session to separate the two
- **M24** is the most reference-dense (8) and the only session that explicitly names its disciplines inside the PRM itself (Ref 8)
- **N24 → M26** stabilises around 6–8 references with at least one widget, one video, one long article, and one perspectives/lenses element
- Interactive widgets shift from being one-of-five (M23) to dominating the pack (M24 has 6 of 8 as widgets)

## Theme clusters

- **Sustainability & systems** — M23 (electric vehicles, lithium-ion supply chain). The only fully sustainability-coded session; M26's UPF strand touches systems thinking from a health angle.
- **Information & communication** — M24 (disinformation, propaganda, regulation, digital footprint). N23 also fits weakly here (fairy tales as communication artefacts that evolve with media).
- **Mobility & frontiers** — N24 (space exploration: law, deregulation, who gets to go) and N25 (human migration: who moves and why). Both ask "who has access to movement and on whose terms?"
- **Body & data** — M25 (health-monitoring devices, where personal data goes) and M26 (twin studies, UPFs, somatotyping). Both treat the body as a source of measurable data and the self as something inferred from it.
- **Society & narratives** — N23 (fairy tales as evolving cultural narrative). The most literary-leaning PRM, with the strongest L&L bias.

Cross-cluster motif: **multiple-perspectives framing recurs in every PRM**. Whether labelled "perspectives", "lenses", "for/against", "pros/cons" or "stakeholder views", the IBO consistently scaffolds Criterion D (Reflection) and Criterion B (Synthesis) by bundling at least one explicitly-multi-viewpoint reference into each pack.
