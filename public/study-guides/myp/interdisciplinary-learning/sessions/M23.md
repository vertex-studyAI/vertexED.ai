> Best fit Approach (MS guidance, page 3, verbatim): "The approach used in assessment in the application of assessment criteria is a 'best fit' model. The examiner or teacher applying an assessment criterion must choose the achievement level that overall best matches the piece of work being marked. It is not necessary for every detailed aspect of an achievement level to be satisfied for that level to be awarded but it must reflect the balance of student achievement against the markband. For example, if student work matches two of the three requirements within a markband but one is seriously lacking, the student should be awarded for the strands that have been met well, but the mark awarded should be at the lower end of the markband to compensate for what is lacking in one strand. If the level of student work spans multiple markbands, compensation depends on the performance in the higher order skills. It is worth noting that the highest level of any given criterion does not represent perfection."

> "Before marking, please familiarize yourself with the **pre-release material**, all sources, the questions and the markscheme."

## Command Terms (from MS, page 3)

| Command term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **State*** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. |
| **Outline*** | Give a brief account or summary. |
| **Describe*** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. |
| **Explain*** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. |
| **Compare and contrast** | Give an account of the similarities and differences between two (or more) items or situations, referring to both (all) of them throughout. |
| **Discuss** | Offer a considered and balanced review that includes a range of arguments, factors or hypotheses. Opinions or conclusions should be presented clearly and supported by appropriate evidence. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. (See also "Explain".) |

\*These are the command terms used throughout the markscheme.

### Question 1 — Solar Car vs Hydrogen Car (12 marks) | Compare & contrast

**Stimulus:** The two sales brochures shown in the tabs above ("The Solar Car" and "The Hydrogen Car" — see Reference Material above).

**Question (verbatim):**
> "Refer to the two sales brochures in the tabs above. **Compare and contrast** the synthesis of mathematics and science in 'The Solar Car' and 'The Hydrogen Car' sales brochures. In your answer you must:
> - analyse the use of mathematics and science in each brochure
> - explain the strengths and weaknesses of each synthesis
> - give an evaluative conclusion."
>
> **(12 marks)**

**MS notes (verbatim):**
> "Responses must refer to the brochures use of mathematics and science, not the cars themselves. If a response only compares the cars, then students score 0 for the first two strands. The conclusion can still gain marks."
>
> "Responses should not be two separate lists about each brochure or discuss each brochure separately. The command term is Compare and Contrast and responses must therefore refer to similarities and differences of the brochures together. If there is no comparison or contrast for the first two strands, highest band available for this strand is 1-3."
>
> "Note: To be valid, examples must reference an element of the brochures."

**Markband rubric (best fit, 12 marks):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1–3** | The student:<br>- **states** how each brochure uses mathematics and science<br>- **states** a strength and weakness of each syntheses |
| **4–6** | The student **directly** compares and contrasts each brochure by:<br>- **outlining** how each brochure uses mathematics and science<br>- **outlining** the strengths and weaknesses of the syntheses<br>- **attempting** a conclusion |
| **7–9** | The student **directly** compares and contrasts each brochure by:<br>- **describing** how each brochure uses mathematics and science<br>- **describing** the strengths and weaknesses of the syntheses<br>- giving a conclusion |
| **10–12** | The student **directly** compares and contrasts each brochure by:<br>- **explaining** how each brochure uses mathematics and science<br>- **explaining** the strengths and weaknesses of the syntheses<br>- giving an **evaluative** conclusion |

**Key MS notes — what the MS rewards / penalises:**
- Rewards: direct comparison/contrast across the two brochures (not separate paragraphs); progression State → Outline → Describe → Explain across bands; evaluative (not just stated) conclusion at the top band.
- Penalises: comparing the cars themselves rather than how the **brochures** use mathematics and science (auto-zero for first two strands); two-list structure that fails to compare/contrast (capped at 1–3 for those strands); examples not anchored to brochure content (deemed "not valid").

### Question 2 — Transport Electrification Proposal (12 marks) | Justify

**Stimulus (verbatim setup):**
> "You are a government official working for the department of transport. Your department is overseeing the transition to electric cars over a 10-year period.
> The total budget for transport is $20 billion. If the project costs more than $20 billion, the extra budget funds will be taken from the budget for the environment.
> The interactive media below shows the projected outcomes for three transition scenarios: 40 %, 70 % or 100 % electric vehicles."

The student can toggle Scenario 1 (40%), Scenario 2 (70%), Scenario 3 (100%) to see the three charts (Total annual transport budget; Stability of electricity supply; Carbon footprint of electric cars — see Reference Material above).

**Question (verbatim):**
> "You need to prepare a proposal justifying which scenario is best.
> By applying your knowledge of science and mathematics, **justify** which scenario is best.
> In your answer you must include:
> - evidence supporting your choice using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning
> - a response to the evidence against your choice using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning
> - an evaluative conclusion."
>
> **(12 marks)**

**MS notes (verbatim):**
> "Note: Do not credit any scientific information that is not from the examination or PRM."
>
> "Note: Mathematical calculations are not required and are therefore to be ignored. Credit is to be awarded for demonstrating and understanding of mathematical concepts."

**Markband rubric (best fit, 12 marks):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1–3** | The student justifies by:<br>- **stating** evidence using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning<br>- **stating** a counter argument using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning |
| **4–6** | The student justifies by:<br>- **outlining** evidence for their choice using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning<br>- **outlining** a counter argument using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning<br>- attempting a conclusion |
| **7–9** | The student justifies by:<br>- **describing** their choice using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning<br>- **describing** a counter argument using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning<br>- providing a conclusion<br><br>**or**<br><br>- **describing** their choice using mathematical **or** scientific reasoning<br>- **describing** a counter argument using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning<br>- providing a conclusion |
| **10–12** | The student fully justifies by:<br>- **explaining** their choice using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning<br>- **explaining** a counter argument using mathematical **and** scientific reasoning<br>- providing an **evaluative** conclusion |

**Key MS notes — what the MS rewards / penalises:**
- Rewards: integrated **mathematical and scientific** reasoning (the conjunction in the top two bands is decisive — both disciplines must be present); explicit counter-argument (not just one-sided advocacy); evaluative conclusion at top band.
- Penalises: any external scientific information not present in the examination or pre-release material is not credited; mathematical calculations themselves are not required (and are ignored) — only the **understanding** of mathematical concepts is credited.

### Question 3 — Self-Employed Delivery Driver (12 marks) | Discuss

**Stimulus:** Reference 5 from the **pre-release material** — the self-employed delivery driver profile (see Reference Material above).

**Question (verbatim):**
> "Using your **interdisciplinary** understanding, **discuss** the challenges and related solutions that electric vehicles present for the self-employed delivery driver in reference 5.
> In your answer you must:
> - explain **two** challenges
> - explain solutions to address **both** challenges
> - include evidence from the pre-release material to support your arguments.
>
> **(12 marks)**"

**MS notes (verbatim):**
> "Note: best fit each strand separately and then best fit all three strands."
>
> "Note: Please familiarise with yourself with the pre-release Material."
>
> "Note: Reference to the delivery driver does not gain credit for the third strand as reference to the **pre-release material**."

**Markband rubric (best fit, 12 marks):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1–3** | The student discusses by:<br>- **stating** two challenges<br>- **stating** two solutions |
| **4–6** | The student discusses by:<br>- **outlining** two challenges<br>- **outlining** two solutions<br>- attempting to use evidence from the pre-release material |
| **7–9** | The student discusses by:<br>- **describing** two challenges<br>- **describing** two solutions<br>- using evidence from the pre-release material |
| **10–12** | The student fully discusses by:<br>- **explaining** two challenges<br>- **explaining** two solutions<br>- using relevant evidence from the pre-release material |

**Key MS notes — what the MS rewards / penalises:**
- Rewards: explicit interdisciplinary framing (e.g. economics/finance + science/engineering + geography of charging infrastructure); explanation of **two** challenges and **two** solutions (paired explicitly); use of **pre-release material** evidence — generic references to "the delivery driver" without anchoring to PRM content do not earn the third strand.
- Penalises: stand-alone reference to the delivery driver scenario in the question itself does not credit the PRM-evidence strand (must cite PRM content).
- Marking model: each of the three strands (challenges, solutions, evidence) is best-fitted separately and then a final best-fit combines them.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Best-fit holistic** | All three questions use 0 / 1–3 / 4–6 / 7–9 / 10–12 markbands. No point-by-point answers; overall quality matched to nearest band. |
| **Command-term ladder** | Within a single question the bands map to a fixed verb ladder: State (1–3) → Outline (4–6) → Describe (7–9) → Explain (10–12). Same pattern in Q1, Q2, Q3. |
| **Conclusion ladder** | Bands 4–6 "attempt a conclusion"; 7–9 "give a conclusion"; 10–12 require an "**evaluative** conclusion" (Q1, Q2). |
| **And vs Or in disciplinary integration (Q2)** | Bands 1–3 and 4–6 accept mathematical **OR** scientific reasoning; band 7–9 requires **AND** for one of the two strands; band 10–12 requires **AND** for both strands. Disciplinary breadth gates the upper bands. |
| **Strand-by-strand best fit (Q3)** | MS explicitly says: "best fit each strand separately and then best fit all three strands." |
| **Evidence anchoring** | Q1: examples must reference brochure elements. Q2: only examination/PRM content credited — external knowledge ignored. Q3: must cite **pre-release material** content, not just the in-question driver scenario. |
| **Auto-zero traps** | Q1: discussing the cars themselves (instead of how the brochures synthesise maths + science) → 0 for first two strands. Q1: separate-list responses without comparing → capped at 1–3 for the comparison strands. |
| **Mathematical-calculation rule (Q2)** | "Mathematical calculations are not required and are therefore to be ignored. Credit is to be awarded for demonstrating an understanding of mathematical concepts." Conceptual reasoning, not arithmetic, is what earns marks. |
| **Pre-release material (PRM)** | Central to Q3 — MS reminds examiners three times to familiarise themselves with the PRM. Reference 5 (delivery driver) is the only PRM source explicitly identified in this MS. |
| **No criterion labels in MS** | Unlike Biology MYP, the IDL MS does not tag questions with Criterion A/B/C/D — single holistic markband per question. |
