## Best fit approach (MS p.4 — verbatim)

> "The approach used in assessment is the application of assessment criteria in a 'best fit' model. The examiner is applying an assessment criterion must choose the achievement level that overall best matches the work being assessed. It is not necessary for every single aspect of a level descriptor to be met for that achievement level to be achieved."

The MS lists the IDL command terms and their definitions:

| Command term | Definition (MS verbatim) |
|---|---|
| **Select** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. |
| **Describe** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. |
| **Formulate** | Express precisely and systematically the relevant concept(s) or argument(s). |
| **Evaluate** | Make an appraisal by weighing up the strengths and limitations. |
| **Explain** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. |
| **Analyse** | Break down in order to bring out the essential elements or structure. To identify parts and relationships, and to interpret information to reach conclusions. |
| **Compare and contrast** | Give an account of the similarities and differences between two (or more) items or situations, referring to both (all) of them throughout. |
| **Create** | Give a brief account or summary. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. (See also "Explain".) |
| **To what extent** | Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument. |

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Best fit, not point-by-point** | Every question on this paper uses an MYP IDL "best fit" markband. There are no explicit "accept this exact phrase" answers. The examiner picks the band whose descriptors most closely match the response. |
| **Verb ladder** | Each band uses a progression of MYP command-term verbs: states → outlines → describes → explains. The verb hit by the response is the primary signal for the band. |
| **Reference-anchoring** | Several questions cap marks if the response does not anchor to the reference: Q3a "Points of view must be in the context of aid distribution. If not, max 3 marks"; Q4 "if comparisons and contrasts are not linked to both articles, a maximum of (2 marks)"; Q2b "if only strengths or only limitations are given, a maximum of (2 marks)". |
| **Synthesis as a graded skill** | Q3b, Q5 and Q6 all require "synthesising" skills/concepts from the two disciplines. Top-band descriptors require an evaluative or explicit conclusion linking back to the related concepts (point of view, culture). |
| **Evidence count thresholds** | Q2b and Q6 use a literal evidence-count rule (≥1 / ≥2 / ≥3 pieces of evidence per band). Q7 top band requires "explicitly linked" evidence from the examination as a whole. |
| **Standardisation notes** | The MS records definitions agreed in the standardisation meeting (e.g. for Q1c: "consider relevance, manageability, originality…"; for Q4: "Purpose = theme, Style = format, origin = source, origin = time"). These are binding on examiners. |
| **I&S key concept (Global interactions)** | Anchors Q1d explicitly and underpins Q3 (aid distribution debate) and Q7 (siting a nuclear plant locally vs globally). The MS reproduces the key-concept definition verbatim from Reference 1. |
| **Two-discipline scaffolding** | Reference 3 (related-concepts diagram) shows examiners which related concepts to expect: from I&S — Culture, Global interactions, Identity, Innovation and revolution, Perspective, Power; from L&L — Audience imperatives, Character, Context, Genres, Intertextuality, Point of view, Purpose, Self expression, Setting, Stylistic choices, Structure, Theme. |
| **Caps** | 12-mark questions (Q3a, Q4, Q7) all have explicit caps for incomplete responses (max 3 if context is wrong, max 2 if no linkage between the two items being compared). |
