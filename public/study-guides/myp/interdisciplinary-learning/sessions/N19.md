The phrase *"The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below"* appears at the 0-mark row of every banded question. Best-fit marking applies — examiners match the overall response to the closest band, then fine-tune within the band.

## Best-fit approach & command-term glossary (from MS p. 3)

> **Best fit approach (verbatim summary).** *"The approach used in assessment is the application of assessment criteria in a 'best fit' model. The purpose of the examiner applying an assessment criterion must be that the achievement level that best matches the holistic judgement of the work is selected. This must not be made by simply ticking off achievement levels in isolation but instead by thinking holistically about how the work matches the descriptor and finding the level that most matches the requirements being met. Note: when marking, please familiarise yourself with the previous markscheme material, in particular the questions and the markschemes."*

The MS reproduces a command-term reference table. Definitions used in this paper:

| Term | Definition (MS reference table) |
|---|---|
| **State** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. |
| **Describe** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. |
| **Explain** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. |
| **Outline** | Give a brief account or summary. |
| **Analyse** | Break down in order to bring out the essential elements or structure. To identify parts and relationships, and to interpret information to reach conclusions. |
| **Compare and contrast** | Give an account of similarities and differences between two (or more) items or situations, referring to both (all) of them throughout. |
| **Construct** | Display information in a diagrammatic or logical form. |
| **Estimate** | Obtain an approximate value. |
| **Identify** | Provide an answer from a number of possibilities. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. |
| **To what extent** | Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument. |

### Question 1 — Lab-grown foods: experimental improvement & application to world hunger (10 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** Pre-release material introduces an experiment relating to lab-grown ("cultured") food production and a wider claim that lab-grown foods could help reduce world hunger. Students are expected to draw on the experimental description in the pre-release pack.

#### 1a — Three improvements to the experiment (6 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Explain* three ways in which this experiment could be improved.** *(6 marks)*

**Note from MS (verbatim):** *"Note: the marking rubric is to be used for each of the three response boxes."*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (per response box, max 2 marks; total max 6):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors described below. |
| 1 | The student identifies an improvement. |
| 2 | The student explains the benefit of the improvement identified. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The same 0/1/2 rubric is applied **independently** to each of the three response boxes.
- Mark 1 is awarded for *identifying* an improvement; mark 2 only when the *benefit* of that specific improvement is then **explained**.
- Best-fit, holistic — examiners are not simply ticking criteria.

#### 1b — Strength and weakness of lab-grown foods for world hunger (4 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Explain* one strength and one weakness of using lab-grown foods to reduce world hunger.** *(4 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 4):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors described below. |
| 1 | The student identifies at least one strength **or** weakness. |
| 2 | The student identifies **one** strength **and one** weakness **or** the student provides a strength **or** weakness **and** provides a reason. |
| 3 | The student identifies **one** strength **and one** weakness **and** the student provides a reason for the strength **or** weakness. |
| 4 | The student identifies **one** strength **and one** weakness **and** the student provides a reason for **both** the strength **and** weakness. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The verb-ladder here is *identifies → provides a reason → provides a reason for both*.
- Marks scale with how completely the student covers **both** sides (strength **and** weakness) **and** how completely each side is **reasoned**.
- A strong strength **without** a weakness is capped at 2; a strength + weakness with reasoning for only one side is capped at 3.

### Question 2 — Alternative milks, dairy industry & society (10 marks)

**Stimulus (verbatim quotation in MS):** *"Alternative milks are better for the environment than dairy, but their growing appeal may cause problems."*

The question revolves around (i) the **key concept of global interactions** as a lens on this quotation, (ii) the change alternative milk has caused in dairy-milk consumption, and (iii) the broader effects of the dairy industry on society.

#### 2a — Key concept of global interactions (2 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Outline* how the key concept *global interactions* supports your understanding of the quotation above, using an example.** *(2 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 2):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1 | The student outlines their choice. |
| 2 | The student outlines their choice with a valid example. |

**Key MS notes:** The terminology is *"outlines their choice"* — the student must explicitly link **global interactions** to the alternative-milks-vs-dairy issue. The example must be **valid** (i.e. genuinely illustrating a global-interactions effect, not merely naming a milk).

#### 2b — Change in dairy-milk consumption (2 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Outline* how alternative milk has led to a change in dairy milk consumption, using an example to support your answer.** *(2 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 2):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1 | The student outlines the change in dairy milk consumption. |
| 2 | The student outlines the change in dairy milk consumption with a valid example. |

**Key MS notes:** Same structural ladder as 2a — *outlines → outlines + valid example*. The "change" can be a decrease in dairy consumption, a shift in market share, or a change in producer behaviour, but it must be **named** rather than implied.

#### 2c — Effects of the dairy industry on society (6 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Discuss* the effects of the dairy industry on society. In your answer, you must include:*
- *the positive effects on society*
- *the negative effects on society.*** *(6 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 6):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The effects on society are **superficially discussed**. The student: <br>• **states** the positive effects <br>• **states** the negative effects. |
| 3–4 | The effects on society are **partially discussed**. The student: <br>• **describes** the positive effects <br>• **describes** the negative effects. |
| 5–6 | The effects on society are **fully discussed**. The student: <br>• **explains** the positive effects <br>• **explains** the negative effects. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The verb-ladder *states → describes → explains* is the spine of this rubric.
- A response that **only** discusses positive **or** only negative effects cannot reach the upper bands — both sides are required at every level above 0.
- "Discuss" here is interpreted by the MS as *covering both sides with appropriate depth*; depth (state vs describe vs explain) is what moves the response between bands.

### Question 3 — Restaurant-menu justification (8 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** A scenario in which the student has chosen a dish for a restaurant menu and must persuade the restaurant manager to accept their choice over alternative dishes. Students draw on the **pre-release material** for evidence.

#### 3 — Justify the dish choice (8 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Justify* your choice to the restaurant manager. In your answer, you must:*
- *give health reasons for the choice of your dish in comparison to the other dishes*
- *give ethical reasons for the choice of your dish in comparison to the other dishes*
- *use evidence from the pre-release material.*** *(8 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 8):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The student addresses the following points: <br>• **states** health reasons for the choice of dish <br>• **states** ethical reasons for the choice of dish. |
| 3–4 | The student addresses the following points: <br>• **outlines** health reasons for the choice of dish <br>• **outlines** ethical reasons for the choice of dish <br>• uses evidence from pre-release material. |
| 5–6 | The student addresses the following points: <br>• **describes** health reasons for the choice of dish in comparison to the other dishes <br>• **describes** ethical reasons for the choice of dish in comparison to the other dishes <br>• uses evidence from pre-release material. |
| 7–8 | The student addresses the following points: <br>• **explains** health reasons for the choice of dish in comparison to the other dishes <br>• **explains** ethical reasons for the choice of dish in comparison to the other dishes <br>• uses evidence from pre-release material. |

**Key MS notes:**
- Three threads run through the rubric: (1) **health reasons**, (2) **ethical reasons**, (3) **comparison to the other dishes** — and they must be evidenced from the pre-release pack.
- The lowest band (1–2) does not require evidence from the pre-release material; bands 3–4 onward do.
- *"In comparison to the other dishes"* is a band-3+ requirement — an isolated description of the chosen dish without comparison cannot reach 5–6 or 7–8.
- The verb-ladder is again *states → outlines → describes → explains*.

### Question 4 — Compare and contrast the adverts (8 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** Two (or more) adverts presented in the pre-release / examination material. Students compare similarities and differences between them.

#### 4 — Compare and contrast (8 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***Compare and contrast* how the adverts use [their content / approach] — that is, the similarities and differences in the way the adverts present their messages.** *(8 marks)*

> **Reconstruction note.** The MS for Q4 makes the **rubric** verbatim available but only the headline of the question stem. The rubric below is verbatim; the question stem language reflects the rubric's "similarities **and** differences" framing.

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 8):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The student addresses **at least one** of the following points by addressing the following points: <br>• **identifies** the similarities **or** differences in the adverts <br>• provides examples to support similarities **or** differences. |
| 3–4 | The student addresses **at least two** of the following points by addressing the following points: <br>• **outlines** the similarities **and** differences in the adverts <br>• provides examples to support similarities **and** differences. |
| 5–6 | The student addresses **at least three** of the following points by addressing the following points: <br>• **describes** the similarities **and** differences in the adverts <br>• provides examples to support similarities **and** differences. |
| 7–8 | The student addresses **all** of the following points by addressing the following points: <br>• **explains** the similarities **and** differences in the adverts <br>• provides examples to support similarities **and** differences. |

**Key MS notes:**
- Once again the verb-ladder *identifies → outlines → describes → explains* is the spine.
- Top band requires **both** similarities **and** differences with supporting examples — a one-sided answer (only similarities or only differences) is capped at the lower bands.
- Examples must be drawn from the adverts themselves — generic claims about advertising do not count.

### Question 5 — Extended synthesis on the food-systems theme (12 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** The pre-release material is the evidence base. Students produce an extended response that integrates content across the paper's food / society / sciences thread.

#### 5 — Extended response (12 marks)

**Question stem (reconstructed from the rubric framing):** *Drawing on the **pre-release material** and your wider studies, develop an interdisciplinary response that addresses the integrating food-systems issue. The student is expected to address the following points:*
- *the relevant disciplinary content*
- *examples from the pre-release material*
- *a coherent interdisciplinary explanation.*

> **Reconstruction note.** The MS for Q5 reproduces only the rubric (verbatim below). The question stem itself is not in the MS pages provided. The rubric verbs (identifies → outlines → describes → explains) are reproduced exactly.

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 12):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | The student addresses **at least one** of the following points: <br>• **identifies** the relevant content <br>• provides examples from the pre-release material. |
| 4–6 | The student addresses **at least two** of the following points: <br>• **outlines** the relevant content <br>• provides examples from the pre-release material. |
| 7–9 | The student addresses **at least three** of the following points: <br>• **describes** the relevant content <br>• provides examples from the pre-release material. |
| 10–12 | The student addresses **all** of the following points: <br>• **explains** the relevant content <br>• provides examples from the pre-release material <br>• provides an interdisciplinary explanation. |

**Key MS notes:**
- 12-mark band-set: 0 / 1–3 / 4–6 / 7–9 / 10–12.
- Pre-release evidence is required at **every** scoring band (1–3 onward).
- Top band (10–12) **adds** a fourth requirement on top of the verb-ladder: an explicit *interdisciplinary explanation* — i.e. the response must show the disciplines being **synthesised**, not merely listed.

### Question 6 — To what extent should scientists who develop technologies with destructive potential be celebrated? (12 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** Pre-release material on scientific technologies that benefit society but also have destructive potential (e.g. nuclear, biotech, food technologies, AI, etc., depending on the pre-release pack).

#### 6 — To what extent (12 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** ***To what extent* should scientists involved in developing scientific technologies for society that also have destructive potential be celebrated? You must include:*
- *reasons why they should be celebrated*
- *reasons why they should not be celebrated*
- *evidence from the pre-release material*
- *an evaluative conclusion.*** *(12 marks)*

**Mark scheme — banded rubric (max 12):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | The student addresses **at least one** of the following points: <br>• **states** reasons why they should be celebrated <br>• **states** reasons why they should not be celebrated <br>• provides evidence from the pre-release material <br>• a conclusion. |
| 4–6 | The student addresses **at least two** of the following points: <br>• **outlines** reasons why they should be celebrated <br>• **outlines** reasons why they should not be celebrated <br>• provides evidence from the pre-release material <br>• a conclusion. |
| 7–9 | The student addresses **at least three** of the following points: <br>• **describes** reasons why they should be celebrated <br>• **describes** reasons why they should not be celebrated <br>• provides evidence from the pre-release material <br>• an evaluative conclusion. |
| 10–12 | The student addresses **all** of the following points: <br>• **explains** reasons why they should be celebrated <br>• **explains** reasons why they should not be celebrated <br>• provides evidence from the pre-release material <br>• an evaluative conclusion. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The *To what extent* command term requires **opinions and conclusions** to be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument (per the command-term glossary).
- Note the rubric distinction: bands 1–3 and 4–6 require only *"a conclusion"*; bands 7–9 and 10–12 require an **evaluative conclusion** — i.e. one that **weighs** the for/against arguments rather than restating them.
- A response that gives only one side (only reasons for celebration **or** only reasons against) is structurally capped — the rubric requires **both** sides at every active band.
- Pre-release evidence is required from band 1–3 upward.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns (N19)

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Verb-ladder rubric** | Almost every banded question uses the same vertical ladder: *identifies / states → outlines → describes → explains*. The **only** thing that distinguishes a 5–6 band from a 7–8 band on most questions is whether the verb is *describes* or *explains*. |
| **Both-sides requirement** | Q1b (strength + weakness), Q2c (positive + negative), Q3 (health + ethical), Q4 (similarities + differences), Q6 (for + against celebration), Q7 (strengths + weaknesses). One-sided answers are systematically capped below the top bands. |
| **Evidence requirement** | "Evidence from the pre-release material" appears in Q3, Q5, Q6 — required from band 1–3/3–4 upward. Q7 instead requires "evidence from the examination". Q1, Q2, Q4 do not have an explicit evidence-from-pre-release requirement in the rubric. |
| **Evaluative conclusion** | Q6 and Q7 both distinguish between *"a conclusion"* (lower bands) and *"an evaluative conclusion"* (upper bands). The conclusion must **weigh** the for/against, not merely restate. |
| **0-mark phrasing** | All banded questions use the same 0-mark line: *"The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below."* |
| **Per-box marking** | Q1a explicitly uses the same 0/1/2 rubric for **each** of three response boxes — a notable structural feature. |
| **Best-fit holistic marking** | The MS opens (p. 3) with the explicit Best-fit Approach instruction: examiners must judge the response **holistically** and find the band that **best matches**, not tick descriptors in isolation. |
| **No criterion labels** | Unlike MYP Sciences papers (which label each question Criterion A/B/C/D), the IDL N19 MS does **not** tag individual questions with criteria — the four IDU criteria (disciplinary grounding, synthesising, communicating, reflecting) are implicit in the question types. |
| **Two band-widths** | 8-mark questions use 0 / 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6 / 7–8 (Q3, Q4, Q7); 12-mark questions use 0 / 1–3 / 4–6 / 7–9 / 10–12 (Q5, Q6); short questions (Q2a, Q2b) use 0/1/2; Q1a uses 0/1/2 per response box; Q1b uses 0/1/2/3/4; Q2c uses 0 / 1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6. |
| **Command-term sourcing** | The MS p. 3 command-term reference table is the same one used across IDL exam sessions — see [M19.md](M19.md) and [N16.md](N16.md) for the same definitions. |
