## "Best Fit" approach (MS verbatim)

> "The approach used in assessment is the application of assessment criteria is a 'best fit' model. The examiner or teacher applying an assessment criterion must choose the achievement level that best matches the piece of work being marked. It is not necessary for every detailed aspect of an achievement level to be satisfied for that level to be awarded but it must reflect the balance of student achievement against the markband. For example, if student work matches two of the three requirements within a markband but one is seriously lacking, the student should be awarded for the strands that have been met well, but the mark awarded should be at the lower end of the markband to compensate for what is lacking in one strand. If the level of student work spans multiple markbands, compensation depends on the performance in the higher order skills. It is worth noting that the highest level of any given criterion does not represent perfection."

**Markband rubric (verbatim):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1 | The answer is **basic**.<br>The student **outlines** why they matched **one** innovation to imagination and/or reason. |
| 2 | The answer is **adequate**.<br>The student **outlines** why they matched **two** innovations to imagination and/or reason. |
| 3 | The answer is **thorough**.<br>The student **outlines** why they matched **three** innovations to imagination and/or reason. |

**Key MS notes:** Placement (which box) is not credited — only the reasoning given for the match earns marks. Count of innovations addressed is the explicit gate.

#### 6b — Evaluate the role of imagination and reason in exploration (16 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** "**Evaluate** the role of imagination and reason in exploration. In your answer you must:
- consider the benefits and limitations of imagination in deciding to explore
- consider the benefits and limitations of reason in deciding to explore
- include a conclusion about the importance of deciding to explore, based on the arguments presented

**Markband rubric (verbatim):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–4 | The answer is **limited**.<br>The student:<br>- **states**:<br>  - Benefits of imagination<br>  **or**<br>  - Benefits of reason<br>  **or**<br>  - Limitations of imagination<br>  **or**<br>  - Limitations of reason<br>- **does not** write a conclusion. |
| 5–8 | The answer is **adequate**.<br>The student:<br>- **describes**:<br>  - Benefits of imagination<br>  **or**<br>  - Benefits of reason<br>  **or**<br>  - Limitations of imagination<br>  **or**<br>  - Limitations of reason<br>- **attempts** a conclusion. |

### Question 7 — Innovation and progress in exploration (10 marks)

**Stimulus:** Pre-release timeline of major scientific and technical innovations from Source 7 / global context "adaptation, ingenuity and progress".

**Question (verbatim):** "Choose **one** major scientific and technical innovation from the timeline. **To what extent** does your chosen innovation demonstrate progress in exploration? In your answer you must:
- refer to the **global context** focus 'adaptation, ingenuity and progress'
- use evidence from the **pre-release material** to support your argument
- write a **conclusion** about how your choice demonstrates progress."

**Markband rubric (verbatim):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The answer is **limited**.<br>The student:<br>- **superficially states** how their choice demonstrates adaption **or** ingenuity<br>- does not conclude about how their choice demonstrates progress. |
| 3–5 | The answer is **adequate**.<br>The student:<br>- **describes** how their choice demonstrates adaption **or** ingenuity<br>- **attempts** to use evidence from the PRM to support their arguments<br>- **attempts a conclusion** about how their choice demonstrates progress. |
| 6–7 | The answer is **detailed**.<br>The student:<br>- **explains** how their choice demonstrates adaptation **and** ingenuity<br>- **uses** evidence from the PRM to support their arguments<br>- **states a conclusion** about how their choice demonstrates progress. |
| 8–10 | The answer is **thorough and perceptive**.<br>The student:<br>- **discusses thoroughly** how their choice demonstrates adaptation **and** ingenuity<br>- Using evidence from the PRM **and beyond** to support their arguments<br>- writes a **thorough conclusion** about how their choice demonstrates adaptation and progress as a result of the arguments presented. |

**Key MS notes:** "Adaptation OR ingenuity" → "adaptation AND ingenuity" is the verb-coverage gate. Top band requires evidence drawn from beyond the pre-release material, plus a thorough (not just stated) conclusion.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns (M17 IDL)

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **"Best fit" markbands** | Every question except Q1a (point-marked, 3 × 1 mark) and Q3a (cap-marked, 2 + 2) uses a multi-band rubric. Examiners pick the band that best matches the overall response, then weigh sub-strands within that band. |
| **Discipline-coverage gates** | Multiple questions cap the score if only one discipline is addressed: Q3b (cap 8/16 if only one side of innovation covered), Q4b (cap 2/10 if only one discipline), Q5 (band ceilings tied to "math OR societies" vs "math AND societies, explicit and balanced"). |
| **Source-count gates** | Q1c: <1 source = max 2; ≥1 = max 4; ≥2 = max 6; ≥3 + outside info = up to 8. Q3b: 0/1/≥2/≥3 evidence pieces gate the four bands. |
| **Bullet-point coverage gates** | Q4a: bands 1–5 / 6–10 / 11–15 / 16–20 require addressing 1–3 / ≥3 / ≥4 / all 5 of the bullet points respectively. |
| **Verb progression** | Standard MYP IDL ladder: states → describes → explains → justifies/evaluates. Used in Q1b, Q2a, Q2b, Q2c, Q4a, Q4b, Q6b, Q7. |
| **Innovation-count gates** | Q6a: 1 / 2 / 3 / all 4 innovations matched-and-reasoned correspond directly to mark 1 / 2 / 3 / 4. |
| **Conclusion as a discriminator** | Q3b, Q6b, Q7 explicitly require a "thorough conclusion" or "evaluative conclusion" for the top band; weaker conclusions cap the response below. |
| **Evidence-from-beyond-sources** | Q1c, Q3b and Q7 reward the use of material beyond the supplied sources/pre-release material at the top band. |
| **Examiner-blacklist clauses** | Q5 explicitly tells markers NOT to credit the standard question "To what extent is it viable to settle on Mars?" — every paper would contain it, so it cannot discriminate. |
| **Cap clauses** | Q5: "If the student only has questions, the score must be in the 1–2 band." Q4b: cap at 2 if only one discipline. Q3b: cap at 8 if one-sided. Q3a: cap at 2 each for similarities and differences. |
| **Strand-of-band marking** | Q2c MS explicitly notes "The strands within each mark band are separated by a dotted line for clarity" — meaning each band has two parallel strands (method + math/real-life context) that must both reach that level. |
| **Implicit → explicit discipline progression** | Q5 lower bands accept implicit discipline references; only the top band (8–10) requires explicit and balanced math + societies coverage. |
| **Repeat / converse counting** | Q3a: features repeated with slight variation count once; converse arguments (X bigger ↔ Y smaller) count once. |
| **Placement vs reasoning** | Q6a: only the reasoning earns marks, not the placement of innovations into imagination/reason boxes. |
