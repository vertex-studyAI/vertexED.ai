## Best-fit Approach (from MS, page 4)

The MS opens with a "Best-fit Approach" guidance table that defines how command terms are applied across the markbands. Reproduced verbatim below as it governs how Q1b, Q3, Q5 and Q6 are marked.

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **State** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. |
| **Outline** | Give a brief account or summary. |
| **Describe** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. |
| **Explain** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. |
| **Discuss** | Offer a considered and balanced review that includes a range of arguments, factors or hypotheses. Opinions or conclusions should be presented clearly and supported by appropriate evidence. |
| **Construct** | Display information in a diagrammatic or logical form. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. |
| **Suggest** | Propose a solution, hypothesis or other possible answer. |
| **To what extent** | Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported by appropriate evidence and sound argument. |

### Question 2 — Surveyor video (Reference 2, mathematical context)

**Stimulus (video transcript reproduced verbatim from MS, page 8):**

> "Video transcript exam only video.
> The ancient Baobab trees have been given a protection order.
> This means that the Baobab trees must not be removed and must not be damaged to make way for the urban development.
> Construction work must be away from the roots of the Baobab trees — but how far?
> A surveyor takes measurements of one of the Baobab trees.
> The surveyor uses the measurements to calculate the height of the tree.
> `tan θ = opp/adj`
> `tan 50 = h/20`
> `20 tan 50 = h`
> `h = 23.8 m`
> The length of the roots of the Baobab tree spread to be the same as the height of the tree.
> Using these calculations, the surveyor instructs that buildings must not be constructed within 11.9 m of the Baobab tree."

#### 2a — Outline how the surveyor protected the Baobab tree using mathematical logic (2 marks) | Criterion A / Mathematics

**Question:** **Outline**, with an example, a way that the surveyor protected the Baobab tree using mathematical logic. **(2 marks)**

**MS note (verbatim):** "descriptions of the 'method' or 'workings' to calculate the height is not relevant as this is information provided in the video."

**Mark scheme — markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1** | The student provides an **outline** of a way that the surveyor protected the Baobab tree using mathematical logic. |
| **2** | The student provides an **outline with an example** of a way that the surveyor protected the Baobab tree using mathematical logic. |

**Examples (from MS):**

- **1 mark** — "the surveyor can get the height of the tree and therefore determine the total length of the roots" — WTTE.
- **2 marks** — "the surveyor can get the height of the tree and half this value to get the length of the roots (i.e. outline of height of tree and then 11.9 for roots) **and** the surveyor must ensure the construction is at least the length of the roots away from the tree" — WTTE.

**Key MS notes:**
- Reciting the trigonometry method/workings does not earn a mark — the MS explicitly excludes "descriptions of the 'method' or 'workings'" because that is given in the video.
- Criterion A (Evaluating, mathematical-logic strand).

#### 2b — Discuss the rounding & significant figures in the surveyor's calculation (2 marks) | Mathematics

**Question:** **Discuss** the rounding and significant figures in the surveyor's calculation of the protective area for the Baobab tree. **(2 marks)**

**Mark scheme — markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor (from MS, page 10) |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1** | Award **(1 mark)** for any of the points below:<br>- The recipient of the surveyor from the line is rounded to the nearest whole number<br>- The height of the surveyor to the top of the tree is the value of 23.8<br>- 11.9 is half value of 23.8 (linked to part c values, also rounded to 1 significant figure) |
| **2** | Award **(2 marks)** for the use of the rounding formula / trigonometry formula:<br>- 1 mark for using the formula and identifying values which give the length of the roots<br>- 1 further mark for explanations that the method does not include the height of the surveyor / the height value as 11.9 m (or that 11.9 m has been rounded down) |

**Key MS notes:**
- Awards a mark for noting that 11.9 m is half of 23.8 m and rounded to 1 s.f., and a further mark for noticing the calculation excludes the surveyor's own height (i.e. the model treats the eye-line as ground-level).
- Criterion A / Mathematics.

#### 2c — Discuss strengths & weaknesses of representing the protected zone mathematically (6 marks) | Criterion A

**Question:** **Discuss** the strengths and weaknesses of using mathematical representations to approximate the area around the Baobab tree that needs to be protected. **(6 marks)**

**MS preamble (verbatim):** "Examples: Not an exhaustive list" — followed by lists of accepted strengths and weaknesses.

**Strengths (MS bullets):**
- simplicity of the form of the surveyor from the line
- accessibility of the trigonometric formula
- it allows the use of mathematics to model real-world situations

**Weaknesses (MS bullets):**
- inaccuracies that result from the theory of model assumptions
- it does not take account of the height of the surveyor
- changes over time not captured in the theory at one point in time
- measuring only one tree at a time is impractical for many trees in the area
- approximately limited in its accuracy

**Mark scheme — markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| **0** | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| **1–2** | The student:<br>- **outlines** the strengths of the use of representations<br>- **outlines** the weaknesses of the use of representations |
| **3–4** | The student:<br>- **describes** the strengths of the use of representations<br>- **describes** the weaknesses of the use of representations |
| **5–6** | The student:<br>- **explains** the strengths of the use of representations<br>- **explains** the weaknesses of the use of representations |

**Key MS notes:**
- The MS rewards balance — both strengths AND weaknesses must be addressed at every band.
- Criterion A.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **WTTE** ("Words to that effect") | Used throughout the rubric examples (Q2a). Paraphrases of the model answer are accepted if the disciplinary meaning is preserved. |
| **BOD** (Benefit of the doubt) | Listed as an examiner annotation on page 3. Used where intent is clear despite imprecise wording. |
| **Example-mandatory bands** | Q1b, Q3, Q5 and Q6 all require an explicit example (from the description / from Reference 1 / from the candidate's own response) at **every** mark band — answers without an example are capped at the previous band or scored 0. |
| **Synthesis premium** | Q3 and Q6 top bands explicitly require disciplinary synthesis ("disciplinary knowledge of Sciences **and** Individuals & Societies", or "evidence from the PDF for either individuals and societies or mathematics"). Single-discipline responses cap mid-band. |
| **Strengths & weaknesses balance** | Q2c and Q6 require both strengths AND weaknesses at every band. A one-sided answer is capped at half-band. |
| **Mathematical-method exclusion** | Q2a explicitly states: "descriptions of the 'method' or 'workings' to calculate the height is not relevant as this is information provided in the video." Reciting the given trigonometry earns 0. |
| **Significant-figures / rounding awareness** | Q2b rewards noticing that 11.9 m is half of 23.8 m rounded to 1 s.f., and that the model excludes the surveyor's own height. Mathematics-discipline marks. |
| **Numeric-design verification** | Q4 top band requires both size **and** cost to be calculated correctly; mid-band accepts size **or** cost. The MS provides a combined-area lookup table (page 18) so the examiner can verify any candidate's three-artefact combination. |
| **Three-component bands (Q5)** | "Mathematical reasoning + sociocultural reasoning + overall perspective" must all be present — missing one component drops the response one full band. |
| **Markband ladder** | The MS uses a uniform markband ladder across long-response questions: states → outlines → describes → explains → explains in detail. Each rung corresponds to a deeper command-term performance. |
| **Criterion mapping (IDL)** | Criterion A (Evaluating): Q1, Q2. Criterion B (Synthesizing): Q3, Q4. Criterion C (Reflecting): Q5, Q6. Mathematics-discipline marks are embedded inside Criterion A/B as numerical accuracy checks (Q2b, Q4). |
| **Examiner annotation toolkit** | Page 3 lists the on-screen annotation tools available: Benefit of the doubt (BOD), Vertical wavy line, Horizontal wavy line, Underline tool, Incorrect Point (X), Tick, Highlight tool, Omission (λ), Unclear (?), On-page comment, Seen, Words to that effect (WTTE), Ellipse. These are MYP e-Assessment tools, not legacy paper annotations. |
