## Best-approach command-term glossary (from MS p. 3)

| Term | Definition (verbatim from MS) |
|---|---|
| **Compare and contrast** | Provide an answer from a number of possibilities. Recognise and state briefly a distinguishing fact or feature. |
| **State** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. |
| **Outline** | Give a brief account or summary. |
| **Describe** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. |
| **Explain** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. |
| **Evaluate** | Make an appraisal by weighing up the strengths and limitations. |
| **Analyse** | Break down in order to bring out the essential elements or structure. To identify parts and relationships, and to interpret information to reach conclusions. |
| **Develop** | Improve incrementally, elaborate or expand in detail. Evolve to a more advanced or effective state. |
| **Formulate** | Express precisely and systematically the relevant concept(s) or argument(s). |
| **Identify** | Provide an answer from a number of possibilities. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. |
| **To what extent** | Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument. |

### Question 1 — Scientific Method & RCTs in Diabetes Drug Discovery (10 marks)

**Stimulus (described, not reproduced):** Two trials in the drug-discovery process for diabetes treatment — one trial uses **insulin**, one trial uses **treatment of diabetes**. The scientific method used by each trial is presented. (The MS instructs examiners to refer to use of insulin or treatment of diabetes — answers are not allowed as these are given in the question.)

#### 1a — Compare and contrast the scientific method used in these two trials (6 marks) | Criterion B

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Compare and contrast** the scientific method used in these two trials. In your answer, you must give:*
- *three similarities*
- *three differences."*

**Examiner instructions (verbatim from MS):**
- "Referral to use of insulin or treatment of diabetes are not allowed as these are given in the question."
- "For differences, elements from both trials must be explicitly referred to. These can be in either order."
- "Answers must relate to the scientific method."
- (1 mark) for each similarity, maximum (3 marks).
- (1 mark) for each difference, maximum (3 marks).

**Mark scheme answer table — accept any of the following pairings:**

| Similarities | Differences |
|---|---|
| Random assignment of patients to the two groups (experimental/control) | Trial 1 is not blinded **and** trial 2 is double blind/blind (or words to that effect) |
| Control group is standard treatment/Control group used as a comparison | Trial 1 has a **dependent variable** of blood glucose and other symptoms measured **and** trial 2 only has blood glucose as a **dependent variable** |
| Experimental group is the new treatment | Trial 1 has a qualitative **and** quantitative **dependent variable and** trial 2 only has a quantitative **dependent variable** |
| Dependent variable is blood glucose | Trial 1 uses all age groups **and** trial 2 uses a restricted age group |
| Dependent variable is quantitative | |

**Key MS notes:**
- 6 marks total: 1 mark per similarity (max 3) + 1 mark per difference (max 3).
- For differences, both trials must be explicitly referenced; the order does not matter.
- "Use of insulin" or "treatment of diabetes" cannot be claimed as similarities/differences — these are given in the question stem.
- Accept WTTE phrasings of "blinded" / "double blind".

#### 1b — Explain two strengths of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) in the drug discovery process (4 marks) | Criterion B

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Explain** two strengths of using randomized controlled trials (RCTs) in the drug discovery process."*

**Examples of strengths the MS will reward (verbatim):**
- *Random* — "The idea that participants are allocated to the experimental/control group randomly, means there is reduced bias, less chance of one group having preferential characteristics. Idea that when patients don't know which group they are in, placebo effects can be seen."
- *Control* — "The idea that other than what is being trialled, everything else is the same, that any effect must be caused by the difference of treatment. Dependent variables clearly defined."
- *Trial* — "Sample of the population i.e. not everyone has to be tested."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1 | The student identifies one strength. |
| 2 | The student identifies **two** strengths.<br>**or**<br>The student provides **one** strength with a reason. |
| 3 | The student identifies **two** strengths, **one** with a reason. |
| 4 | The student identifies **two** strengths **and** provides a reason for **both** strengths. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The 1-vs-2 strengths and the reason qualifier together drive the band; both strengths AND a reason for each are required for full marks.
- "Sample of the population i.e. not everyone has to be tested" is an explicitly accepted strength under "Trial".

### Question 2 — Author's Purpose & Narrative Development (10 marks)

#### 2a — Identify the author's purpose for each selection (2 marks) | Criterion A

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Identify** the author's purpose for each selection below."*

**Stimulus:** Two text selections (Selection 1 and Selection 2) presented as drag-and-drop targets. Draggable purposes given to the candidate: **Entertain / Persuade / Describe / Inform**.

**Mark scheme answers:** Selection 1 is **persuade**; Selection 2 is **inform**.

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not match any of the selections with the correct author's purpose. |
| 1 | The student matches **one** of the selections with the correct author's purpose. |
| 2 | The student matches **both** selections with the correct author's purpose. |

**Key MS notes:** Strict 1-mark-per-correct-match; partial credit only for matching one of the two.

#### 2b — Develop the missing narrative elements for the provided climax (8 marks) | Criterion B

**Question (verbatim):** *"From the perspective of a child with diabetes, **develop** the missing narrative elements for the provided climax."*

**Provided climax (verbatim from MS):** *"My mother lost her job, which means we don't have any way to cover the cost of my insulin. My current supply will run out in 30 days. We don't know what to do, or who to turn to."*

**Narrative-element labels (verbatim from MS):**
- Exposition — Introduction
- Rising action — Building story
- Climax — **Point of tension provided in the question**
- Falling action — Tension eases
- Resolution — Story concludes

**Examiner instructions (verbatim):**
- "Only elements from the perspective of the child and relating to the climax can receive credit."
- "The student must write in each box the narrative element that respects the literary component specified above the box as explained below."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The student **states**:<br>• Exposition<br>• Rising action<br>• Falling action<br>• Resolution |
| 3–4 | The student **outlines**:<br>• Exposition<br>• Rising action<br>• Falling action<br>• Resolution |
| 5–6 | The student **describes**:<br>• Exposition<br>• Rising action<br>• Falling action<br>• Resolution |
| 7–8 | The student **develops**:<br>• Exposition<br>• Rising action<br>• Falling action<br>• Resolution |

**Key MS notes:**
- The four band labels (states / outlines / describes / develops) map to a single rising scale of narrative-elaboration depth.
- Both perspective (child) and link to the climax must be present — generic narrative entries do not earn marks.
- The climax itself is not awarded (it is given in the stem); credit is only for the four surrounding elements.

### Question 3 — Justify Best Healthcare System from Indicators (8 marks) | Criterion B / D

**Stimulus (described):** A reference table containing data on national healthcare systems against four indicators — **Quality of Health Care, Burden of Healthcare, Prescription Spending, Doctor-to-Patient ratio**.

**Question (verbatim):** *"Based on the information above, **justify** which country has the best healthcare system. In your answer, you must address all **four** indicators of healthcare."*

**Examiner instructions (verbatim):**
- "To apply best fit each mark is worth (1 mark) and each indicator is worth (2 marks)."
- "Indicators are: Quality of Health Care, Burden of Healthcare, Prescription Spending, Doctor to Patient ratio."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The student addresses the following points:<br>• **outlines** 2 indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system.<br>**or**<br>• explains 1 indicator justifying their choice of healthcare system. |
| 3–4 | The student addresses the following points:<br>• **outlines** 4 other indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system.<br>**or**<br>• explains 2 indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system. |
| 5–6 | The student addresses the following points:<br>• **outlines** 2 indicators **and** **outlines** 2 indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system.<br>**or**<br>• explains 3 indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system. |
| 7–8 | The student addresses the following points:<br>• **explains** all 4 indicators justifying their choice of healthcare system. |

**Key MS notes:**
- Each indicator is worth 2 marks; bands move from "outline" toward "explain" as depth increases.
- All four indicators must be present at the top band — partial coverage caps at lower bands.
- The student must commit to a single country ("their choice"); justification must reference data, not opinion.

### Question 4 — Contrast Synthesis of Language & Literature with Science (12 marks) | Criterion C

**Stimulus (described):** Two speakers each present an argument that synthesises language & literature and science. The candidate is required to contrast — not merely compare — how both speakers integrate the two disciplines.

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Contrast** how both speakers synthesize language and literature and science in their arguments. In your answer, you must include:*
- *elements of language and literature from both speakers*
- *elements of science from both speakers*
- *a conclusion."*

**Examiner instructions (verbatim):**
- "If no examples are given to support the differences, then the maximum mark is 6."
- "If only one speaker is discussed, then 0 should be awarded."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | The student attempts to **contrast** by addressing the following points:<br>• **states** the differences in language and literature from both speakers<br>• **states** the differences in science from both speakers<br>• no conclusion is given. |
| 4–6 | The student attempts to **contrast** by addressing the following points:<br>• **outlines** the differences in language and literature from both speakers<br>• **outlines** the differences in science from both speakers<br>• attempts a conclusion. |
| 7–9 | The student **contrasts** by addressing the following points:<br>• **describes** the differences in language and literature from both speakers<br>• **describes** the differences in science from both speakers<br>• provides a conclusion. |
| 10–12 | The student fully **contrasts** by addressing the following points:<br>• **explains** the differences in language and literature from both speakers<br>• **explains** the differences in science from both speakers<br>• provides an evaluative conclusion. |

**Key MS notes:**
- Hard-cap rule: omitting examples caps the response at 6/12.
- Hard-zero rule: discussing only one speaker = 0/12. Both speakers are mandatory throughout.
- Top band (10-12) requires an *evaluative* conclusion — not just a summary or restatement.

### Question 5 — Drug Company Opposition (Reference 6) (20 marks)

#### 5a — Formulate the drug company's opposition to Reference 6 (12 marks) | Criterion B

**Question (verbatim):** *"You are a representative for a drug company opposing the proposition in reference 6. **Formulate** your opposition. In your answer, you must use:*
- *arguments written in the same box*
- *PRM does not need to be referenced or cited."*

**Examiner instructions (verbatim):**
- "Credit two arguments written in the same box."
- "PRM does not need to be referenced or cited."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | The student:<br>• attempts an opening statement<br>• states **one** argument supporting the drug company's point of view.<br>• attempts a closing statement. |
| 4–6 | The student:<br>• **states** an opening statement<br>• **outlines two** arguments supporting the drug company's point of view<br>• attempts to use evidence from the PRM<br>• states a closing statement. |
| 7–9 | The student:<br>• **outlines** an opening statement that expresses the drug company's point of view<br>• **describes two** arguments supporting the drug company's point of view<br>• uses evidence from the PRM to directly support **one** argument<br>• a closing statement is given in the debate. |
| 10–12 | The student:<br>• **outlines** an opening statement that expresses the drug company's point of view and their subsequent arguments<br>• **justifies two** arguments supporting the drug company's point of view<br>• uses evidence from the PRM to directly support **both** arguments<br>• a closing statement is given directly linking to **both** arguments in the debate. |

**Key MS notes:**
- The four-element structure (opening, two arguments, evidence linkage, closing) is rigidly tracked across bands.
- "PRM" = pre-release material. Citation is not required, but evidence linkage is — the difference between bands 7-9 and 10-12 hinges on whether **both** arguments are supported by PRM evidence and whether the closing statement directly links to both.
- The role-play frame ("You are a representative for a drug company") must be sustained — generic anti-proposition writing is not credited.

### Question 6 — To what extent is healthcare a right? (12 marks) | Criterion D

**Question (verbatim):** *"**To what extent** is healthcare a right? In your answer, you must include:*
- *the extent to which healthcare is a right*
- *the extent to which healthcare is not a right*
- *evidence to support your arguments*
- *a conclusion."*

**Examiner instructions (verbatim):** "If a candidate only discusses one side, the maximum allowable score is (6 marks). Responses should relate to whether it should be a right, and not about the pros and cons of it being a right."

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | The student:<br>• **states** the extent to which healthcare is a right<br>• **states** the extent to which healthcare is not a right<br>• attempts a conclusion. |
| 4–6 | The student:<br>• **outlines** the extent to which healthcare is a right<br>• **outlines** the extent to which healthcare is not a right<br>• attempts a conclusion. |
| 7–9 | The student:<br>• **describes** the extent to which healthcare is a right using evidence<br>• **describes** the extent to which healthcare is not a right using evidence<br>• provides a conclusion. |
| 10–12 | The student:<br>• **explains** the extent to which healthcare is a right using evidence<br>• **explains** the extent to which healthcare is not a right using evidence<br>• provides an evaluative conclusion. |

**Key MS notes:**
- Hard-cap rule: discussing only one side caps the response at 6/12.
- Scope rule: responses must address whether healthcare *should* be a right — discussing the *pros and cons* of it being a right is **off-task** and not credited.
- The top band requires **explanation with evidence on both sides** plus an **evaluative** conclusion.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns (M21 IDL)

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Banded mark schemes** | Every question except 1a/1b/2a uses a banded rubric (1–2 / 3–4 / 5–6 / 7–8 etc.). Bands map cleanly onto the command-term ladder: states → outlines → describes → explains / develops / justifies. |
| **Command-term ladder** | The MS uses a consistent four-step depth scale across most banded questions: state, outline, describe, explain (or its top-band equivalent: develop, justify, explain with evaluation). |
| **Hard caps for partial coverage** | Q4 caps at 6/12 if no examples given; Q6 caps at 6/12 if only one side is discussed. Q4 also has a hard-zero if only one speaker is discussed. |
| **Role-play frames** | Q5a places the student as "a representative for a drug company" — the frame must be sustained for credit. |
| **Reflection on own work** | Q5b explicitly limits credit to analysis of the candidate's *own* synthesis in 5a; generic content is ignored. Q7 rewards reflection on the candidate's *own* perception of medical systems, not the systems in the abstract. |
| **Mandatory both-sides** | Q6 requires both "is a right" and "is not a right" arguments; Q1a requires both similarities and differences (3 of each); Q4 requires both speakers throughout. |
| **PRM (pre-release material)** | Repeatedly referenced in the MS. Q5a does not require formal citation but rewards explicit evidence linkage to PRM in both arguments at the top band. |
| **Off-task warnings** | Q6: discussing pros-and-cons of healthcare as a right is off-task (the question asks whether it *should* be a right). Q1a: referring to "use of insulin / treatment of diabetes" as similarities or differences is not allowed because they are given in the stem. |
| **Evaluative conclusions** | Top bands across Q4, Q6, Q5b, Q7 all require an *evaluative* (not summative) conclusion — a key discriminator between bands 7-9 and 10-12 / 5-6 and 7-8. |
| **No marking for citation form** | Q5a explicitly: "PRM does not need to be referenced or cited" — content over form. |
| **Both-arguments-supported** | Q5a's top-band discriminator is that **both** arguments (not just one) are supported by PRM evidence, and the closing statement links to **both**. |
