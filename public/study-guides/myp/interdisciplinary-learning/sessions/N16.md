### Question 1 — Disciplinary Grounding: CO2 and Bean-Plant Growth (14 marks)

**MS instruction at top:** "Note: Before marking, please familiarize yourself with the pre-release material, all sources, the questions and the markscheme."

**Stimulus (described in MS):** The investigation produced three trials of graphs of bean-plant growth at four atmospheric CO2 conditions: zero CO2, atmospheric CO2 (×1), 2× atmospheric CO2, and 3× atmospheric CO2.

---

#### 1a — Compare the graphs generated by the three trials (4 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Compare** the graphs generated by the three trials."*

**Markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–3 | Award **(1 mark)**, up to a maximum of **(3 marks)**, for any response from: <br>• The graph for zero CO2 / trial 1 indicates that plants will not grow <br>• The graph for atmospheric CO2 / trial 2 indicates that plants grow at a slow pace/rate <br>• The graph for above atmospheric CO2 / trial 3 indicates that plants grow at a fast pace/rate <br>• The plants exposed to above atmospheric CO2 / trial 3 levels of CO2 grow taller |
| 4 | The student includes three of the points below **and** makes the comparison to growth rates of atmospheric to above atmospheric CO2 levels. <br>• The graph for zero CO2 / trial 1 indicates that plants will not grow <br>• The graph for atmospheric CO2 / trial 2 indicates that plants grow at a slow pace/rate <br>• The graph for above atmospheric CO2 / trial 3 indicates that plants grow at a fast pace/rate <br>• The plants exposed to above atmospheric CO2 / trial 3 grow taller. <br>**Therefore plants exposed to above atmospheric CO2 levels grow faster than plants exposed to atmospheric level of CO2** (this comparison may be implicit in their wording of the 2nd and 3rd bullet). |

**Key MS notes:** The 4th-mark threshold is the explicit *comparative* statement linking atmospheric vs above-atmospheric CO2 — implicit comparison embedded in the bullet wording is acceptable.

#### 1b — Evaluate the experimental method (8 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Evaluate** the experimental method used in this experiment. In your answer you should: identify strengths and limitations in the experimental method; suggest improvements."*

**MS guidance:** "To achieve more than (4 marks) both strengths **and** limitations need to be included in the answer. To achieve more than (4 marks) suggestions for improvement must be given."

**Markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | The answer is **limited**. The student identifies one from: <br>• Limited information provided by the researcher on control variables e.g. temperature, watering, soil type <br>• Some control variables are given <br>• An issue with the number of trials e.g. should use more than 10 plants <br>• The independent and dependent variables are not stated clearly <br>• Any appropriate strength or limitation with the details provided in the method <br>• The researcher did not specify a hypothesis <br>• Lack of numerical value for CO2 <br>• Position of the light source relative to the pots |
| 3–4 | The description is **adequate**. The student identifies two issues from the same list above. **The student suggests one improvement.** |
| 5–6 | The explanation is **clear**. The student identifies three issues from the list above. **The student suggests two improvements.** |
| 7–8 | The evaluation is **thorough**. The student identifies four issues from the list above. **The student suggests two improvements.** |

**Key MS notes:** The rubric explicitly couples the *number of issues identified* with the *number of improvements suggested* across the bands. Failing to give improvements caps the answer at 4 marks.

#### 1c — Suggest two changes for a study on animal life (2 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Suggest** two changes to the investigation above that would make it suitable for a conscientious scientist interested in the impact of climate change on animal life."*

**Mark scheme answer (max 2 marks; award 1 mark each):**
- You should not use the zero CO2 or O2 part of the experiment.
- You should ensure the wellbeing of the animal is carefully monitored.
- You should ensure there is sufficient water for the animal.
- You should ensure there is sufficient food for the animal.
- You should ensure there is sufficient space for the animal.
- You should ensure the temperature is appropriate.

**Key MS notes:** All six bullets are interchangeable acceptable responses; the question rewards transfer of an experimental design from plants to animals with ethical/welfare adaptations.

### Question 2 — Overfishing: Stakeholder Perspectives & Sustainability Analysis (16 marks)

**Stimulus (described in MS):** Sources 3, 5 and 6 are referenced (visual-references — described, not reproduced). They concern overfishing and its implications for marine life. #### 2a — Stakeholders and their perspectives (4 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** *"With reference to **Sources 3, 5 and 6** provided in the tabs below, select **two** different stakeholders and **outline** their different perspectives about fishing and the implications of overfishing. An example has been provided from a marine biologist. Implications could be taken from economic, social, political, environmental, ethical, legal or technological factors."*

**MS note:** "The answers given below are sample answers worth (1 mark) each, other sensible perspectives and implications are acceptable providing they link to the stakeholder."

**Sample-answer table from MS:**

| Stakeholder | Perspective | Implications of overfishing |
|---|---|---|
| **Example: Marine biologist** | Scientific data can be collected about fishing | Scientists will have to advise policy makers |
| Consumer | Fish is an essential source of protein / Popular food | As species decline the price of fish will increase |
| Fisher | Fishing is essential to my livelihood | The population of fish will drop and I will have no income or job |
| Corporate fishing company | We have efficient methods that allow us to catch many fish at a time | They will have less efficiency and smaller catches |
| Environmentalist | We must ensure we protect marine environments | The marine environment will be changed forever / Destruction of habitats |
| Policy maker | There should be regulations that are followed in national and international waters | More legislation and fines for illegal fishing |

**Marking:** 1 mark per cell — i.e. each correct stakeholder-paired *perspective* and *implication* is worth one mark up to the cap of 4 marks (2 stakeholders × 2 cells).

#### 2b — Analyse the impact of fishing on sustainability of marine life (12 marks)

**Question (verbatim):** *"**Analyse** the impact of fishing on the sustainability of marine life. With reference to the sources provided in the tabs, use the analysis table below to provide relevant information and reach a conclusion based on your analysis. In your answer you should: <br>• consider a strength and weakness of fishing in terms of sustainability <br>• mention an opportunity for sustainability of marine life <br>• mention a threat that may affect the sustainability of marine life <br>• give a conclusion."*

**Markband rubric:**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 0 | The student does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. |
| 1–2 | No examples or quotations are used. The analysis is **brief and limited**. The student writes a simple analysis that identifies one from: <br>• consider the strengths of the sustainability of fishing methods <br>• consider the weaknesses of the sustainability of fishing methods <br>• take into account opportunities for sustainability of marine life <br>• mention threats that make affect the sustainability of marine life. <br>The conclusions are limited. |
| 3–5 | Using at least one example/quotation. The answer is **adequate**. The student writes an analysis with reason or evidence to explain a connection with two from the same list. The conclusions are adequate. |
| 6–8 | Using two examples/quotations. The answer is **detailed and balanced**. The student writes an analysis with valid reasons and evidence to explain a connection with three from the list. The conclusions are detailed and balanced. |
| 9–12 | Using at least three examples/quotations. The answer is **thorough and perceptive**. The student writes an effective analysis with valid well supported reasons and evidence to explain a connection with all four from the list (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats). **Key MS notes:** This is effectively a SWOT-style analysis. Progression up the band is driven by (a) coverage of all four SWOT elements and (b) number of source examples / quotations used.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Details |
|---|---|
| **Levelled markbands, not point-marks, dominate** | Q1b, Q2b, Q3a, Q3b, Q4b, Q5, Q6, Q7 — every extended task — uses 4-band rubrics (limited / adequate / detailed / thorough). Only Q1a (max-4 hybrid), Q1c (point-marked), Q2a (cell-by-cell), Q4a (1 mark per item) are point-marked. |
| **Coverage gates** | Bands often *gate* progression on coverage of named bullet points. Examples: Q3a (4 sub-bullets, top band needs all 4); Q4b (4 elements: benefits/limitations/evidence/conclusion, top band needs all 4); Q6 (4 features of sustainability, 1→2→3→4 across bands); Q7 (4 reflection bullets, 1→2→3→4 across bands). |
| **Source-evidence gate** | Q2b, Q3a, Q4b explicitly require source examples/quotations to enter mid-to-top bands. Q3a band 1–3 is reserved for responses with *no* source examples. Q2b ladder: 1 quotation → 2 → ≥3 across the bands. |
| **Discipline-balance cap** | Q3b: "A maximum of (5 marks) can be given if only one of the disciplines from science and individuals and society has been mentioned." Q4a: "maximum of (2 marks) for each individual discipline" — forces 2 from science + 2 from I&S. |
| **Format-following gate** | Q5: "To receive (8 marks) and above, the context of the script for the social guidelines must be followed" — answers ignoring the document format are capped at 7. |
| **Coupled criteria within one rubric (Q1b)** | Strengths-and-limitations identification is coupled to *number of improvements suggested*: 1–2 (one issue, no improvements); 3–4 (two issues, one improvement); 5–6 (three issues, two improvements); 7–8 (four issues, two improvements). The "more than 4 marks requires improvements" rule is stated explicitly at the top of the question. |
