# 2-Week IDL Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of IDL per day**. IDL has no fixed syllabus — your prep is about *technique* (synthesis, source use, reflection, command-term ladder) applied to whatever **pre-release material (PRM)** you receive. Adjust the daily slot to fit your schedule.

> **Before Day 1**: confirm you have the PRM for your sitting. If it is already released, print it and read it once cold before starting Day 1. The single biggest predictor of a top band is *explicit linkage to the PRM*.

## Priority if time is tight

If IDL time gets squeezed, protect these in order:

1. **Master the synthesis bullet** — both disciplines explicitly named, then woven; single-discipline answers are capped at half-marks or below in nearly every paper. This is the single highest-leverage skill.
2. **Drill the "to what extent" template** — opinion + agree-side + disagree-side + justified conclusion. Covers 8 of 10 sessions' reflective questions.
3. **Memorise the verb ladder cold** — states -> outlines -> describes -> explains/justifies/evaluates. Every banded answer must land on the top rung.
4. **Practise reflective journal under timed conditions** — Q6 / Q7 / Q4 closes nearly every paper; bullet-coverage gate + both-sides + PRM evidence + justified conclusion is a fixed recipe.
5. **Drill PRM source quoting** — top bands require >=2 or >=3 explicit quotations with reference numbers. Cite verbatim, not paraphrase.
6. **Memorise the M24 concept lists** — 12 maths + 12 L&L concepts. Concept-naming is the explicit top-band gate in M24 and the implicit "key concept" gate in N16 Q3b, N19 Q2a, N21 Q1d, M22 Q1b.
7. **Past paper M24 timed** — only session with both paper and MS; closest to current format (compressed, banded, PRM-anchored).

## What top markbands really need

High-leverage techniques drawn from MS patterns across all 10 sessions:

- **Name both disciplines explicitly**, not implicitly. "From a mathematical perspective..." / "From a language and literature perspective...". Single-discipline answers are hard-capped (M21 Q4 hard-zeros if only one speaker discussed; M23 Q2 caps at 6/12; M17 Q4b caps at 2/8; N16 Q3b caps at 5/10).
- **Cite PRM verbatim with reference numbers** — paraphrasing is read down a band. Aim for >=2 distinct references per 12-mark question.
- **Name the related concepts** — "this uses *Quantity* and *Models*..." rather than "this uses numbers and graphs". M24's top band requires explicit, accurate concept use; earlier sessions require key-concept naming (systems / global interactions).
- **Cover both sides** when the question implies it (strengths/weaknesses, for/against, similarities/differences, positive/negative consequences). One-sided responses are structurally capped.
- **Land a justified conclusion** at the end of every extended response — not just "in conclusion" but a defended judgement supported by PRM evidence. The conclusion ladder (no / attempts / provides / *justified*) is the single biggest band-mover on extended questions.
- **Honour the format/role gate** — script format, role-play frame, stakeholder card, aid-distribution context. Auto-zeros and hard caps live here.
- **Communicate clearly and coherently** — the verbatim intra-band tiebreaker phrase. Use signposting, paragraphs, and a clear opening sentence per strand.
- **Cover all bullet points** in the question stem — top-band entry is gated by full bullet coverage in N16 Q3a, N16 Q6/Q7, M17 Q4a/Q6a, N20 Q3a/Q3b/Q5, N19 Q5–Q7, M19 Q7, M24 Q4.

## Common student traps

- **Single-discipline bias** — answering with only Maths *or* only Sciences when both are required. Half-mark cap or hard-zero in 7+ sessions.
- **No source examples** — writing in generalities instead of quoting PRM. Drops you to band 1–3 on questions like N16 Q3a.
- **Off-context responses** — answering an adjacent question instead of the one set. M21 Q6 explicitly warns "responses should relate to whether it should be a right, *not* about the pros and cons of it being a right". M22 Q2a discounts descriptions of the *method* of calculation when the question asks about strengths/weaknesses of the *representation*.
- **Comparing the wrong objects** — M23 Q1 auto-zeros the first two strands if you compare the *cars* instead of the *brochures synthesising maths + science*.
- **Format-not-followed cap** — script (N16 Q5), legal-support areas (M19 Q5a), role-play frame (M21 Q5a), correct stakeholder card (M22 Q1a), aid-distribution context (N21 Q3a). Hard caps of 3 marks or auto-zero.
- **No conclusion or weak conclusion** — "in conclusion, this is important" is *attempts a conclusion* (band 3–4 / 4–6). You need a justified judgement to reach the top band.
- **Listing concepts instead of using them** — M24's gate requires concepts to *drive* the explanation, not appear in a closing list.
- **Ignoring "outside material" cues** — M17 Q1c top band requires outside (non-PRM) sources too; M19 Q7 requires a Service-as-Action example from band 3–4 upward.
- **Treating the reflective Q as an afterthought** — Q6/Q7/Q4 carries 6–30 marks and has the same bullet-coverage + both-sides + PRM-evidence + justified-conclusion structure as every other extended Q.
- **Spelling panic** — M24 verbatim: "Spelling errors should not impact the marks awarded." Don't burn time editing, burn it on PRM quotes and the conclusion.

## Past-paper practice list

Order to attempt them in (newest first; sit M24 first because it's the only one with the live paper):

1. **M24** (42 marks, ~75 min) — Maths + L&L; only session with public paper + MS; closest to current format. *Sit twice if time allows: once for full marks, once for concept-naming drill.*
2. **M23** (36 marks, ~60 min) — Maths + Sciences; shortest paper; clean drill of the *and-both* discipline-balance gate.
3. **M22** (62 marks, ~110 min) — Maths + I&S; includes Q6 reflective drill (12 marks) and the trigonometric exclusion-zone format.
4. **N21** (70 marks, ~125 min) — L&L + I&S; humanitarian context; strong source-count gates (Q2b) and aid-distribution format gate (Q3a).
5. **M21** (80 marks, ~140 min) — Sciences + L&L + I&S triple; healthcare; role-play gate (Q5a) and "to what extent" closing question.
6. **N20** (80 marks, ~140 min) — Maths + L&L + I&S triple; Olympics; Q3a explicit-evidence gate, Q5 city-coverage cap.
7. **N19** (68 marks, ~120 min) — Sciences + I&S; food systems; both-sides gates throughout.
8. **M19** (80 marks, ~140 min) — Maths + I&S; freedom of speech; Q5a legal-area hard-zero gate, Q7 SAA hook.
9. **M17** (100 marks, ~175 min) — Maths + I&S; maps & navigation; Q5 Mars-viability examiner blacklist; rich source-count gates.
10. **N16** (120 marks, ~210 min) — Sciences + I&S; sustainability; longest paper; full Q1b strengths-+-limitations gate, Q5 script format gate, Q6/Q7 4-bullet reflective gate. *Optional* — only attempt if you have completed M24 and at least two others; the format is older and longer than what you will sit.

**Strategy**: do M24 fully and timed first (Day 8). Do M23 fully and timed second (Day 11). Use the remaining sessions for *targeted question drills* on weaknesses, not full timed sittings — the format compression means M24 + M23 are the closest match to your live exam.
