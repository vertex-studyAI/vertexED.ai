## Question 1 — Weighing at a Market (5 marks)

**Stimulus:** A diagram of a basic balance scale used at a market. The handler uses batteries and metal weights. As shown in the diagram, the combined weight of **two** metal weights and **three** batteries is 305 grams (g). One metal weight measures 100 g.

### Q1a (3 marks) — Find

Using the information from the diagram, **find** the weight of one battery.

**Mark scheme:**
- •¹ Evidence of substituting one metal weight for 100 g correctly (seeing 200 or 200 + 3b = 305; accept not seeing this step)
- •² Evidence of correct operation leading to the weight of one battery (subtracting 200 from 305 or seeing 105 or 3b = their 105 or dividing their 105 by 3)
- •³ Their correct answer: b = 35 (gm)
- 35 with no working: award 2 marks
- 35 with one correct operation step: award 3 marks
- Footnote: Accept seeing only numbers in equations or seeing expressions in words

### Q1b (2 marks) — Calculate

Ten similar sized tomatoes weigh the same as **three** metal weights and **two** batteries. **Calculate** the weight in grams of one tomato.

**Mark scheme:**
- •¹ Evidence of one correct operation step to find the total: (10 tomatoes) = 3 × 100 + 2 × their 35, or seeing their 370, or their 370/10
- •² their 370 correctly divided by 10: 37 (g)
- If a further algebraic step divides the final answer by 10 or 100 or 1000: do not award the •²
- 37 (gm) without working: award 1 mark
- Footnote: Award ECF after their wrong weight in part (a) of one battery only if there is working for •¹

## Question 5 — Bridge Height Using Trigonometry (5 marks)

**Stimulus:** An engineer is examining a weak bridge from a safe distance. A theodolite (measuring instrument) at height 1.2 m is placed 57.25 m from the nearest centimetre to the bridge base. The angle of elevation from the horizontal to the top of the arch at B is measured at 22° to the nearest degree. A diagram shows the theodolite at height 1.2 m, horizontal distance 57.25 m, angle 22°, point A at the base and B at the top of the bridge. Formulas provided: Pythagoras' Theorem c² = a² + b² and trigonometric ratios (sin, cos, tan).

### Q5 (5 marks) — Calculate

**Calculate** the height from the top of the bridge at B to the ground vertically below at A to the nearest centimetre.

**Mark scheme:**
- tan 22°
- substitutes correctly into tan ratio
- calculates the height above the theodolite
- (their) height + 1.2
- rounds their height correctly to the nearest cm
- tan22° so height = 57.25 × tan22° = 23.1305...
- •³ 23.1305..., + 1.2 = 24.33 (m). Accept 24.33 (m)
- Accept seeing rounding to nearest cm in earlier step
- Alternatively (using cosine ratio): substitutes correctly → cos22° = 57.25 / hypotenuse → hypotenuse = 61.746... → then calculates the height above the theodolite → their height + 1.2 = 24.33 (m)
- 23.1305 with no working: award 2 marks; 23.13 with no working: award 3 marks; 24.33 with no working: award 4 marks; 24.33 with one correct step: award 5 marks
- Footnote: WTTE: accept incomplete calculator display
