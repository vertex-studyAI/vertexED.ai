Covers all 18 sessions: M16, N16, M17, N17, M18, N18, M19, N19, N20, M21, N21, M22, N22, M23, N23, M24, N24, M25. (No M20 — cancelled COVID-19.)

## Exam structure (consistent across sessions)

- **Format**: On-screen e-Assessment. All questions on-screen; answers typed, drawn (graph tool), or selected (drop-down / MCQ).
- **Duration**: Approximately 1 hour 45 minutes (shorter sessions ~47 marks up to ~110 marks depending on year).
- **Questions**: 7–12 questions per paper, each subdivided into parts (a, b, c …). Marks range from [1] to [17] per part.
- **Criteria**: Every paper assesses all four criteria. Criterion B (design) and Criterion D (evaluate/discuss) always have dedicated multi-mark question blocks.
- **Stimulus material**: Every question is anchored to a real-world context (article extract, data table, diagram, photograph). The context changes; the underlying chemistry skills do not.
- **Formulae and data**: A data booklet / periodic table is provided on-screen. Relative atomic masses are given; Avogadro's constant and key equations are typically supplied where needed.

## Criterion breakdown

| Criterion | Description | Typical marks per paper | Typical question types |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **A** — Knowing and Understanding | Recall definitions, name/identify elements/compounds, explain reactions, perform calculations (moles, stoichiometry, pH, q=mcΔT), balance equations, draw Lewis structures | 25–45 | State, Identify, Calculate, Explain, Outline, Name, Draw, Balance, Determine |
| **B** — Inquiring and Designing | Design a full experiment: formulate a focused question, hypothesis (with justification), identify and control variables, write a step-by-step method, list equipment, state safety precautions, describe data collection | 14–18 | Design, Formulate, Identify (IV/DV/CV), Outline (method/apparatus), State (safety) |
| **C** — Processing and Evaluating | Process raw data (calculate averages, handle anomalies), plot or interpret graphs, evaluate experimental method, suggest improvements, assess reliability and validity, apply ECF in calculation chains | 15–25 | Calculate, Plot, Present, Draw, Evaluate, Suggest, Comment, Identify (trend/anomaly), Determine |
| **D** — Reflecting on the Impacts of Science | Extended discussion of real-world, societal, economic, or environmental impacts; argue perspectives; justify using evidence from stimulus; evaluate solutions | 9–17 | Discuss, Evaluate, Justify, Suggest, Compare, Assess |

**Key patterns by criterion:**
- Criterion B is always one discrete question block, usually anchored to an investigation introduced earlier in the paper. Marks are assigned by markband (holistic).
- Criterion D is always the final question block (or penultimate). Extended-response answers typically need 3–6 distinct points for top-band marks.
- Criterion A dominates by question count (roughly half the paper's parts).
- Criterion C data-processing questions often appear mid-paper, tied to the same context as Criterion A questions.

## Mark scheme patterns

### WTTE (Words To That Effect)
- Present in **all 18 sessions** — the default tolerance marker.
- Applies to any answer requiring a written description or explanation; any scientifically equivalent phrasing scores the mark.
- Implication: do not memorise set phrases; understand the concept.

### ECF (Error Carried Forward)
- Present in **all 18 sessions** with calculation chains.
- An incorrect answer in part (b) that is used correctly in part (c) will score in part (c).
- Explicitly flagged at individual sub-parts (e.g., "ECF from Q3c"). Always show working.
- Occasionally restricted: "no ECF" noted in 2–3 sessions where the error would give an unrealistic value.

### ORA (Or Reverse Argument)
- Present in at least **14/18 sessions**.
- Common in rates-of-reaction explanations, equilibrium shifts, and energy questions where the inverse argument is equally valid (e.g., "increased temperature increases rate" ORA "decreased temperature decreases rate").

### CON (Contradiction)
- Explicitly flagged in at least **4/18 sessions** (M23 Q1c, others implied).
- If a student writes a correct answer followed by a contradicting incorrect statement, the mark is awarded as 0.
- Implication: do not pad answers. Stop after the correct point.

### Other conventions
- **[1] max [N]**: More acceptable answers listed than marks; any correct N answers score.
- **Accept ⇌ or =**: Reversible reaction arrows accepted interchangeably in several sessions.
- **Allow ...**: Examiner notes an additional acceptable phrasing or spelling variant.
- **Markband (Criterion B and D)**: Holistic banding, not point-by-point. Must demonstrate all aspects of the highest band attempted to score it.

## High-frequency question types

These specific question structures appear in the majority of sessions — learn to answer them fluently.

1. **Locate and explain an element's position in the periodic table** — group, period, number of valence electrons, reactivity trend. Appears in 18/18 sessions.

2. **Write or balance a chemical equation** — symbol equation; sometimes ionic or half-equation. 16/18 sessions.

3. **Calculate molar mass / moles / number of particles** — n=m/M chain, often [2]–[3] marks. 16/18.

4. **Explain rate of reaction using collision theory** — catalyst, temperature, concentration, surface area. 16/18.

5. **Design an investigation** (Criterion B block) — IV, DV, 2–3 CVs, step-by-step method, equipment list, safety, data collection table. 18/18.

6. **Formulate a hypothesis** — "If [IV] increases, then [DV] will [change] because [scientific reason]". 14/18.

7. **Identify the independent / dependent / controlled variables**. 18/18.

8. **Calculate an average / identify anomalous results**. 16/18.

9. **Plot a graph**: axes labels with units, suitable scale, accurate points, line/curve of best fit. 14/18.

10. **Evaluate experimental method**: reliability, validity, improvements, sources of error. 15/18.

11. **Explain bonding and properties** — ionic vs covalent; melting point, conductivity, solubility linked to structure. 14/18.

12. **Explain exothermic / endothermic using bond energies or energy diagrams**. 12/18.

13. **Extended discussion of real-world/ethical impact** (Criterion D) — give ≥3 developed points; consider different stakeholder perspectives. 18/18.

14. **Interpret chromatography / Rf values** — calculate Rf, identify substances, comment on purity. 11/18.

15. **Hazard / GHS symbol identification and safe handling**. 10/18.

## Revision priorities

### Tier 1 — Guaranteed to appear (every session)
- Periodic table navigation and trends (groups, periods, valence electrons, reactivity)
- Atomic structure (proton/neutron/electron numbers, electron configuration, isotopes)
- Experimental design: IV/DV/CV, hypothesis, method, safety, data table (full Criterion B)
- Data processing: averages, outliers, graphs, evaluation, improvements (Criterion C)
- Extended real-world discussion with evidence-based argument (Criterion D)

### Tier 2 — Appear in nearly every session (15–17/18)
- Moles and stoichiometry: n=m/M, molar ratios, percentage calculations
- Balancing equations (word and symbol)
- Rates of reaction and collision theory
- Acids, bases, neutralisation, pH scale
- States of matter, particle theory, changes of state
- Organic functional groups, naming, polymers
- Environmental chemistry (greenhouse effect, acid rain, pollution)

### Tier 3 — Frequent but not universal (10–14/18)
- Ionic and covalent bonding; Lewis structures
- Electron configuration (shells and subshells)
- Combustion calculations (q=mcΔT)
- Exothermic / endothermic, energy diagrams
- Properties of metals and alloys
- Electrochemistry (electrolysis, batteries, half-equations)
- Hazard symbols (GHS)

### Tier 4 — Periodic topics (5–9/18)
- Equilibrium and Le Chatelier's principle
- Chromatography and separation techniques
- Redox and reactivity series
- Radioactivity and half-life
- Thermochemistry (ΔH calculations, bond energies)

### Exam technique tips
- **Answer in the required format**: "State" = 1–2 words; "Explain" = scientific reason required; "Discuss" = multiple developed points.
- **Show all working in calculations**: ECF applies — a wrong sub-answer used correctly downstream still scores.
- **Do not contradict yourself** (CON rule): once you've given a correct answer, do not add a statement that negates it.
- **For Criterion B**: explicitly label IV, DV, and CVs by name. List ≥3 controlled variables with how they are controlled. Write a step-by-step numbered method. Include a blank data collection table.
- **For Criterion D**: use the stimulus material — quote figures or references. Argue both sides before giving a reasoned conclusion. Aim for 3–6 distinct points at the top band.
- **WTTE is your friend**: focus on scientific accuracy of meaning, not exact phrasing.
