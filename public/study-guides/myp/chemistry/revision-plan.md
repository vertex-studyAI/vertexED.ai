# 2-Week Chemistry Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of Chemistry per day**, with other subjects filling the rest. Adjust the daily slot to fit your schedule.

---

## Week 1 — Content Consolidation

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 1** | Criteria B & D strategy | Read [experimental-design.md](topics/experimental-design.md) top to bottom. Write one full Criterion B answer from scratch (any past paper). Self-mark: IV with values, DV with units, ≥3 CVs with control methods, numbered method, data table, safety precautions. |
| **Day 2** | Periodic table + Atomic structure | Read [periodicity.md](topics/periodicity.md) and [atomic-structure.md](topics/atomic-structure.md). Practise locating elements (group, period, valence electrons, reactivity trend) and writing electron configurations. These appear in every single paper — guaranteed marks. |
| **Day 3** | Stoichiometry + Chemical reactions | Read [stoichiometry.md](topics/stoichiometry.md) and [chemical-reactions.md](topics/chemical-reactions.md). Practise the n=m/M chain, molar ratios, and balancing symbol equations from [M25](sessions/M25.md) and [N24](sessions/N24.md). |
| **Day 4** | Bonding + Organic chemistry | Read [bonding-and-structure.md](topics/bonding-and-structure.md) and [organic-chemistry.md](topics/organic-chemistry.md). Drill Lewis structures (lone pairs are the most common mistake). Practise naming functional groups and drawing structural formulae. |
| **Day 5** | Rates of reaction + Acids & bases | Read [rates-of-reaction.md](topics/rates-of-reaction.md) and [acids-bases-salts.md](topics/acids-bases-salts.md). Memorise the four rate factors and their collision-theory explanations. Practise neutralisation equations and pH calculations. |
| **Day 6** | Energy in reactions + Electrochemistry | Read [energy-in-reactions.md](topics/energy-in-reactions.md) and [electrochemistry.md](topics/electrochemistry.md). Focus on exo/endothermic energy diagrams, q=mcΔT, and electrolysis half-equations. |
| **Day 7** | Rest / light review | Skim [overview.md](overview.md) section 2 (topic frequency table). Identify any gaps from Days 1–6. Note the Tier 1 and Tier 2 priority lists. |

## Week 2 — Past Papers + Criterion D

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 8** | Criterion D essays | Read the Criterion D markband in [impacts-of-chemistry.md](topics/impacts-of-chemistry.md). Write one practice extended response on plastics sustainability or nuclear energy. Aim for 3–6 distinct developed points using evidence from the stimulus; argue both sides before concluding. |
| **Day 9** | Full past paper (timed) | Do [N24](sessions/N24.md) under timed conditions (80 marks). Mark yourself strictly. Note every question you dropped marks on — especially Criterion B and D. |
| **Day 10** | Fix weaknesses | Go back to the topics you dropped marks on in Day 9. Re-read those topic pages, then redo just those question types. Pay special attention to the most common mistakes: "rate" without specifying what's measured, Lewis structures missing lone pairs, isotope definitions missing one half. |
| **Day 11** | Criterion C skills | Read [data-processing.md](topics/data-processing.md). Practise calculating averages and identifying outliers, plotting graphs (axes labels with units, line of best fit), and writing evaluation pairs: weakness + specific, actionable improvement. |
| **Day 12** | Second full past paper | Do [M25](sessions/M25.md) (most recent, 96-mark format — closest to what you'll sit). Mark it. Note which criterion you lost the most marks in. |
| **Day 13** | Criterion B drill | Write one full design answer cold. Check: focused research question, hypothesis in if-then format with scientific justification, IV with values, DV with units, ≥3 CVs with control methods, numbered step-by-step method, equipment list, data collection table, safety precautions. |
| **Day 14** | Command terms + light review | Read [command-terms.md](command-terms.md). Focus on "Explain" vs "Describe" vs "Outline" vs "Discuss" — these are where marks are lost. Final skim of your weakest topic page. |

## Priority if time is tight

If Chemistry time gets squeezed by other subjects, protect these three:

1. **Criterion B design** — 14–18 marks, entirely formulaic, fully learnable with a checklist
2. **Periodic table + atomic structure** — appears in every paper without exception; the quickest Criterion A marks to secure
3. **Criterion D essay structure** — one well-practised template (3–6 points, both perspectives, evidence from stimulus) covers any real-world topic
