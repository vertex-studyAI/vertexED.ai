### Question 9 — Algae-based Plastics / Sustainability (Criterion D) [13 marks]

**Stimulus:** Most plastics are made from chemicals that come from oil. Scientists are finding innovative solutions to produce plastics that reduce their impact on the environment. One solution is to produce plastic using algae.

Oil-based plastics are made from fossil fuels and have unlimited uses and have unsustainable production (they release greenhouse gases). Oil-based plastics are long lasting and certain products can be recycled to be used up to 400 years after disposal.

In the oceans, plastics form large islands of waste that pose threats to wildlife. Fish, turtles and birds can eat the plastic or get entangled in it.

As a sustainable alternative, plastics can be made using algae. Large volumes of water are needed to grow the algae. It can absorb dissolved carbon dioxide as it grows. When growing, algae take in dissolved carbon dioxide from water or sea. Sea water or fresh water can be used. Currently, algae-based plastics have limited uses such as wrappers and bags. These types of plastics need to be stored carefully as they are sensitive to the environment and may break down beyond any regulations that are harmful to the environment. However, algae-based plastics biodegrade very quickly, and due to high production rates are needed to meet demand.
