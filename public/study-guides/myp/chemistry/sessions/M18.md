- **Total marks:** 86
- **Number of questions:** 7 (each with multiple sub-parts)
- **Criteria covered:** A (Knowing and understanding), B (Inquiring and designing), C (Processing and evaluating), D (Reflecting on the impacts of science)
- **Paper focus:**
  - Questions 1–2: Criterion A — Knowing and understanding (key concept: relationships)
  - Questions 3–5: Criterion B/C — Investigation skills (key concept: change)
  - Questions 6–7: Criterion D — Applying science / Reflecting on impacts (key concept: systems; orientation in space and time with focus on natural and human landscapes and resources)
- **Context — Question 1:** Formic acid (simplest carboxylic acid)
- **Context — Question 2:** Hard water and dissolved ions (Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺)
- **Context — Question 3–5:** Calcium carbonate in animal bones and teeth; eggshell investigation
- **Context — Question 6–7:** Crude oil, fractional distillation, oil spills, crude oil transport

## Mark scheme conventions used

| Convention | Questions used | Meaning |
|------------|---------------|---------|
| **WTTE** | Q6b | "Words to that effect" — accept answers with the same meaning, not necessarily exact wording |
| **ORA** | Q5e, Q6b | "Or reverse argument" — accept the logically opposite/converse statement |
| **ECF** | Q3c, Q3d, Q4b | "Error carried forward" — award marks based on a wrong answer from a previous part if method is correct |
| **ORA** | Q5e | e.g., if limestone particles are smaller → rate is greater; ORA = if oyster shell particles are larger → rate is slower |
