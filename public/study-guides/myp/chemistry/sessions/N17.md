## Mark scheme conventions used

| Convention | Where used | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **WTTE** (Words To That Effect) | Q1g, Q2a, Q2b, Q3e, Q3h, Q4f, Q5b | Accept answers that convey the same meaning in different words |
| **ORA** (Or Reverse Argument) | Q2d | Accept the logically equivalent reverse argument |
| **ECF** (Error Carried Forward) | Q2b | Award mark based on incorrect answer from previous part if method is correct |
| **CON** | Not explicitly used in this paper | — |

### WTTE instances
- Q1g: "Same electron configuration → react the same way" — exact wording not required
- Q2a: Research question must relate to volatility of esters
- Q2b: "intermolecular forces are stronger" — accept "bonds are stronger"
- Q3e: Larger temperature rise = more reactive
- Q3h: One trial not reliable, need averages
- Q4f: Destructive method / cannot keep coin
- Q5b: Result is inconclusive / gold could be less than 24K

### ORA instances
- Q2d: Either cold temperature argument OR dry conditions argument — accept equivalent reverse reasoning

### ECF instances
- Q2b: ECF from Q2a — if the research question in (a) links chain length and volatility, accept this in (b)

### Notes on specific answers NOT accepted
- Q1a: Do **not** accept "group 14" phrasing issues (accept group 4 or group 14)
- Q1b: Do **not** accept "?" in place of a blank coefficient
- Q1c: Do **not** accept "toxic fumes" — must name the gas (SO₂ or CO₂) and its environmental effect
- Q1d: Do **not** accept high melting point for lead
- Q4c: Do **not** award the dependent variable mark for volume or time alone
