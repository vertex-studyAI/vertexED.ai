### Question 8 — Carbon Dioxide Reduction Systems (Criterion D) [15 marks]

**Stimulus:** Scientists have been developing different systems of carbon dioxide sinks, each with their own advantages and disadvantages. A developing island nation with large industrial areas wants to follow internationally-agreed CO₂ emission levels. The island's economy is based on agriculture and tourism; 24% of the country is desert and this is increasing, impacting the area available for agriculture and tourism.

Three systems presented:

**System 1 – Ferrock**
- A new material which absorbs CO₂ when it is manufactured
- Made from waste iron and glass bottles which would normally be dumped in landfills
- Can replace cement; stronger and more stable than traditional building materials; high strength in salt water environments
- Environmentally friendly: uses waste materials; carbon production releases large amounts of CO₂ through quarrying
- *Application of science:* absorbs carbon dioxide in its manufacture; much harder and more stable than traditional building materials; high strength and different applications
- *Employment:* Workers to collect waste glass bottles; workers to transport iron dust to Ferrock factory; workers to make Ferrock bricks
- *Cost:* Removal of 50 kg of CO₂ through Ferrock production costs USD/10

**System 2 – Carbon Dioxide Capture (CDC)**
- Device that absorbs CO₂ from the atmosphere by dissolving it in a carbonate solution
- Scientists exploring combination of trapped CO₂ with H₂ to produce hydrocarbons usable as fuel
- *Application of science:* developed on small scale; using renewable energy to efficiently capture CO₂; scientists researching how to combine CO₂ and H₂ to form fuels
- *Employment:* Scientists to develop carbon dioxide capture process; scientists to develop processes to transform captured carbon dioxide into fuel; workers to install and maintain equipment
- *Cost:* Removal of 50 kg of CO₂ costs USD 10

**System 3 – Conversion of Desert Areas**
- Deserts created by deforestation and climate change; disrupts water cycle and decreases soil quality
- A plant growth material retaining water and nutrients transforms desert into arable land
- Substances in plant cell walls added to the land allow it to retain water
- System trialled in China (desert decreased by 13,000 km² in past 15 years)
- *Application of science:* plant growth material retains water and nutrients; used successfully in similar situations for 30 years
- *Employment:* Workers to manufacture the plant material; workers to cultivate land; scientists to ensure process is sustainable
- *Cost:* Removal of 50 kg of CO₂ costs USD 20

| Convention | Location | Meaning |
|---|---|---|

| **ORA** (Or Reverse Argument) | Q7f (hydrogen vs petrol combustion) | Accept the argument expressed from the opposite direction |
| **CON** (Concluding appraisal) | Q8 markband rubric | Specific strand in extended-response marking requiring a reasoned conclusion |
