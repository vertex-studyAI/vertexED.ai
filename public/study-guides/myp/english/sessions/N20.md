#### Q1e
- Criterion: A and B
- Marks: 20 (A — 10 marks, B — 10 marks)
- Command term: Compare and contrast
- Question text: **Compare and contrast** the two excerpts' representations of **artificial human companions**. *(Directly readable from MS page 9)*

(Approximately 350 words recommended)

**Mark Scheme:**

Note: Examiners award a mark for **each** of Criterion A and B below.
- Candidates should not be penalised if they exceed or fall short of the recommended word count, unless the quality of the response is affected.
- The use of bullet points will not affect Criterion A.
- Candidates who do not compare AND contrast will be awarded a maximum of 5 marks for Criterion A.
- If students only write about one text, they cannot go beyond 2 marks for Criterion A.
- In descriptor strands i and ii, not all elements need to be addressed in the piece for the mark to be awarded. In descriptor strand iii, terminology can be implied.

**Criterion A — Analysing (10 marks)**

Responses should focus on comparing and contrasting the techniques the creators use to represent artificial human companions in both texts.

Candidates' ability to compare and contrast the two texts (strand iv) should be the deciding factor when placing students in a band for Criterion A.

| Marks | Mark descriptor (A: Analysing) | Indicative content |
|-------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| 0 | The student does not reach a standard described by any of the descriptors below | — |
| 1–2 | The student: i. makes no/irrelevant reference to the texts; ii. states obvious and superficial ideas about text one or text two | The candidate states obvious and superficial ideas about text one or text two. |
| 3–4 | The student: i. provides limited analysis; ii. evaluates some insight, imagination or sensibility and some similarities of perspectives and ideas; iii. makes a general reference in place of linguistic/literary devices; iv. selects some relevant details and examples to develop ideas | The candidate describes obvious connections between the two texts. |
| 5–6 | The student: i. provides adequate analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience; ii. justifies opinions and ideas with some examples and explanations, though this may not be consistent; iii. uses some appropriate terminology | The candidate addresses the prompt and image. |
| 7–8 | The student: i. provides adequate analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices; ii. justifies opinions and ideas with examples and explanations; iii. selects sufficient relevant details and examples to develop ideas | The candidate produces an engaging response that demonstrates an understanding of the global context. |
| 9–10 | The student: i. provides thorough analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices; ii. consistently justifies opinions with detailed responses or explanations; iii. selects sufficient relevant details and examples to develop ideas with precision | The candidate discusses visible connections and may draw conclusions within and beyond the two texts. |

**Criterion B — Organizing (10 marks)**

Candidates are required to write about both texts. If students only write about one text, they cannot go beyond 2 marks for Criterion B. If a candidate uses bullets, Criterion B will be awarded a maximum of 2 marks.
