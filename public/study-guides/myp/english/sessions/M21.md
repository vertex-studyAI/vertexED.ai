# M21 — May 2021 English Language and Literature

## Exam Structure
- Total marks: 80
- Criteria: A (Analyzing) [30 marks across Q1a–Q1e], B (Organizing) [10 marks in Q1e + 10 marks in Q3], C (Producing text) [10 marks in Q2 + 10 marks in Q3], D (Using language) [10 marks in Q2 + 10 marks in Q3]
- Number of questions: 3 tasks (Q1 has 5 sub-parts: 1a–1e)
- Text(s) provided:
  - Text 1: Poem — *Peaceful Transition* by Tony Hoagland (1953–2018). Written in 2016. The poem responds to changes in the Earth's climate. Topic: environmental degradation, human inaction, and dark irony about ecological collapse.
  - Text 2: Short film — *Greta Thunberg*, a Time magazine documentary film about the activism of their 2019 Person of the Year, Greta Thunberg, a Swedish youth advocate for fighting climate change.
  - Images 1 & 2 (for Task 2): Two photographs — Image 1 shows a group of young people (students) gathered together; Image 2 shows a young man sitting alone outdoors.

## Texts Provided in Full

### Text 1: Peaceful Transition — Tony Hoagland (1953–2018)

*In this poem written in 2016, Tony Hoagland responds to changes in the Earth's climate.*

The wind comes down from the northwest, cold in September,
and flips over the neighbor's trash receptacles.

The Halifax newspaper says that manatees are falling into the sea.
Storms are rising in the dark Pacific.

Pollution has infiltrated the food chain down to the jellyfish level.
It is now reaching a crisis! The End of the Ascent of Man
is being observed in a range of buffalo on Sweetness Street.

It is now hoping it will be a peaceful transition.

Today out of the north the cold wind comes down,
and I go out to see
the neighbor's trash cans have been toppled in the drive.
I am hoping the flocks of geese increase.
I am hoping the flocks of geese increase.
I am hoping the flocks of geese increase.

Let it be as airwaves that we didn't even notice
this title forgetting the name for sea level. Then the sky.
Let the fish rearrange their green governments
as the last salmon sold at the open market.
Let it not be one of millions. Human time is done.

It is now hoping it will be a peaceful transition.

Today out of the north the cold wind comes down,
and I go out to see

The wind has found a way to make its little nest
out in the various thorns.

*— Tony Hoagland (1953–2018)*

### Text 2: Greta Thunberg (Time magazine short film)

*In this short film, Time magazine documents the activism of their 2019 Person of the Year, Greta Thunberg, who is a Swedish youth advocate for fighting climate change.*

The film is a documentary-style short film featuring footage of Greta Thunberg's school strikes, protests, meetings with world leaders, and global climate marches. It includes voiceovers from Greta herself and news coverage from various media outlets. Key visual elements include:
- Greta in Hampton, VA (11/9/2019) at a climate strike
- Images of global climate protests and marches
- News coverage (BBC, CTM, Fox News) about youth climate activism
- Footage of Greta sailing across the Atlantic
- Crowds holding signs including "There is no Planet B", "March for Climate Justice", "In Case of Emergency Break the System"
- Greta being welcomed by politicians (including "Welcome, Greta Thunberg" from John Bercow in UK Parliament)
- Title card: "The Voice of the Planet" — 16-year-old climate activist taking Europe to New York
