# N23 — November 2023 English Language and Literature

## Exam Structure
- Total marks: 80
- Criteria: A (Analyzing) [30 marks — Q1a–Q1e], B (Organizing) [10 marks in Q1e + 10 marks in Q3], C (Producing text) [10 marks in Q2 + 10 marks in Q3], D (Using language) [10 marks in Q2 + 10 marks in Q3]
- Number of questions: 3 tasks (Q1 has 5 sub-parts: Q1a, Q1b, Q1c, Q1d, Q1e)
- Text(s) provided:
  - Text 1: Prose extract — excerpt from South African writer Nadine Gordimer's *The Conservationist*, which describes a group of children that has discovered a nest of bird's eggs. (Non-fiction literary prose)
  - Text 2: Short film — "Remember Childhood?" — filmmaker depicts a day titled "Me and Margo"; the film follows a family trip to pick cherries (film stills/screenshots with subtitles)
  - Images 1 & 2: Two photographs provided for Task 2 (Image 1 shows a young woman in a field/grass with golden light; Image 2 shows a dramatic blue sky with stars/cosmos)

### Text 1 — Excerpt from *The Conservationist* by Nadine Gordimer

**Pale freckled eggs**

Swaying over the ruts to the gate of the third pasture. Sunday morning, the owner of the farm suddenly sees: a clutch of pale freckled eggs set out before a half-circle of children. Some are squatting, like a vendor in a market. There is pride of ownership in that girl's gifted stance and the way the others are crowded round — the eggs so much larger to her — but the sense of ownership in that girl stirs envy to the toes. The eggs are arranged like marbles, the backbones of the children have fattened a nest in the long dead grass for both eggs and children.

The emblem on the car's bonnet, itself made in the shape of a prismatic flash, scores his vision with a vertical horseshoe sword of dazzle. This is the piece at which a child always appears, even if none has been in sight, racing across the field to open the gate for him. But none runs now, though there is a good child among the group: one very young boy, wearing a jersey made long so that the legs are not visible yet, leaves the engine running and gets out. One very young boy, wearing a jersey made long so that the legs are not visible yet, leaves the engine running and gets out. The eggs are arranged like marbles, together. Some are squatting, like a vendor in a market. There is pride of ownership in that girl's gifted stance — so much larger to her — but the sense of ownership in that girl stirs envy to the toes (wearing a woman's dress, but it may be a boy) puts out his hands over the eggs and lightly shuffles them a little closer together. He calls out to them all but they don't talk back into his palms. The eggs are a creamy buff, thick-shelled, their glaze smoothed and slightly opened at the round Black hands are translucent-looking apricot-pink. There is no sound from either end of the long dark grass for both eggs and children.

Eleven pale freckled eggs. A whole clutch of guinea fowl eggs.

He asks a question of the cross-legged one and there are giggles. He points down at them and does not touch them, and was again. The children don't understand the language; the palms come up and the children are dumb. The most pigeon-toed child puts its head on one side, smiling as if under the weight of praise, and offers the eggs forward slightly.

### Text 2 — Short film: "Remember Childhood?" (featuring "Me and Margo")

A short film by a filmmaker depicting a day titled "Wednesday" (with a flashback structure). The film follows a family — the filmmaker, a sister named Daria, and their family — on a trip to Cirese (a cherry orchard/farm). The film uses home-video style footage, flashback title cards, and upbeat music to capture the joy of cherry-picking, family connection, and childhood. The film ends with the caption "just for a little" and includes visual moments of the children picking cherries, the father carrying a child on his shoulders, and the family interacting warmly in the orchard.
