### Task 1 — Analysing (30 marks, Criteria A and B)

---

#### Q1a
- Criterion: A (Analysing)
- Marks: 2
- Command term: Comment (reconstructed from MS)
- Question text: **Comment** on how the post perhaps grows in the first essay. *(Reconstructed from MS — exact wording uncertain; MS heading reads: "Comment on how the post perhaps grows in the first essay")*

**Mark Scheme:**

Award 1 mark per relevant point, up to 2 marks.

Band 0:
- The student makes no/irrelevant reference to the draft/layout of text 1 and its effect on the audience.

Band 1:
- The student:
  - i & ii. provides limited analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience
  - iii. does not justify opinions and ideas with examples or explanations

Indicative content:
- A level 1 response identifies a post perhaps grows in the first essay but does not explain how or why this is achieved. Or it may offer an example without an explanation.
- For example: "The tone shifts from negative to positive." / "The music becomes uplifting."

Band 2:
- The student:
  - i & ii. provides adequate analysis of the content, technique, or of the effects of the creator's choices on an audience
  - iii. justifies opinions and ideas with some examples or explanations

Indicative content:
- A level 2 response comments on a shift in tone including how or why this is achieved and cites specific examples/details from the text for support.
- For example: "The tone shifts from negative to positive with the girl's statement that it is up to us how this story ends/the change to uplifting music/brighter lighting/ action/slow-motion scenes." / "At the end of the film the tone shifts from sad to happy to persuade the audience to change their lifestyles."

#### Q1b
- Criterion: A (Analysing)
- Marks: 3
- Command term: Analyse (reconstructed from MS)
- Question text: **Analyse** how the filmmaker portrays the purpose in using glass houses for the audience. *(Reconstructed from MS — exact wording uncertain)*

**Mark Scheme:**

Award marks up to 3 marks.

Band 0:
- The student makes no/irrelevant reference to the filmmaker's purpose in using glass houses.

Band 1:
- The student:
  - i & ii. provides limited analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices
  - iii. does not justify opinions and ideas with examples or explanations

Indicative content:
- A level 1 response identifies a basic purpose of the glass houses without citing the film/text. Or it may simply identify a technique used without effect.
- For example: "People use glass houses for gardening."

Band 2:
- The student:
  - i & ii. provides adequate analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience
  - iii. justifies opinions and ideas with some examples or explanations

Indicative content:
- A level 2 response associates the purpose of the glass houses and justifies the connection with examples/details from the film.
- For example: "The glass houses help the filmmaker show the problem of air pollution/urban living as a contrast to the ideal of living with nature (e.g. images of digging, bee-keeping, farming)."

Band 3:
- The student:
  - i & ii. effectively analyses the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience
  - iii. justifies opinions and ideas with several examples and explanations

Indicative content:
- A level 3 response provides considerable insight/imagination or sensitivity and substantial exploration of perspectives and ideas.
- The candidate addresses the prompt and demonstrates an understanding of its larger context and implications.
- The candidate makes thoughtful, nuanced analysis of the creator's techniques and choices.
- For example, the candidate selects and analyses relevant examples and justifies them with convincing and thorough explanations rather than indicative ones.
- The candidate explores the filmmaker's use of film techniques (e.g. music/colour/sound/action/slow motion/glass, etc.) and their effect on the audience in a nuanced, convincing way.

#### Q1e
- Criterion: A (Analysing) and B (Organizing)
- Marks: 20 (A — 10 marks, B — 10 marks)
- Command term: Compare and contrast
- Question text: **Compare and contrast** how the creators persuade us to connect with the outdoors. *(Taken directly from MS page 9)*
- Note: Approximately 350 words recommended. Examiners award a mark for each of Criterion A and B below.

**Mark Scheme:**

**Criterion A — Analysing (10 marks)**

Responses should focus on comparing and contrasting the techniques the creators use to persuade us to connect with the outdoors in both texts.

Candidates' ability to compare and contrast the two texts (strand iv.) should be the deciding factor when placing students in a band for Criterion A.

Candidates are required to write about both texts. If students only write about one text, they cannot go beyond (2 marks) for Criterion A.

Candidates who do not compare and contrast will be awarded a maximum of (5 marks) for Criterion A.

In descriptor strands i and ii, not all elements need to be addressed in the piece for the mark to be awarded. In descriptor strand iii, terminology can be implied.

|-------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| 0 | The student does not claim a standard described by any of the descriptors below. | |

| 3–4 | The student: i & ii. provides adequate analysis of the content, technique, or of the effects of the creator's choices on an audience; iii. justifies opinions and ideas with some examples or explanations; iv. identifies obvious differences between the two texts. | The candidate identifies obvious connections between the two texts. |
| 5–6 | The student: i & ii. provides competent analysis of the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience; iii. effectively analyses the content, technique, or the effects of the creator's choices on an audience; iv. justifies opinions and ideas with some examples and explanations; identifies clear differences and similarities between texts with some analysis. | The candidate describes obvious connections between the two texts. |
