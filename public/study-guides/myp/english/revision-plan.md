# 2-Week English LAL Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of English per day**, with other subjects filling the rest. Adjust the daily slot to fit your schedule.

---

## Week 1 — Skills Consolidation

| Day | Focus | What to do | Time |
|-----|-------|------------|------|
| **Day 1** | Q1e compare-and-contrast essay structure | Read [command-terms.md](command-terms.md) section on "Compare and contrast". Write one full Q1e essay on M22 texts (*A Wild Attack of Locusts* / *A Lesson on Resilience*) — 35 minutes timed. Self-mark: did you cover both texts? Did you compare AND contrast? | 50 min |
| **Day 2** | Task 3 non-literary writing — speech and proposal | Read the Task 3 patterns in [overview.md](overview.md) §Task 3. Learn conventions for: speech (address, rhetorical question, call to action), proposal (purpose, rationale, request), letter (salutation, formal register, sign-off). Write a Task 3 speech on any topic from M21, N16, or N22. | 55 min |
| **Day 3** | Task 2 literary writing — using the image | Read the Task 2 patterns in [overview.md](overview.md) §Task 2. Practise writing a ~350-word creative piece using the M24 image stimulus (decision / monologue). Focus: anchor to the image; use at least two deliberate literary devices; vary sentence length. | 50 min |
| **Day 4** | Film analysis — Q1c and Q1d technique vocabulary | Study the film technique vocabulary: camera angle (close-up, wide, bird's-eye), lighting, music/sound, voice-over, slow motion, colour, text overlay. Using the M21 *Greta Thunberg* documentary or M24 *The Walk* film, write one Q1c and one Q1d response. Check: technique named + specific example + effect on viewer. | 45 min |
| **Day 5** | Analyse and Explain — written text Q1a/Q1b | Read the "Analyse" and "Explain" entries in [command-terms.md](command-terms.md). Practise Q1a and Q1b responses from M25 (*Blueprints?* poem) and N24 newspaper article. Apply the model: technique → embedded quote → effect on reader. | 45 min |
| **Day 6** | Q1e Criterion B — organisation and structure | Re-read your Day 1 Q1e essay. Focus only on Criterion B. Rewrite the structure: write in thematic paragraphs, not text-by-text. Each paragraph must weave both texts together. Check you are not using bullet points. | 50 min |
| **Day 7** | Rest / light review | Skim [overview.md](overview.md) topic frequency table. Note the two dominant themes: nature/environment (6 sessions) and identity/self (6 sessions). Jot down three techniques writers and filmmakers use for each theme. No extended writing today. | 20 min |

## Priority if time is tight

If English time gets squeezed by other subjects, protect these:

1. **Q1e compare-and-contrast structure** — 20 marks every paper; entirely learnable if you practise one essay and self-mark against the hard rules (both texts, compare AND contrast, no bullet points)
2. **Task 3 non-literary forms** — 30 marks; speeches and proposals appear in 6 of 14 sessions; know the conventions of both cold
3. **Task 2 — use the image** — ignoring the image scores 0 for Criterion C; one drill session eliminates this risk entirely
4. **Film technique vocabulary** — Q1c/Q1d appear in every paper; ten technique names (close-up, voice-over, slow motion, lighting, colour, music, wide shot, title card, match cut, direct address) cover the vast majority of mark scheme accepted answers
5. **Analyse formula** — technique + embedded quote + effect on reader; this pattern covers the top band for Analyse, Explain, Evaluate, and Comment responses
6. **Task 3 register discipline** — no bullet points, no informal address; Criterion B is capped at 2/10 for bullet points and Criterion D suffers for wrong register; both are avoidable errors

## Final 48 Hours

### 47–24 hours before the exam

- Sit one final past paper under strict timed conditions: M25 (80 marks) or M24 (70 marks). Recommended: M25, as it is the most recent session with a full mark scheme.
- Allocate: Q1a–1d (15 min) → Q1e (35 min) → Task 2 (35 min) → Task 3 (35 min). Stop at time.
- Mark every component using the criteria descriptors in [overview.md](overview.md). Be honest: assign the markband that best fits — do not round up.
- Write a list of no more than three specific things to fix.

### 24–12 hours before the exam

- Fix only those three things. Do not attempt to revise new content.
- Re-read the four Q1e hard rules. Write them on a card: (1) cover both texts; (2) compare AND contrast; (3) no bullet points; (4) thematic paragraphs, not text-by-text.
- Re-read the Task 3 form conventions for the two forms you feel least confident about.
- Re-read the Task 2 rule: use the image or score 0 for Criterion C.

### Final 12 hours

- No new revision. Sleep.
- On the morning: skim the command-terms hierarchy (Identify → List → Outline → Describe → Comment → Explain → Analyse → Evaluate) so the response depth expected by each command term is fresh.
