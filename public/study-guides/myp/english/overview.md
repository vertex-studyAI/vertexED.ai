# English LAL — Overview

## Exam Format

The MYP English Language and Literature on-screen examination consists of **3 tasks** completed in a single sitting.

- **Task 1 (Analysing):** A set of analytical questions on two provided texts (one written, one film/visual). Sub-questions 1a–1d test individual analytical skills; Q1e is an extended compare-and-contrast essay assessed on two criteria.
- **Task 2 (Producing Literary Text):** One extended creative writing task using an image stimulus.
- **Task 3 (Producing Non-literary Text):** One extended non-literary writing task in a specified form and global context.

**Total marks:** 70 marks (most sessions from 2022 onward) or 80 marks (M21, M25, N19–N23, N24). Earlier sessions (N16) used a 105-mark format with a different structure.

> Note: The 80-mark papers (M21, M25, N19–N24) and the 70-mark papers (M22–M24) have the same structure but differ in how the Q1a–1d sub-marks are distributed. M21 and M25 distribute 30 marks across Task 1; 70-mark papers also do — the difference lies in whether the paper header counts marks differently. Always check the session file.

**Timing (where stated):**
- Q1a–1d (short analytical questions): 15 minutes recommended
- Q1e (compare-and-contrast essay): 35 minutes recommended
- Task 2: 35 minutes recommended
- Task 3: 35 minutes recommended

|-----------|--------------|--------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|

| B — Organising | Use of organisational structures; coherence and logic; paragraphing; structural devices | — | 10 | — | 10 | 20 |
| C — Producing text | Insight, imagination, sensitivity; stylistic and literary choices; development of ideas | — | — | 10 | 10 | 20 |
| D — Using language | Range of vocabulary and sentence structures; register and style; accuracy of grammar, syntax and punctuation | — | — | 10 | 10 | 20 |

> Note: In 70-mark sessions (M22, M23, M24, N22) the Task 1 Q1a–1d total is still 10 marks but the overall total is stated as 70 — this appears to be a paper-header discrepancy across sessions. ## Task 2 and 3 Patterns

### Task 2 — Producing Literary Text

Task 2 always uses image stimuli (two photographs) and asks for a creative piece of approximately 200–350 words. Text types and scenarios vary each session but cluster into recognisable patterns:

**Recurring Task 2 text types / scenarios:**
- Scene in a story inspired by one image (most common format): M21 (scene with doubt), M22 (sci-fi scene with discovery), M23 (character travels through time), M24 (monologue about a decision), M25 (description of an event), N22 (shift in perspective), N23 (original piece expressing a strong emotion), N24 (scene about connecting with surroundings)
- Screenplay / script format: M18 (mystery movie screenplay)
- Dialogue: N16 (dialogue between two characters), N19 (conversation as a character reflecting on the past)
- Narrative with a specific focus: N20 (beginning of an adventure story), N21 (narrative about a moment of victory)

**Global contexts explicitly named in Task 2 or Task 3:**
| Session | Task 2 global context (implied or stated) | Task 3 global context (stated) |
|---------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| M21 | Identities and relationships | Identities and relationships |
| M22 | (science fiction / discovery) | Personal and cultural expression |
| M23 | (mystery / time travel) | Globalization and sustainability |
| M24 | (orientation in space and time implied) | Orientation in space and time |
| M25 | (scientific / technological innovation implied) | Scientific and technological innovation |
| N16 | (no global context stated) | Globalization and sustainability |
| N19 | (outdoors / nature) | Scientific and technological innovation |
| N20 | (adventure / narrative) | (environment / plastic — Fairness and development implied) |
| N21 | (victory / moments) | Orientation in space and time |
| N22 | (shift in perspective / nature) | Scientific and technical innovation |
| N23 | (childhood / emotion) | Identities and relationships |
| N24 | (connecting with surroundings) | Fairness and development |

**Identities and relationships** is the most frequently named global context (at least 3 explicit appearances; implied in several more).

## Mark Distribution

### Task 1 — Q1a–1d sub-part pattern

The 10 marks for short analytical questions are distributed across 4 sub-questions (1a–1d). The typical pattern from 2021 onward:

| Sub-question | Typical marks | Text it addresses |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| Q1a | 2–3 marks | Text 1 (written) |
| Q1b | 2–4 marks | Text 1 (written) |
| Q1c | 2–3 marks | Text 2 (film/visual) |
| Q1d | 2–3 marks | Text 2 (film/visual) |
| **Q1a–1d total** | **10 marks** | Both texts |

Observed exact distributions (from sessions with full data):
- M21: Q1a=4, Q1b=2, Q1c=2, Q1d=2 → total 10
- M22: Q1a=2, Q1b=3, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 10
- M23: Q1a=4, Q1b=3, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 12 (note: possibly distributed as Q1a=4, Q1b=3, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 = 12 in the paper; overall Task 1 is still 30 marks)
- M24: Q1a=2, Q1b=3, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 10
- M25: Q1a=2, Q1b=3, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 10
- N22: Q1a=2, Q1b=4, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 11 (with Q1e carrying 20 = 31 total; likely rounding)
- N23: Q1a=3, Q1b=2, Q1c=2, Q1d=3 → total 10
- N24: Q1a=2, Q1b=3, Q1c=3, Q1d=2 → total 10

### Q1e marks

Always 20 marks, split equally: **Criterion A = 10 marks**, **Criterion B = 10 marks**.

### Q2 marks

Always 20 marks: **Criterion C = 10 marks**, **Criterion D = 10 marks**.

### Q3 marks

Always 30 marks: **Criterion B = 10 marks**, **Criterion C = 10 marks**, **Criterion D = 10 marks**.

### Mark penalties to know

- Writing about only one text in Q1e: maximum **2 marks** for Crit A and **2 marks** for Crit B.
- Not comparing AND contrasting in Q1e: maximum **5 marks** for Crit A.
- Using bullet points in any extended writing: maximum **2 marks** for Criterion B.
- Not using the image/prompt in Task 2: **0 marks** for Criterion C.

## High-Yield Revision Areas

1. **Compare and contrast essay (Q1e — 20 marks, always).**
   This is the single highest-mark question on the paper. It tests Criterion A (analysis) and Criterion B (organisation) equally. Practice requires: identifying and labelling techniques in both texts, structuring comparative paragraphs (not text-by-text), using connectives of similarity and difference, and building an argument across the response.

2. **Task 3 non-literary writing (30 marks).**
   The highest-mark task overall. Forms rotate — speeches and proposals appear most frequently (6 of 14 sessions). Know the conventions of: speech, letter, proposal, blog post, interview, advertisement script, opinion piece, product review, and podcast script. The MS consistently rewards: form conventions (register, address, structure), engagement with the global context, and fluent purposeful language.

3. **Task 2 literary writing (20 marks).**
   Almost always image-based. The stimulus image must be used or Criterion C scores 0. The scene / narrative / creative piece must demonstrate: insight and imagination, deliberate stylistic choices (imagery, characterisation, mood, point of view), and language accuracy. ~350 words is the recurring advisory length.

4. **Film analysis (Q1c and Q1d — 5 marks across two questions).**
   A film or visual text appears in every single session. Questions test: identifying film techniques (camera angles, music, voice-over, close-up, slow motion, lighting, colour), explaining their effect on the audience, and analysing a specific clip or moment. Being precise about technique + effect is what separates bands.

5. **Written text analysis (Q1a and Q1b — 5 marks across two questions).**
   Covers a wide range of text types (prose, poetry, play, non-fiction article, personal essay). The MS consistently rewards: naming a technique, quoting from the text, and explaining the effect on the reader. Unsupported identification ("the writer uses imagery") never earns full marks.

6. **Key recurring themes for Task 1 source texts.**
   Nature/environment (6 sessions) and identity/self (6 sessions) are the two dominant themes. Familiarity with how writers and filmmakers approach: human relationships with the natural world, what it means to belong or find oneself, resilience in adversity, and the power of sensory/aesthetic experience is directly transferable.

7. **Organisation skills for extended writing (Criterion B — appears in Q1e and Q3).**
   Criterion B is worth 10 marks in Q1e and 10 marks in Q3 — 20 marks total on the paper. It rewards: clear paragraph structure, logical sequencing of ideas, cohesive devices, appropriate register, and — in Q1e — weaving comparison throughout rather than treating texts separately. Bullet points cap the mark at 2.
