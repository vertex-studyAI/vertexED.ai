# MYP Assessment Criteria

## Key concepts

The MYP English Language and Literature on-screen exam is marked against four criteria, each worth a maximum of 20 marks out of 80 total. Every task on the paper maps to one or more criteria — no criterion is assessed on every task.

| Criterion | Full name | Questions it appears in | Typical marks per paper | What it tests |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|------------------------|---------------|
| A | Analysing | Q1a, Q1b, Q1c, Q1d (10 marks); Q1e (10 marks) | 20 | Reading and analysis: content, context, language, structure, technique, style; effects on audience; use of terminology; comparison and contrast |
| B | Organising | Q1e (10 marks); Q3 (10 marks) | 20 | Organisation of ideas: logical structure, coherence, paragraphing, cohesive devices, register appropriate to form |
| C | Producing text | Q2 (10 marks); Q3 (10 marks) | 20 | Creative/purposeful writing: insight, imagination, stylistic and literary choices, development of ideas, awareness of audience impact |
| D | Using language | Q2 (10 marks); Q3 (10 marks) | 20 | Language accuracy and range: vocabulary, sentence structures, register and style, grammar, syntax, punctuation |

## Criterion A — Analysing

### What it tests

Criterion A assesses how well a candidate analyses written and audiovisual texts. "Use of language" is interpreted broadly by mark schemes to include **content, context, language, structure, technique and style** — but candidates are not expected to address all of these in a single response. The focus is on: naming techniques, showing where they appear (with quotes or references), explaining their **effect on the reader or viewer**, and — in Q1e — comparing and contrasting across two texts.

### Band descriptors (from past mark schemes)

The short analytical questions (Q1a–1d, 2–4 marks each) and the compare-and-contrast essay (Q1e, 10 marks) share the same underlying strand language.

**For short questions (Q1a–1d) — markband language (M25, N24, M23, M22 MSs):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Makes no reference to the question focus. |
| 1 (or 1–2 for a 3-mark question) | Provides **limited** analysis of content, technique, **or** the effects of the creator's choices; **does not** justify opinions and ideas with examples or explanations. |
| 2 (or 2–3) | Provides **adequate** analysis of content, technique, **or** effects; justifies opinions and ideas with **some** examples or explanations, though this may not be consistent. |
| 3 (top band, for 3-mark questions) | **Effectively** analyses content or technique and the effects of the creator's choices; **consistently** justifies opinions and ideas with examples **and** explanations. |

**For Q1e (compare-and-contrast essay) — Criterion A, 10 marks (M25, N24, M22, M23 MSs):**

| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Does not reach a standard described by any descriptor. |
| 1–2 | **Limited** analysis of content, context, language, structure, technique **or** style; **rarely** justifies with examples; uses **little or no** terminology; evaluates **few** similarities **or** differences, making **minimal** connections. |
| 3–5 | **Adequate** analysis; justifies with **some** examples though not consistently; uses **some** terminology; evaluates **some** similarities and differences, making **adequate** connections. |
| 6–8 | **Effectively** analyses content, context, language, structure, technique **or** style; **sufficiently** justifies with examples and explanations; uses **accurate** terminology; makes **substantial** connections. |
| 9–10 | **Perceptive** analysis; gives **detailed justification** with a range of examples and **thorough** explanations; uses **accurate** terminology; **perceptively compares and contrasts**, making **extensive** connections. |

### What earns top marks (real MS language)

The following phrases appear at the top band of actual mark schemes across multiple sessions:

- "The candidate discusses subtle connections and may draw conclusions within and beyond the two texts." (M25, N24 Q1e MSs)
- "Candidates will tend to analyse with a sophisticated comparison and contrast and a perceptive exploration of features across the texts." (M25 Q1e MS)
- "Technique + embedded quote + effect + explanation, sustained." (pattern from M25 Q1b MS: "The poem uses alliteration in 'scary speculation' to emphasise how stressed out the speaker is … the repetition of 's' calls attention to her fear when she thinks about her future.")
- "Candidates' ability to compare and contrast the two texts (strand iv.) should be the **deciding factor** when placing students in a band for Criterion A." (appears verbatim in M25, N24, M23, M22 MSs)

### What loses marks

- **Identifying without explaining:** "The poem uses imagery to show uncertainty." → level 1 only. The effect on the reader must be stated.
- **Paraphrasing the text instead of analysing it:** Retelling what happens scores no marks for analysis.
- **Single-text response in Q1e:** If only one text is discussed, the **maximum is 2 marks** for Criterion A — regardless of how good the analysis is.
- **Comparing without contrasting (or contrasting without comparing) in Q1e:** Maximum **5 marks** for Criterion A.
- **Terminology not used:** Upper bands (6–10) explicitly require "accurate terminology." At 3–5, terminology can be "some" or implied; at 1–2, it is absent.

## Criterion B — Organising

### What it tests

Criterion B assesses how well a candidate organises their writing. It appears in two places: **Q1e** (the compare-and-contrast essay, 10 marks) and **Q3** (the non-literary writing task, 10 marks). In both contexts it rewards: clear structural choices that serve the purpose of the piece, logical sequencing of ideas, coherence and cohesion, purposeful paragraphing, and — at the top band — structural devices that actively enhance meaning (e.g., crafting sentence/paragraph length, circular structures, deliberate return to an opening idea).

### Band descriptors (from actual mark schemes — identical wording across M25, N24, M23, M22, N21)

| Marks | Descriptor (Q1e and Q3 — same wording) |
|-------|----------------------------------------|
| 0 | Does not reach a standard described by any descriptor. |
| 1–2 | Makes **minimal** use of organisational structures; eg writing about each text separately (Q1e), or a basic chronological structure with elements absent (Q3); organises opinions and ideas with a **minimal degree of coherence and logic**; points may be unconnected. |
| 3–5 | Makes **adequate** use of organisational structures that serve the context and intention; eg an attempt to compare and contrast the texts (Q1e), or a straightforward structure adhering to the given form (Q3); organises opinions and ideas with **a degree of coherence and logic**; begins to make connections. |
| 6–8 | Makes **effective** use of organisational structures; eg compares and contrasts across the two texts (Q1e), or a secure structure with structural devices beginning to add to the overall effect (Q3); organises opinions and ideas in a **coherent and logical** manner with **ideas building on each other**. |
| 9–10 | Makes **sophisticated** use of organisational structures that serve the context and intention **effectively**; eg structural devices such as crafting the length of sentences and/or paragraphs enhance coherence and cohesion (Q3), or comparison and contrast is woven throughout in a sustained way (Q1e); **effectively** organises opinions and ideas in a **sustained, coherent and logical** manner with ideas building on each other in a **sophisticated** way. |

### Hard penalties specific to Criterion B

- **Bullet points in any extended writing (Q1e or Q3):** Criterion B is capped at **2 marks** — this penalty appears verbatim in every session's mark scheme from M22 onward.
- **Text-by-text structure in Q1e** (writing about Text 1 fully, then Text 2 fully, as two separate essays): This is explicitly given as an example of a **1–2 band** response. The mark scheme states: "eg, writing about each text separately." Moving beyond 2 marks requires an attempt to integrate and compare.
- **No clear line of argument / unconnected points:** The 1–2 band descriptor explicitly mentions "points may be unconnected" as a failure mode. Coherence is the threshold skill.

## Criterion C — Producing Text

### What it tests

Criterion C assesses creative and purposeful writing — the **what** of the piece (ideas, content, perspective) rather than the **how** (language accuracy). It appears in **Q2** (literary writing, 10 marks) and **Q3** (non-literary writing, 10 marks). The mark scheme rewards: insight, imagination, and sensitivity; deliberate stylistic and literary choices that show awareness of impact on an audience; and the selection of relevant details and examples to develop ideas. At the top band the writing is described as "highly engaging" and "inspired by" or "reflecting on" the prompt, with "perceptive stylistic choices" and "extensive relevant details developed with precision."

### Band descriptors (from actual mark schemes — consistent wording across all sessions)

| Marks | Descriptor (Q2 and Q3) | Examiner's marking note |
|-------|------------------------|------------------------|
| 0 | Does not reach a standard described by any descriptor. For Q2: response uses neither the image nor the theme. For Q3: response is not linked to the global context or theme. | |
| 1–2 | Demonstrates a **limited** degree of insight, imagination or sensitivity; makes **minimal** stylistic choices; demonstrates **limited** awareness of impact on an audience; selects **few** relevant details. | Candidate attempts a basic link to the image or prompt (Q2); makes little or no reference to the global context (Q3). |
| 3–5 | Demonstrates **some** insight, imagination or sensitivity; makes **some** stylistic choices; demonstrates **adequate** awareness of impact on an audience; selects **some** relevant details. | Candidate addresses the prompt and image (Q2); demonstrates some understanding of the global context (Q3). |
| 6–8 | Demonstrates **considerable** insight, imagination or sensitivity; makes **thoughtful** stylistic choices; demonstrates **good** awareness of impact on an audience; selects **sufficient** relevant details. | Candidate produces an **engaging** response that elaborates on the prompt and image (Q2); demonstrates understanding of the global context (Q3). |
| 9–10 | Demonstrates a **high degree** of insight, imagination or sensitivity; makes **perceptive** stylistic choices; demonstrates **sophisticated** awareness of impact on an audience; selects **extensive** relevant details with **precision**. | Candidate produces a **highly engaging** response that is inspired by the prompt and image (Q2); reflects on the prompt and demonstrates excellent understanding of the global context (Q3). |

### What earns top marks

- **Q2 (literary):** The image must be used as a genuine anchor. Top-band responses go beyond the literal content of the image to build atmosphere, character, or perspective. The mark scheme phrase "inspired by the prompt" implies the image sparks something original rather than being merely described.
- **Q3 (non-literary):** The global context must be explicitly integrated, not just mentioned. "Demonstrates excellent understanding of the global context" (9–10 band) means the piece engages with the big idea (fairness, innovation, orientation in time) throughout, not just in the opening line.
- Deliberate stylistic choices are rewarded at every band. At 6–8 they must be "thoughtful"; at 9–10, "perceptive." A perceptive choice is one that serves the piece's specific purpose — not generic imagery or a default metaphor.
- The mark scheme for every session states: "Reward all valid responses." There is no single correct interpretation of the image or prompt.

## Criterion D — Using Language

### What it tests

Criterion D assesses the accuracy and quality of the candidate's language — the **how** of the writing. It appears in **Q2** (10 marks) and **Q3** (10 marks). Three strands are assessed: (i) range and appropriateness of vocabulary and sentence structures; (ii) register and style appropriate to the context and intention; (iii) accuracy of grammar, syntax, and punctuation. The mark scheme notes for every session: "Examiners are reminded to consider **best fit/balance** in rewarding marks for all strands in Criterion D" — a strong register with some grammar errors does not automatically fall to a low band.

### Band descriptors (identical wording across all sessions: M25, N24, M23, M22, N21)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Does not reach a standard described by any descriptor. |
| 1–2 | Uses a **limited** range of appropriate vocabulary; writes in an **inappropriate** register and style that **does not** serve the context and intention; uses grammar, syntax and punctuation with a **minimal degree of** accuracy; errors **often hinder** communication. |
| 3–5 | Uses an **adequate** range of appropriate vocabulary and sentence structures; **sometimes** writes in a register and style that serves the context and intention; uses grammar, syntax and punctuation with **a degree of** accuracy; errors **sometimes hinder** communication. |
| 6–8 | Uses a **varied** range of appropriate vocabulary and sentence structures; writes in a register and style that **serves** the context and intention; uses grammar, syntax and punctuation with a **considerable degree of** accuracy; errors **do not hinder** effective communication. |
| 9–10 | Uses an **effective** range of appropriate vocabulary and sentence structures; writes in a **consistently purposeful** register and style that serves the context and intention; uses grammar, syntax and punctuation with a **high degree of** accuracy; errors are **minor** and communication is **effective**. |

### What earns top marks

- **Register is the threshold skill.** A response can have some grammatical errors and still reach 9–10 if the register is "consistently purposeful" and the errors are minor. The mark scheme's "best fit/balance" instruction protects strong writers with occasional slips.
- **Vocabulary must be varied and chosen for effect.** "Adequate" range (3–5) becomes "varied" (6–8) and "effective" (9–10). The key shift from 5 to 6 is variety; from 8 to 9 is precision — words chosen specifically rather than generically.
- **Sentence structures should vary.** All three top-band descriptors include both vocabulary AND sentence structures. A piece with strong vocabulary but only simple sentences will not reach 9–10.
- **Spelling errors do not affect marks.** The general marking guidelines across all sessions state: "spelling errors should not impact the marks awarded in any part of the examination."

## How criteria combine across the three tasks

| Task | Question | Criterion | Marks | What is being assessed |
|------|---------|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Task 1 | Q1a | A | 2–4 | Short analytical question on Text 1 (written) |
| Task 1 | Q1b | A | 2–4 | Short analytical question on Text 1 (written) |
| Task 1 | Q1c | A | 2–3 | Short analytical question on Text 2 (film/visual) |
| Task 1 | Q1d | A | 2–3 | Short analytical question on Text 2 (film/visual) |
| Task 1 | Q1e | A | 10 | Compare-and-contrast essay: analysis |
| Task 1 | Q1e | B | 10 | Compare-and-contrast essay: organisation |
| Task 2 | Q2 | C | 10 | Literary creative writing: content and craft |
| Task 2 | Q2 | D | 10 | Literary creative writing: language accuracy |
| Task 3 | Q3 | B | 10 | Non-literary writing: organisation |
| Task 3 | Q3 | C | 10 | Non-literary writing: content and craft |
| Task 3 | Q3 | D | 10 | Non-literary writing: language accuracy |
| **Total** | | | **80** | |

**Key observations from this table:**

- Criterion A is tested only in Task 1. If Task 1 goes badly, there is no way to recover those 20 marks elsewhere.
- Criterion B is tested in Q1e and Q3 — 20 marks total, split across analysis and production. Bullet points in either lose most of those marks.
- Criteria C and D are never tested in Task 1. Strong writers should not over-invest time in Tasks 2 and 3 at the expense of Q1e, which is worth 20 marks alone.
- Q3 (Task 3) is the single highest-value question — 30 marks (B + C + D) — and is also the task where the most common structural failure (bullet points) has the most damaging effect.

## Revision priorities

1. **Q1e is the highest single mark available for Criterion A** (10 marks). Practise writing comparative paragraphs that weave both texts together — not text by text. The deciding factor is strand iv: quality of comparison and contrast.

2. **Know the three non-negotiable rules for Q1e:** one text only = max 2 for A and B; no contrast (or no comparison) = max 5 for A; bullet points = max 2 for B. Avoiding all three costs nothing.

3. **For Criterion A short questions, always name → quote → effect.** Level 1 is always "identifies without developing." Level 2 needs either an example OR an explanation. Level 3/top needs example AND explanation. This formula works for every command term (Comment, Analyse, Explain, Evaluate, Examine).

4. **For Criterion B in Q3, commit to the form.** The mark scheme examples at 3–5 say "adheres to the given form." At 6–8, structural devices "begin to add to the overall effect." Know the conventions of speech, letter, blog post, opinion piece, and proposal — these five forms cover the vast majority of Task 3 questions across all sessions.

5. **For Criterion C, use the image or prompt specifically.** Q2 scores 0 for Criterion C if neither the image nor the theme is used. At 6–8, the response "elaborates" on the prompt; at 9–10, it is "inspired by" it. Vague or generic creative pieces that ignore the specific image stay at 3–5.

6. **For Criterion D, prioritise register over error-free writing.** The mark scheme's "best fit/balance" instruction means a fluent, purposeful piece with occasional grammatical errors can still reach 9–10. Register — formal for letters and proposals, persuasive for speeches, literary for fiction — is the more controllable and more heavily weighted variable.

7. **Spelling errors never cost marks.** The general marking guidelines across every session from M22 to M25 state this explicitly. Do not waste exam time over-correcting spelling.

8. **Task 3 (30 marks) is the most valuable task on the paper** — but it is also where bullet points are most damaging (caps B at 2/10, losing up to 8 marks). Write in full paragraphs in every extended response without exception.
