# 2-Week Geography Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of Geography per day** alongside other subjects.

---

## Week 1 — Structure + High-Frequency Topics

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 1** | Research Methods — Q3 Evaluate | Read [research-methods.md](topics/research-methods.md). Practise one Q3 response: 3 strengths + 3 limitations + appraisal. This appears in **every single paper** — master the formula. |
| **Day 2** | Research Methods — Q4 Design | Practise writing a full Q4: Formulate a RQ (named place + focus), Justify it, outline methods. Check: RQ must be specific or you lose a mark. |
| **Day 3** | Natural Hazards | Read [natural-hazards.md](topics/natural-hazards.md). Do Q1–Q2 style questions from M21, M22, M23, M24. Appears in 7/11 sessions — the most likely substantive topic. |
| **Day 4** | Climate Change | Read [climate-change.md](topics/climate-change.md). Practise a "to what extent" essay plan on climate change. Appears in 7/11 sessions. |
| **Day 5** | Biomes | Read [biomes.md](topics/biomes.md). Appears in 6/11 sessions and carries heavy marks when present (often the 18-mark extended task). Know 2–3 biomes with specific examples. |
| **Day 6** | Extended task genres | Read [overview.md](overview.md) §genres. Practise writing in 2 different genres (e.g. formal email + speech). The genre is **always different each session** — know the format conventions for each. |
| **Day 7** | Rest / light review | Skim topic frequency table in [overview.md](overview.md). Identify personal weak spots. |

---

## Week 2 — Past Papers + Essay

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 8** | "To what extent" essay technique | Read overview §mark scheme rules. Write a timed essay plan: position → developed argument → genuine counterargument → synthesis conclusion. The 24-mark essay = 29% of all marks. |
| **Day 9** | Full past paper (timed) | Do [N24](sessions/N24.md) under timed conditions. Mark strictly. Note every dropped mark. |
| **Day 10** | Fix weaknesses | Go back to topics you dropped marks on in Day 9. Re-read those topic pages. |
| **Day 11** | Secondary topics | Read [globalization.md](topics/globalization.md) and [migration.md](topics/migration.md) — both appeared in 3–4 sessions and are plausible for 2026. |
| **Day 12** | Second full past paper | Do [M25](sessions/M25.md) (most recent paper). Mark it. Pay attention to the Q3/Q4 structure — these haven't changed. |
| **Day 13** | Command terms drill | Read [command-terms.md](command-terms.md). Focus on Explain vs Justify vs Evaluate — the three where marks are most commonly dropped. |
| **Day 14** | Essay + light review | Write one final timed essay from scratch. Skim biomes + natural hazards notes. |

---

## Priority if squeezed

If Geography time gets cut, protect these three:

1. **Q3 Evaluate formula** — 8 marks, completely predictable structure, every paper
2. **Q4 Research design** — 12 marks, same structure every paper, learnable in 2 sittings
3. **The 24-mark essay** — 29% of total marks; one strong essay technique covers any topic
