## Q1 — Globalization (6 marks)

**Stimulus**: "Globalization can have positive and negative impacts."

### Q1a (2 marks) — Identify two positive impacts of globalization
**Mark scheme accepted answers** (1 mark each, need two):
- Increased tourism
- Access to internet
- Appreciating other cultures
- Learning other languages
- Cheaper goods

> Note: Single words that do not signify the impact = 0 marks (e.g. just writing "tourism" is not enough — must indicate it as an impact).

### Q1b (4 marks) — Explain a negative consequence of one impact from Q1a

**Markband**:
| Marks | Descriptor | Example response |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | States one negative consequence | "Increased tourism causes more pollution." |
| 2 | Outlines one negative consequence (one sentence with a single elaboration) | — |
| 3 | Describes one negative consequence (no comprehensive reasons) | "Increased tourism causes more pollution from planes and other transport. These modes of transport release carbon dioxide." |
| 4 | Explains one negative consequence with clear reasoning | "Increased tourism causes more pollution. Many modes of transport rely on fossil fuels. Because tourism has become cheaper, tourism's carbon footprint has increased." |

> Note: Responses don't need to link back to Q1a impact — can still get 4 marks even if they pick a different impact.
> Only one consequence should be considered in detail. More important than length: quality and reasons given.

## Q2 — Food security: Tajikistan (8 marks)

**Stimulus**: Podcast transcript + action plan table (Stages 1–4 of food security research investigation for Tajikistan government).

**Context**:
- Tajikistan: Central Asia; 60%+ employed in agriculture; 47% live on <$1.33/day; highest childhood malnutrition rate in Europe & Central Asia
- Country imports 75% of its food needs
- Research question: "How can Tajikistan reduce their reliance on other countries to increase their food security?"
- Action plan stages: (1) Research sub-questions [Launched], (2) Secondary data collection [In progress], (3) Primary data — interview 5 farmers [Not started], (4) Prepare presentation for government [Not started]

**Task**: Evaluate the action plan.

**Markband** (note: "suggesting alternative methods can be interpreted as addressing limitations"):
| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1 | States the strength(s) of the investigation |
| 2 | Outlines the strength(s) — only one strength needs to be mentioned and elaborated (quality rewarded, not quantity); if 3+ strengths outlined → 3 marks |
| 3 | Explains the strength(s) of the investigation — 1 well-developed strength sufficient for 3 marks |
| Marks | Descriptor (Limitations) |
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1 | States the limitation(s) — e.g. "Only five farmers are interviewed" |
| 2 | Outlines the limitation — e.g. "Only five farmers interviewed of population where 60% employed as farmers"; 1 elaborated limitation = 2 marks |
| 3 | Explains the limitation — "Only five farmers interviewed of a population where 60% employed as farmers. The sample size will not represent problems and consultant will not gain sufficient information." |
| Marks | Descriptor (Appraisal/Overall) |
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1 | States an appraisal — "Overall, the investigation was unsuccessful." |
| 2 | Outlines appraisal — weighs positives AND negatives, makes overall judgement; appraisal can appear anywhere (not just conclusion) |

> Key insight: The appraisal (overall judgement) does NOT have to be a concluding statement — it can appear anywhere in the response. Examiners look for implicit or explicit weighing.

## Q3 — Research design: Globalization (12 marks, criterion-marked)

**Stimulus**: Same as Q1. Statement of inquiry: *"Local and global processes are interconnected as a result of globalization, creating challenges and opportunities."*

**Task**: A local government wants to understand social OR environmental impacts of globalization on trade/exchange. Design an action plan.

### Q3a (2 marks) — Formulate a clear and focused research question for the investigation
**Markband**:
| Marks | Descriptor | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | Student formulates a RQ that is somewhat clear but not focused, OR is focused but not clearly connected to the SoI | "How has free trade changed the lives of factory workers?" |
| 2 | Student formulates a clear AND focused RQ that is connected to the SoI | "How has increased trade in Myanmar increased the lives of factory workers?" |

> Key: RQ must be (a) clear/specific, (b) focused on a particular place/group, (c) connected to the statement of inquiry. Vague RQs lose marks.

### Q3b (4 marks) — Justify why your research question will allow the government to achieve their goal
**Markband**:
| Marks | Descriptor | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | States a simple reason why RQ will achieve goal | "More trade has led to more people being employed by clothing factories." |
| 2 | Outlines how RQ allows government to understand globalization's impact (short 2-clause sentence) | — |
| 3 | Student explains how RQ allows government to understand globalization clearly; one specific argument + clear link to SoI | "More trade has led to more people being employed by clothing factories. The minimum wage has been put in place to ensure workers are not exploited. The government will understand the social impact of globalization." |
| 4 | Student explains connection in detail, provides reasoning and link to SoI | "More trade has led to more people being employed by clothing factories. The minimum wage... The poverty levels will help understand the impact of more trade on people who are employed by factories. This will help understand the social benefits and limitations of increased globalization." |

> Note: One well-developed argument/factor is enough to get full marks. Don't need multiple reasons — need DEPTH.

## Q4 — Biomes: Podcast script (18 marks, Criterion A)

**Stimulus**: Podcast transcript where you are a guest being asked about biomes. Host's question: *"How are humans using scientific and technical innovations to harm or to protect biomes?"*

**Task**: Write a script in response. Must include:
- **State** a title for the episode
- **Explain** how humans are using scientific and technical innovations to harm or protect natural environments
- Reference at least **two biomes** from MYP studies
- Provide a **closing statement**

**Criterion A markbands** (0–18):
| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1–2 | Limited contextual/conceptual understanding in outline; limited examples; limited terminology |
| 3–4 | Adequate understanding in description; satisfactory examples; appropriate terminology |
| 5–6 | Substantial knowledge with detailed description of how innovations harm/protect biomes; relevant examples; accurate terminology |
| 7–8 | Ideas build logically; effective transitions within/between sentences; language appropriate to audience and podcast purpose |
| 9–12 | Consistent use of effective language; logical ideas; clear for audience; rhetorical questions and concise sentences used to engage audience |
| 13–18 | Full marks band: language features (rhetorical questions, analogies, concise sentences) used consistently to engage; all podcast requirements met |

> Key insight: This is a LANGUAGE + CONTENT question. You need: correct geography (two biomes + innovations) AND appropriate podcast language (engaging, rhetorical questions, clear for a general audience). Don't write like an essay — write like you're speaking.

## Q6 — Natural hazards: Lahaina wildfire, Hawaii (8 marks)

**Stimulus**: Source C — perspective of an 80-year-old resident of Lahaina. Context: Lahaina town council has proposed two strategies for reducing disaster risk.

**Task**: Analyse the effectiveness of the two strategies from the perspective of an 80-year-old citizen.

**Markband**:
| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1–2 | States perspective; brief; little detail — "Overall, the investigation was unsuccessful" style response |
| 3–4 | Outlines effectiveness of strategies from 80-year-old perspective; provides limited detail; short paragraph likely |
| 5–6 | Describes effectiveness of BOTH strategies in detail from 80-year-old perspective; relevant details about age-related challenges included |
| 7–8 | Analyses effectiveness; details are consistently accurate; relevant conclusion on both strategies drawn; analytical conclusion required |

> Key insight: 80-year-old perspective matters — may have difficulty accessing real-time information, difficulty evacuating quickly, may not use social media. Evacuation drills may help tell them where to go but physical difficulty with moving quickly remains. Analytical conclusion must weigh both strategies' effectiveness.

## Q7 — Natural hazards: Essay (24 marks, Criterion A)

**Task**: In a well-structured essay, answer: *"To what extent can differences in income influence the severity of the impacts of natural disasters?"*

Must include:
- Different perspectives
- At least one example of a natural disaster to support arguments
- Appropriate geographical terms

**Criterion A markbands**:
| Marks | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy |
| 1–2 | Limited contextual/conceptual understanding; outline; limited examples; limited terminology |
| 3–4 | Adequate understanding in description; satisfactory examples; appropriate terminology (e.g. names specific disaster but detail may be lacking) |
| 5–6 | Substantial knowledge; full description of management of disaster impacts; examples accurate; terminology appropriate |
| 7–8 | Ideas build on each other logically; effective transitions within/between sentences; shows relationships between ideas |
| 9–12 | Frequent use of transitions; shows relationships between ideas |
| 13–24 | Upper markbands: demonstrates synthesis across perspectives; clear conclusion with justified judgement |

> Key argument framework:
> - **YES income influences severity**: poorer countries/communities have weaker infrastructure, less warning systems, less insurance, slower recovery; example — Haiti 2010 earthquake vs Chile 2010 earthquake (similar magnitude, vastly different death tolls due to income)
> - **COUNTERARGUMENT**: geography also matters (coastal vs inland, plate boundaries); governance matters; population density; nature of disaster
> - **CONCLUSION**: Income is a MAJOR factor but not the only one — most powerful argument is recovery time (poorer countries take far longer to rebuild)

## Key mark scheme patterns (M25)
1. **Quality over quantity**: For all developed-answer questions, one well-elaborated point beats three shallow points
2. **Appraisal can be implicit**: For evaluate questions, the overall judgement doesn't need to be in a conclusion paragraph
3. **Command terms are strict**: "Identify" gets 0 if you write an explanation; "Explain" gets 2 marks max if you only describe
4. **Best-fit model**: If you hit 2 out of 3 markband requirements, you still get the mark — examiners reward, not penalise
5. **Examples optional for Q1–Q3**: But mandatory for Q4 and Q7 — check task requirements
