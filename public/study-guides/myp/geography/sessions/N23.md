## Q3 — Research methods: Evaluate the action plan (8 marks)

**Stimulus**: The local tourist operators in Kaikōura are investigating Option A and Option B (8 weeks to make their final decision). Action plan:
- **Goal**: Decide if Option A or Option B is the best way to manage tourist numbers in South Bay
- **Phase 1 (4 weeks)**:
  - Interview tourist operators in neighbouring towns who have charged a fee for tourists entering the area
  - Interview residents in Kaikōura about their opinions on building a car park
  - Carry out a traffic count survey over three weeks to identify peak tourist times
- **Phase 2 (2 weeks)**:
  - Review data
  - Explain the strengths and weaknesses of each proposal
- **Phase 3 (1 week)**:
  - Decide on option A or B to manage tourist numbers
  - Create a written report to present to the community on the option chosen

**Task**: **Evaluate** the tourist operators' action plan.

This question is split across three separate markbands: Strengths + Limitations + Appraisal (total 8 marks).

### Strengths markband (0–3 marks):
| Marks | Descriptor | Example response |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | States the strength(s) of the action plan | "A range of primary data is used." Only one strength needs to be mentioned. A 1-mark response is one sentence referring to a single strength. |
| 2 | Outlines the strength(s) of the action plan | "Both quantitative and qualitative primary data, such as traffic counts and interviews, are used to collect data." One strength mentioned and elaborated with brief detail. If a student outlines 3 or more strengths, they can be awarded 3 marks. |
| 3 | Explains the strength(s) of the action plan | One strength with detailed reasoning. |

### Limitations markband (0–3 marks):
| Marks | Descriptor | Example response |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | States the limitation(s) of the action plan | "The traffic count won't give data for the whole year." One limitation briefly referred to. |
| 2 | Outlines the limitation of the action plan | "The traffic count is only conducted over 3 weeks and won't give data for different seasons." One limitation mentioned and elaborated with brief detail. If a student outlines 3 or more limitations, they can be awarded 3 marks. |
| 3 | Explains the limitation(s) of the action plan | "There could be differences in traffic in different seasons, which would impact whether the car park is needed or whether the data would not be reliable." One limitation with detailed reasoning. |

### Appraisal markband (0–2 marks):
| Marks | Descriptor | Example response |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | States an appraisal of the action plan | "Overall, the strengths outweigh the limitations." For 1 mark, the appraisal is likely a summary of strengths and limitations or a brief judgement on the investigative process. |
| 2 | Outlines an appraisal of the action plan | "The weakness of the traffic count will not stop the tourist operators from completing their investigation. Overall, the strengths outweigh the limitations." The appraisal statement will weigh up both positive and negative points and make an overall judgement on the students' investigative process. |

> Key insights:
> - Do NOT credit a statement as a strength or limitation AND an appraisal — it counts as one or the other, not both.
> - The appraisal can appear ANYWHERE in the response — it does not have to be a concluding statement.
> - 3 or more outlined strengths/limitations = bumped up to 3 marks even if each is only briefly elaborated.

## Key mark scheme patterns (N23)

1. **Map reference required for Q2**: Without explicitly referencing named locations, grid references, or map features, maximum is capped (3 marks, not 4). Always name the map feature being described.

2. **Three-part evaluate structure (Q3)**: Strengths (3) + Limitations (3) + Appraisal (2) = 8 marks. The appraisal does NOT need to be a conclusion — it can appear anywhere. Do NOT double-count a point as both a limitation and an appraisal.

3. **Research question focus (Q4a)**: A RQ must be (a) clear, (b) focused on a named place/group AND type of impact, and (c) connected to the SoI. Missing any one of these = 1 mark, not 2. Simply rewording the SoI = 0.

4. **Source value/limitation pattern (Q7b, Q7c)**: These follow a strict 1/2/3 mark pattern: state → outline (brief detail) → explain (2–3 sentences elaborating one point in detail). Reasoning is NOT required at the 3-mark level — depth and elaboration of ONE point is.

5. **Biome justify question (Q6)**: Must name the activity AND the biome AND give reasons AND a conclusion for 4 marks. A conclusion is essential but does not need to appear at the end. Examples are helpful but not mandatory.

6. **Criterion A (Q5, Q8) rewards content only**: Format/communication is assessed separately under Criterion C/D. Students who write accurate geography but in poor essay form can still score well on Criterion A.

7. **Best-fit model applies throughout**: Examiners reward what is present and match to the nearest markband overall. If a student meets 2 of 3 requirements in a markband, they receive that band's mark. Mark positively — do not penalise incorrect points.

8. **Source A statistics (Q5) must be used**: At higher mark levels the student must demonstrate they have engaged with the stimulus data (the WTO statistics) to support their explanations of sustainable trade and SDG progress.
