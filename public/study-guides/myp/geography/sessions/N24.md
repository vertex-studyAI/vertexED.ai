## Key mark scheme patterns (N24)

1. **Quality over quantity across all questions**: For every developed-answer question, one well-elaborated point with reasoning consistently beats three shallow points. This is explicitly stated in almost every mark band.
2. **"Outline" is a genuine mid-level command**: Outline = brief account or summary. One sentence with brief elaboration = 2 marks. Students often write too much for "outline" questions — a clear two-clause sentence is sufficient.
3. **Appraisal/overall judgement can appear anywhere**: For Q3 (evaluate), the overall judgement does not have to be a concluding statement — it can appear anywhere in the response.
4. **Criterion A questions require both content AND language**: Q5 (speech) and Q8 (essay) are marked on content knowledge (Criterion A) AND language/format (Criterion C). Even excellent geography knowledge will be limited without appropriate register and structure.
5. **Speech vs essay registers are distinct**: Q5 requires persuasive, community-audience speech language (greeting, persuasive tone). Q8 requires formal essay language (introduction, paragraphs, conclusion as separate elements). Both require elements to appear as separate paragraphs/sections to earn format marks.
6. **Student's own context is accepted in Q4 and Q5**: For Q4 (migration RQ) and Q5 (natural resource speech), students choose their own place and context. The mark scheme examples (Germany/Syrian refugees; Cadiz Mine/Australia/Gold) are illustrative — any valid geographic context is acceptable.
7. **Best-fit model strictly applied**: If a student spans two markbands, the overall quality determines where they sit. Hitting 2 out of 3 requirements in a band is sufficient — examiners reward, not penalise.
8. **Both cities accepted in Q7**: Either Barcelona or Madrid can be argued as more impacted by climate change — provided the justification uses Source B data. There is no single correct answer.
9. **"Formulate" requires both clarity AND focus**: A research question that is clear but not focused (no specific place/migration/time) only gets 1/2. Must have both qualities plus connection to the statement of inquiry.
10. **N24 topic**: This paper focuses heavily on **population/demography** (Q1–4) and **climate change** (Q6–8), with **natural resources/sustainability** in Q5. This is a notable shift from N23/M25 which emphasised natural hazards and globalization.
