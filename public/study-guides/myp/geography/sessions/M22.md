## Q1 — Natural hazards: 2004 Indonesia Tsunami (2 marks)

**Stimulus**: Video + transcript about the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami. Key facts in transcript:
- Indo-Australian and Eurasian plates collided under the Indian Ocean
- 26 December 2004, 30 km below ocean surface — fault ruptured, creating giant ripples (tsunami)
- Wave hit Banda Aceh, Indonesia at 30 metres high
- Force equivalent to sixty hurricanes; destroyed 100,000 houses and one third of critical infrastructure along the Banda Aceh coast
- Forced many people to leave home in search of safety
- Impact felt in coastal cities of multiple countries worldwide

**Source A**: Interactive before/after slider image showing Banda Aceh coastline before and after the tsunami (aerial satellite imagery). Visible total destruction of coastal area.

**Task**: **State** two impacts of the 2004 tsunami in Indonesia.

**Mark scheme accepted answers** (1 mark each, up to 2):
- Loss of life
- Flooding
- Destruction of houses
- Destruction of infrastructure
- Loss of trees/vegetation
- Forced migration / displacement

> Note: Accept any valid response. Award 1 mark per impact, up to a maximum of 2 marks. Responses do not have to be shown in the source material.

## Q3 — Research methods: NGO investigation evaluation (8 marks)

**Stimulus**: NGO "Tsunami Response Indonesia" investigation website.

**Statement of inquiry**: *Place and space determine the impact of natural disasters on communities.*

**Research question**: How did the 2004 tsunami impact the movement of people within Aceh Province, Indonesia?

**Goal**: Use mobile data to track movement of people following the December 26th, 2004 tsunami and create a map to show results.

**Methodology**:
- Location of a person determined by where they are when they make a call
- Use mobile phones to work out where people are located when they use their phones
- Track location of 1.9 million mobile phones (approximately half the population of Aceh Province)
- Timeframe for data collection: December 27th, 2004 – January 12th, 2005

**Map produced**: Choropleth map showing "Internally Displaced People, 12 January 2005" across districts of Aceh Province. Total number of people displaced ranges from 2,400–5,500 (lightest) to 50,001–116,984 (darkest). Some districts have "data not available."

> - **CONCLUSION**: A convincing conclusion weighing which perspective is most important is required for top marks. Sustainable extraction is essential but the extent depends on enforcement, economic alternatives, and addressing other drivers simultaneously.

## Key mark scheme patterns (M22)

1. **Quality over quantity**: For all developed-answer questions, one well-elaborated point beats three shallow points. This is explicitly stated in almost every mark scheme note.
2. **Appraisal can appear anywhere**: For Q3 evaluate, the overall judgement does not need to be in a conclusion — it can appear anywhere in the response.
3. **"Outline" is a 2-mark ceiling without elaboration**: For outline questions, you must have a fact + one elaborating clause. Bare facts score 1 only.
4. **Alternative perspective must be genuine**: For Q7, you must disagree with the quoted resident's view (that desalinization is not worth it). Agreeing with them earns 0.
5. **Best-fit model**: If you hit 2 out of 3 markband requirements, you still get the mark — examiners reward, not penalise.
6. **Blog ≠ essay**: Q5 is criterion-marked for communication style — a blog format (first-person, sub-headings, rhetorical questions, emotive language) is required alongside correct geographical content.
7. **Research question specificity**: Q4a rewards specificity — a clear place + a clear strategy + connection to SoI. Rewording the SoI = 0.
8. **Source B origin is exact**: Q6b accepts only exact website name (allthatiswater.org / Allthatiswater / www.allthatiswater.org).
9. **Essay requires two perspectives**: Q9 Criterion D requires two distinct perspectives (for and against/other angle) with development. One-sided essays cap at marks 1–2 on Criterion D.
10. **Format marks are structural**: Q5 (blog) and Q9 (essay) both award separate format marks — these are easy marks but require the physical structural elements to appear as separate paragraphs.
