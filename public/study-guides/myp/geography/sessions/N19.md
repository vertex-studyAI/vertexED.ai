## Q2 — Natural resources: Scarcity (4 marks)

**Command term**: **Explain**

**Question text**: Natural resources can become scarce. Using an example from your MYP studies, **explain** one factor that can cause scarcity of natural resources.

**Key note**: The maximum mark is 3 marks if the student does not include an example. An explanation of a factor without an example would receive 3 marks; a description without an example would receive 2 marks.

**Markband**:
| Marks | Descriptor | Example response |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing worthy | — |
| 1 | Student **states** one factor that can cause natural resources scarcity | "Population growth." (A 1-mark response is likely to be one sentence that hints at a single factor.) |
| 2 | Student **outlines** one factor, using an example | "Population growth is causing greater demand for food and this puts pressure on land." (A 2-mark response is likely to be a single sentence with two clauses or two/three short sentences; the detail provided will be brief.) |
| 3 | Student **describes** one factor, using an example (without comprehensive reasoning) | "Population growth is causing greater demand for food. As the greatest growth of population is in the least developed countries, there is pressure on land. This leads to deforestation and over-grazing which increases desertification." (One factor can be considered in detail without any comprehensive reasoning.) |
| 3–4 | Student **explains** one factor, using an example | "Population growth is causing greater demand for food. As the greatest growth of population is **in Sub-Saharan Africa**, there is pressure on land. This leads to deforestation and over-grazing which increases desertification, **reducing the productivity of the land**." (One factor should be considered in detail, along with reasoning why human activities could cause the trend shown. An example may be given to support the explanation.) |

> Key: Without an example, the maximum mark is 3. With a named example (e.g. Sub-Saharan Africa, specific resource), full 4 marks are available. Quality and depth over quantity — one well-elaborated factor with reasoning.

## Q5 — Tourism / Natural environments: Petition (18 marks, Criterion A)

**Command term**: **Explain** (within a petition format)

**Stimulus (video transcript)**: A TV report on the impacts of tourism on the natural environment — "overtourism":
- Popular destinations becoming overrun with tourists in an unsustainable way
- Mount Everest, Nepal: thousands of tonnes of rubbish and human waste left each year
- Galapagos Islands, Ecuador: unique species threatened by pollution and disturbance
- Great Barrier Reef, Australia: tourism contributing to loss of coral
- Mediterranean coast beaches: pollution from plastics and waste causing severe damage to sea and marine wildlife
- "If something is not done urgently, these natural environments will be lost to future generations."

**Task**: The video has inspired you to start a petition to convince world leaders to take action and protect natural environments.
- Write an appropriate **title** for your petition
- **Drag and drop** one image to be shown on your petition (from: coastal erosion, crowded beach, tourists at ruins, plastic/waste, globe image)
- Write a brief **caption** for your chosen image
- A local TV channel asks to interview you. Write your **interview response**: Can you **explain** to our viewers how natural environments are threatened by overtourism and how this can be managed sustainably for future generations? (Must refer to examples from MYP studies.)

## Q7 — Natural hazards: Vulnerability Venn diagram (4 marks)

**Command term**: **Describe**

**Question text**: With reference to **Source B** below, **describe** the relationship between natural disaster factors and vulnerability.

- Left circle (green): Natural disaster factors — Magnitude, Frequency

## Q9 — Natural hazards / Technology: Essay (18 marks, Criterion A + D)

**Note from mark scheme**: Italics are used to indicate parts of the response rewarded under Criterion A. Underlining indicates parts rewarded under Criterion D. Bold indicates the difference in depth/detail between markbands.

**Criterion A markbands** (0–8):
| Marks | Descriptor | Notes/Example |
|-------|-----------|--------------|

> Key argument framework:
> - **YES, technology reduces impacts in HICs**: Early warning systems (Japan seismic alerts, text alerts), improved building standards (shock absorbers, earthquake-resistant design), search and rescue robots/drones, satellite monitoring (Wildfire Alert), prediction systems
> - **COUNTERARGUMENT / LIMITATIONS**: Hurricane Katrina (USA, 2005) — HIC with advanced systems but over 25,000 people forced to seek shelter in Superdome; socio-economic inequality within HICs means not everyone can respond appropriately; California: even with earthquake warnings, people are told to "duck, cover and hold" — if unprepared, casualties still occur; education may be more important than technology alone
> - **CONCLUSION**: Technology can reduce impacts significantly in HICs, **but it is not the only method** — it is most effective when combined with other techniques (education, governance, community preparedness). Not all residents of HICs have equal access to or benefit from technology.

## Key mark scheme patterns (N19)

1. **Examples are essential for Q2 maximum marks**: Without a named example, the maximum score on Q2 is 3 marks, not 4. Always name a specific place or context.
2. **Appraisal placement is flexible (Q3)**: The overall judgement in evaluate questions can appear anywhere in the response — examiners identify implicit weighing, not just concluding sentences.
3. **Quality over quantity throughout**: For all developed-answer questions (Q2, Q3, Q4b, Q4d, Q4e, Q7, Q8), one well-elaborated point consistently earns more marks than three shallow points.
4. **Criterion A vs Criterion D distinction (Q9)**: These are assessed separately. A response can have strong knowledge (Criterion A) but weak analytical structure (Criterion D), and vice versa. Best-fit applies to each criterion independently.
5. **Communication questions (Q5) weight language equally with content**: Criterion A (knowledge) is only 6 of 18 marks. The remaining 12 marks assess format, language appropriateness, and organizational structure. Write persuasively, not academically.
6. **"To what extent" requires explicit counter-argument and conclusion**: For Q9, reaching the top markband requires acknowledging where technology fails within HICs (e.g. Hurricane Katrina), not just listing positive examples.
7. **List command term (Q4c) = no explanation needed**: "List" questions are awarded at 1 mark per valid item. Do not waste time explaining — save it for outline/explain questions.
8. **Data must be referenced for graph questions (Q6)**: Simply stating the direction of a trend earns 1 mark; quoting actual figures from the source earns 2 marks.
