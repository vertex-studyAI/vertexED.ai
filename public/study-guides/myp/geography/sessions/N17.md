## Key mark scheme patterns (N17)

1. **Source reference is mandatory for Q1 and Q2**: Generalised responses that ignore the source are capped at 2 marks (Q1) or 4 marks (Q2). Must explicitly draw on the source data.

2. **Criterion B (Q3, Q4) emphasises process over content**: Marks are awarded for quality of research design thinking — specificity of RQ, justification of relevance, appropriateness of methods — not subject knowledge.

3. **Cumulative elaboration model for Q6 (Describe)**: Each higher mark = same point with one more layer of reasoning, not a new point. Practise adding "so that..." or "because..." clauses.

4. **Q7b justification requires cause-effect chains**: Stating the innovation is good = 1–2 marks. Full marks needs chained consequences: innovation → mechanism → outcome → SDG link → scale/breadth argument.

5. **Q5 and Q8 are dual/triple criterion questions**: Criterion A (knowledge), C (communication/format/structure), and D (critical thinking) are marked independently. A brilliant essay with poor format can score high on A but low on C. Each criterion must be addressed intentionally.

6. **"To what extent" (Q8) must include counterarguments**: Responses that only present one side (even if detailed) cannot reach the top Criterion D band. Challenges, limitations, and conditions for success are required.

7. **Best-fit model applies throughout**: Examiners reward what is present, not penalise for what is missing. Hit the majority of descriptors in a band to secure that mark.

8. **Any valid response accepted for short-answer questions (Q4d–4h, Q6a–6b)**: The mark scheme lists examples but explicitly states "accept any other valid response" — do not memorise specific answers, understand the underlying concept.
