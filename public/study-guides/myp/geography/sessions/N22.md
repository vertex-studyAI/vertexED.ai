## Q1 — Land degradation: Sahel / Great Green Wall (2 marks)

**Stimulus**: Source A — map and infographic about Africa's Great Green Wall Project.

**Context**:
- The Sahel region is the arid savannah south of the Sahara Desert, crossing 11 countries
- The Great Green Wall Project, started in 2007, aims to restore 100 million hectares of degraded land in the Sahel region by 2030 and create 10 million jobs in rural areas
- The Great Green Wall is expected to be 8,000 km long and 15 km wide; runs along the Barrier Reef; uses a mixture of trees, grasslands, and plants across the Sahel region
- 45% of Africa's land has been degraded; as a result of climate change, changing weather patterns, over-grazing, population growth, unsustainable practices
- Nearly 15% of the project was estimated to have been completed in 2022 (Source A note)
- Source A data: Total restored area up to 2019: 503 hectares across Ghana, Mali, Chad

### Q1a (1 mark) — Identify one human factor that has caused land degradation in the Sahel region

**Mark scheme accepted answers** (any one):
- Overgrazing
- Population growth
- Agriculture
- Over-farming

> Note: Identifying "unsustainable practices" is NOT a human factor and should be awarded 0 marks.

### Q1b (1 mark) — Identify one natural factor that has caused land degradation in the Sahel region

**Mark scheme accepted answers** (any one):
- Extreme weather
- Drought
- Changing weather patterns

> Accept any valid response.

## Key mark scheme patterns (N22)

1. **Quality over quantity — consistent across all questions**: One well-elaborated point at the right depth beats three shallow points. For Q2, Q3b, Q4, Q7, Q8: one argument developed fully = full marks.

2. **"Outline" = state + one elaboration**: In N22 the command term "outline" is used for Q3c, Q3d, Q3e, and Q6b. In every case, 1 mark = bare statement; 2 marks = statement + brief elaboration in the same or following sentence.

3. **Appraisal can be implicit and anywhere**: For Q2 (evaluate), the overall judgement does NOT need to be in a concluding paragraph. It can appear anywhere in the response and can be implicit (weighing positive against negative).

4. **Speech vs essay format matters**: Q5 has separate marks for format (C1), communication style (C2), and organization (C3). A factually correct response written in essay style will lose marks on C2. Use of first person, rhetorical questions, emotive vocabulary, and personal pronouns is required for C2 5–6.

5. **Two benefits required for Q5 full marks**: Explaining only ONE benefit of migration caps the content (Criterion A) at 4 marks regardless of how well that one benefit is explained.

6. **Data from graphs helpful but not essential**: For Q6b (describe relationship), referencing specific figures from the graph is likely but NOT essential for 3 marks — sufficient descriptive detail alone qualifies.

7. **Both social AND environmental sustainability required for Q9 upper marks**: Responses addressing only one dimension cannot reach Criterion A 5–6 or 7–8. Similarly, perspectives must be balanced/equally considered for Criterion D 5–6 and 7–8.

8. **Best-fit model throughout**: Mark positively. If a response hits 2 of 3 markband requirements, award the mark. Do not penalise for incorrect points alongside correct ones.
