# Command Terms

Official MYP Geography definitions (from mark schemes). These are the exact words examiners use — get them wrong and you'll answer the wrong question.

## The terms

| Term | Official definition | Marks it typically appears for | What to actually do |
|------|--------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Identify** | Provide an answer from a number of possibilities. Recognize and state briefly a distinguishing fact or feature. | 1–2 | Name it. One sentence max. No explanation needed. |
| **State** | Give a specific name, value or other brief answer without explanation or calculation. | 1–2 | Single word or phrase. Shortest answer possible. |
| **Outline** | Give a brief account or summary. | 2–3 | Brief description — more than identify but no deep reasoning. |
| **Describe** | Give a detailed account or picture of a situation, event, pattern or process. | 2–4 | Paint a picture of WHAT is happening. Use data from source if given. No causes/reasons needed. |
| **Explain** | Give a detailed account including reasons or causes. | 3–4 | WHAT + WHY. Use connectives: "because", "this means that", "as a result". |
| **Analyse** | Break down in order to bring out the essential elements or structure. To identify parts and relationships, and to interpret information to reach conclusions. | 4–6 | Break it into components, show how they connect, draw a conclusion. |
| **Justify** | Give valid reasons or evidence to support an answer or conclusion. | 4 | State your position + give 2–3 specific reasons/evidence for it. Not just description. |
| **Evaluate** | Make an appraisal by weighing up the strengths and limitations. | 4–8 | Both sides (strengths AND limitations/weaknesses). Conclude which outweighs. |
| **To what extent** | Consider the merits or otherwise of an argument or concept. Opinions and conclusions should be presented clearly and supported with appropriate evidence and sound argument. | 8–12 | Argue a position, acknowledge counter-arguments, conclude with justified judgement. |
| **Formulate** | (Q3 type) Express precisely and systematically. | 2 | Write a clear, focused research question — specific, investigable, relevant to the stimulus. |

## Mark allocation logic

### For short-answer questions (Identify/State/Outline)
- 1 mark per valid point
- No marks for repetition
- No marks for vague answers ("it affects people")

### For explain/describe questions (markband model)
Typical 4-mark markband:
- **0**: Nothing worthy of credit
- **1–2**: Basic — states a point but doesn't develop it; or development is too general
- **3–4**: Developed — clear point with explanation AND specific detail/example

### For evaluate/justify questions
Examiners look for:
- At least one strength AND one limitation (for evaluate)
- Specific evidence, not vague claims
- A conclusion that weighs the two sides

### For criterion-based Q3 (12 marks)
Marked against sub-criteria (a, b, c, d), each worth 2–4 marks with their own markbands. See individual session pages for exact criteria.

## Common mistakes

- **Identify → writing an essay**: wastes time, gets no extra marks
- **Explain → only describing**: "Flooding causes damage" = describe. "Flooding causes damage because poor households cannot afford to repair homes" = explain
- **Evaluate → only listing strengths**: must weigh strengths against limitations
- **Justify → re-stating the question**: must give *reasons*, not just repeat the claim
- **To what extent → no conclusion**: must reach a clear judgement at the end
