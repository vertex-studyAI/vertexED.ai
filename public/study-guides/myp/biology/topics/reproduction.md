# Reproduction

## Topic overview

Covers sexual vs asexual reproduction, mitosis vs meiosis, fertilisation and genetic variation, the menstrual cycle with its four hormones, puberty, and how meiosis generates genetic variation. Appears consistently under Criterion A (knowledge) and occasionally links to Criterion D (impacts of reproductive technology).

---

## Key concepts

### Sexual vs asexual reproduction

**Asexual reproduction**:
- Involves one parent only
- Offspring are genetically identical to the parent (clones)
- Uses mitosis
- Advantages: fast; energy-efficient; guaranteed reproduction in stable environments

**Sexual reproduction**:
- Involves two parents (or gametes from two different individuals)
- Offspring are genetically unique (due to meiosis + fertilisation)
- Produces genetic variation in the population
- Advantages: variation allows adaptation to changing environments; natural selection can act

### Mitosis vs meiosis

**Mitosis**:
- Produces 2 genetically IDENTICAL daughter cells
- Daughter cells are diploid (same chromosome number as parent cell — 46 in humans)
- Function: growth, repair, replacement of cells, asexual reproduction
- No genetic variation produced

**Meiosis**:
- Produces 4 genetically NON-IDENTICAL daughter cells
- Daughter cells are haploid (half the chromosome number — 23 in humans)
- Function: production of gametes (sperm and eggs); sexual reproduction
- Produces genetic variation

**Why sex cells have 23 chromosomes** (N22 Q3c):
- Two sex cells will come together at fertilisation
- To produce offspring with 46 chromosomes (diploid)
- If gametes were diploid, the offspring would have 92 chromosomes

**Venn diagram (N21 Q2b)** — similarities and differences:
| Mitosis only | Both | Meiosis only |
|---|---|---|
| Two daughter cells | Begins with a single parent cell | Four daughter cells |
| Daughter cells have 46 chromosomes | Begins with two copies of each chromosome | Genetically non-identical daughter cells |

Mark scheme: one correctly placed in EACH zone = 1 mark; all correctly placed = 2 marks

**M17 Q1e** — two types of cell division and two differences:
- Mitosis / Meiosis
- Differences: genetically identical vs not genetically identical; same chromosome number vs halved; somatic cells vs gametes
- Do NOT accept "sexual vs asexual reproduction" as the names of the types

**M18 Q3a** — the process that produces gametes: Meiosis (NOT mitosis, homeostasis, or photosynthesis)

### Fertilisation and genetic variation

**Fertilisation** = combination of genetic material from a sperm and an egg (N21 Q2c, M18 Q3b)

How fertilisation leads to genetic variation (N21 Q2c — 4 marks):
- Every individual has different genetic material
- Every sex cell / gamete is unique (as a result of meiosis)
- Genetic material from two parents is combined
- Every sex cell has an equal chance of being fertilised → offspring are unique from both parents

How fertilisation leads to variation — M18 Q3b framing (3 marks):
- Each parent has different genetic material / traits / genes / alleles
- Half of the offspring's genetic material comes from each parent
- Combination of genetic material creates a new, unique individual

Mark scheme note: Accept references to crossing over in relation to gametes.

### Why variation is important (M21 Q1f — 5 marks):

1. Variation results in a range of characteristics
2. Individuals with favourable characteristics are more likely to survive (survival of the fittest)
3. Survivors are more likely to reproduce
4. Characteristics are passed on to offspring
5. Species becomes better adapted to its environment / favourable characteristics become more common

### Sex determination (N22 Q3d)

- Females: XX; Males: XY
- Eggs always contain X chromosome
- Sperm can contain X (→ female offspring) or Y (→ male offspring)
- Sperm determines sex of offspring

### The menstrual cycle

**Four hormones and their roles** (M18 Q3c, N24 Q2b–c):

| Hormone | Produced by | Role |
|---|---|---|
| FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) | Pituitary gland | Causes egg to mature in the ovary |
| LH (Luteinising Hormone) | Pituitary gland | Triggers ovulation (release of mature egg) |
| Oestrogen (Estrogen) | Ovaries | Repairs and thickens uterus lining; stops FSH being produced so only one egg matures; stimulates pituitary to release LH |
| Progesterone | Ovaries | Maintains lining of uterus during middle of cycle and during pregnancy |

**How oestrogen and progesterone work together to prepare for pregnancy** (N24 Q2b):
- Oestrogen thickens the uterus lining
- Progesterone maintains the uterus lining
- So that a fertilised egg / zygote can implant and survive

**Role of LH** (N24 Q2c):
- Triggers ovulation (release of a mature egg)

**What if oestrogen does NOT stop FSH production** (M18 Q3d):
- More than one egg could mature
- Could lead to twins / multiple embryos / hypersecretion of oestrogen
- Do NOT accept effects of oestrogen — must be about FSH

**M18 Q3c** — graph labelling of hormone curves over 28 days:
- The sharp peak (spike) on the graph = LH (triggers ovulation)
- The gradual rise and fall = oestrogen
- The sustained plateau in the second half = progesterone
- The lower wave in the first half = FSH
- One correct = 1 mark; two correct = 2 marks; all correct = 3 marks

### Reproduction in cell division context (N21 Q2a)

Two reasons cells must divide:
- Growth
- Repair or replacement
- (Sexual) reproduction / to produce gametes
- (Asexual) reproduction
Note: "reproduction" alone is accepted for one mark only.

### Genetic variation from meiosis (M21 Q1)

- Meiosis produces genetically non-identical gametes because chromosomes are shuffled (independent assortment) and crossing over occurs between homologous chromosomes
- This creates new allele combinations in gametes
- When gametes from two different individuals combine at fertilisation, even more variation is produced
- This variation is the raw material for natural selection

**Punnett squares and genetics** (M21 Q1d–e, N23 Q1d–e):
- M21 Q1d: Mother with no dimples (dd); Father with dimples (Dd)
  - Punnett square: Dd × dd → 50% Dd (dimples), 50% dd (no dimples)
  - Probability of dimples = 50% or 1/2 or 1:1
- N23 Q1d: Cystic fibrosis — both carriers (Aa × Aa)
  - Punnett square: AA, Aa, Aa, aa → 75% unaffected, 25% affected
  - Probability of being carrier but not affected = 50% (Aa × 2 out of 4)

## Mark scheme insights

1. **Meiosis vs mitosis Venn (N21 Q2b)**: Partial credit — one item correctly placed in each zone (Mitosis, Both, Meiosis) earns 1 mark. All correct earns 2 marks. "Begins with a single parent cell" = BOTH; "genetically non-identical" = Meiosis only.
2. **Fertilisation and variation (N21 Q2c)**: Four distinct points needed. "Every sex cell is unique" is a key mark that students often miss (they focus on "two parents combine").
3. **Menstrual cycle hormone graph (M18 Q3c)**: The drag-and-drop requires recognition of the LH SPIKE (sharp peak around day 14 = ovulation trigger). Confusing LH and FSH is the most common error.
4. **Why sex cells have 23 chromosomes (N22 Q3c)**: Two-part answer — (1) TWO sex cells fuse at fertilisation; (2) to restore 46 chromosomes in offspring. Both parts needed for 2 marks.
5. **FSH absent / oestrogen fails to stop FSH (M18 Q3d)**: Answer must be about FSH effects (multiple eggs mature), not oestrogen effects. Do NOT accept effects of oestrogen.
6. **Punnett square probability (M21 Q1e)**: Accept 50% OR 1/2 OR 1:1 OR 2/4 OR 2:2. ECF applies — if wrong genotype in Q1d, still award marks for consistent Punnett square.
7. **Genotype notation**: Accept Aa or aA. Do NOT accept "a" alone (N23 Q1 — must write full genotype "dd" not just "d").
8. **Puberty Venn (N22 Q3b)**: "Pubic hair grows" = BOTH; "menstruation starts" = Females only; "facial hair grows" = Males only.

## Common mistakes

- Writing "asexual reproduction produces identical offspring using mitosis" — correct — but then also writing "sexual reproduction uses meiosis" without mentioning FERTILISATION is also needed
- Saying "sex cells have fewer chromosomes because they are smaller" — the reason is so that fertilisation restores the correct chromosome number
- Mixing up oestrogen and progesterone roles — oestrogen THICKENS the lining; progesterone MAINTAINS it
- Confusing the LH spike (triggers ovulation) with the FSH wave (causes egg to mature)
- Writing only "two parents combine" for variation question — must also mention MEIOSIS makes unique gametes
- Forgetting to give both parts of the chromosome number explanation (N22 Q3c needs BOTH "two cells fuse" AND "to give 46")
- Using "gene" when the mark scheme requires "allele" (especially in genetics questions about dominant/recessive)

## Revision priorities

1. Four menstrual cycle hormones: name, source, and function of each — practise matching them to graph curves
2. Mitosis vs meiosis: 2 cells vs 4 cells; diploid vs haploid; identical vs varied — use the Venn format
3. Fertilisation → genetic variation: three mechanisms — (1) meiosis produces unique gametes, (2) two parents combine, (3) any gamete can fertilise any other
4. Why sex cells have 23 chromosomes: must give the TWO-PART answer
5. Puberty changes: know which are male-only, female-only, and both
6. Genetics: practise Punnett squares for monohybrid crosses; know how to calculate probabilities as percentages, fractions, and ratios
7. Sex determination: XX/XY; egg always gives X; sperm determines sex
