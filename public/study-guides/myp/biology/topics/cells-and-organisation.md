# Cells and Organisation

---

## 1. Key Concepts

### Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

| Feature | Prokaryotic | Eukaryotic |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Nucleus | No — DNA is free in cytoplasm (nucleoid region) | Yes — membrane-bound nucleus |
| Size | Smaller (~1–10 µm) | Larger (~10–100 µm) |
| DNA | Circular, no histones | Linear chromosomes with histones |
| Membrane-bound organelles | No | Yes (mitochondria, ER, Golgi, etc.) |
| Ribosomes | Yes — 70S (smaller) | Yes — 80S (larger) |
| Cell wall | Yes — peptidoglycan (in bacteria) | Sometimes (cellulose in plants; chitin in fungi) |
| Examples | Bacteria, Archaea | Animals, plants, fungi, protists |

**Mark scheme key phrase:** Prokaryotic cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus / do not have membrane-bound organelles — do NOT say "they have no nucleus" without the qualifier "membrane-bound."

### Organelles and Functions

| Organelle | Function | Mark scheme notes |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| Nucleus | Contains DNA; controls cell activities; site of transcription | Do not accept "brain of the cell" |
| Mitochondria | Site of aerobic respiration; produces ATP | Do NOT accept "stores energy" or "source of energy" (M19) |
| Chloroplast | Site of photosynthesis; contains chlorophyll | Must mention light energy conversion |
| Ribosome | Site of protein synthesis | Found in all cells (prokaryotic + eukaryotic) |
| Cell membrane | Controls what enters/exits the cell (selective permeability) | |
| Cell wall (plant) | Provides structural support; prevents cell from bursting | Made of cellulose |
| Vacuole (plant) | Stores water, salts, sugars; maintains turgor pressure | |
| Endoplasmic reticulum (rough) | Transports proteins made by ribosomes | "Rough" because ribosomes are attached |
| Golgi apparatus | Modifies and packages proteins for secretion | |

**N24 Q1a (organelle functions):** Common mark scheme — mitochondria = "site of aerobic respiration / produces ATP." Do not accept "energy production" without ATP.

### Cell Specialisation

Cells become specialised (differentiated) during development — they develop specific structures to carry out specific functions.

| Cell type | Adaptations | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| Sperm cell | Long tail (flagellum) for movement; many mitochondria; acrosome containing enzymes | Fertilise egg cell |
| Red blood cell | Biconcave shape (large SA); no nucleus (more space for haemoglobin); flexible | Transport oxygen |
| Root hair cell | Long extension (large SA); no chloroplasts; many mitochondria | Absorb water and minerals by osmosis/active transport |
| Guard cell | Contains chloroplasts; changes shape to open/close stomata | Control gas exchange and water loss |
| Nerve cell (neuron) | Long axon; many dendrites | Transmit electrical signals |

**N24 Q1c (sperm cell):** Sperm are haploid — produced by meiosis — contains 23 chromosomes in humans. Mark schemes require "haploid" explicitly.

### Levels of Organisation

Ordered from smallest to largest:

**Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ System → Organism → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biosphere**

| Level | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Cell | Basic unit of life | Muscle cell |
| Tissue | Group of similar cells with the same function | Muscle tissue |
| Organ | Group of tissues working together | Heart |
| Organ system | Group of organs working together | Circulatory system |
| Organism | Complete living thing | Human |
| Population | Group of same species in an area | All lions in the Serengeti |
| Community | All populations in an area | All organisms in a forest |
| Ecosystem | Community + its abiotic environment | Tropical rainforest |
| Biosphere | All ecosystems on Earth | Earth |

**M19 Q1a:** Drag-and-drop — put "liver cell → liver tissue → liver → digestive system → organism" in order. A common error is confusing tissue and organ.

### SA:V Ratio (Surface Area to Volume Ratio)

**Formula:** SA:V = Surface Area ÷ Volume

As a cell grows larger, volume increases faster than surface area → SA:V ratio decreases → diffusion becomes less efficient.

**Why it matters:**
- Small cells have a high SA:V → substances diffuse in/out quickly.
- Large organisms need specialised exchange surfaces (e.g. lungs, gills, villi) to compensate for low SA:V.

**M24 Q1e (worked example with cubes):**

| Cube side (mm) | SA (mm²) | Volume (mm³) | SA:V ratio |
|---------------|----------|--------------|------------|
| 1 | 6 | 1 | 6:1 |
| 2 | 24 | 8 | 3:1 |
| 3 | 54 | 27 | 2:1 |
| 4 | 96 | 64 | 1.5:1 |

**Mark scheme note (M24):** Do NOT accept the ratio alone without explanation. Must link decreasing SA:V to slower diffusion/reduced efficiency of gas exchange.

**Consequences of low SA:V:**
- Multicellular organisms develop exchange surfaces (lungs, gills, villi in small intestine).
- Organisms in hot climates have large ears (elephants) — large SA for heat loss.
- Organisms in cold climates have compact shapes — low SA to conserve heat.

### Cell Division

#### Mitosis
- Produces **2 genetically identical** daughter cells.
- Products are **diploid** (same chromosome number as parent cell).
- Purpose: growth, repair, asexual reproduction.
- Phases: Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase (PMAT).

#### Meiosis
- Produces **4 genetically different** daughter cells.
- Products are **haploid** (half the chromosome number).
- Purpose: production of gametes (sex cells — sperm and eggs).
- Involves **two divisions** (meiosis I and II).
- Introduces genetic variation through **crossing over** and **independent assortment**.

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Number of divisions | 1 | 2 |
| Daughter cells | 2 | 4 |
| Chromosome number | Diploid (2n) | Haploid (n) |
| Genetic variation | No (clones) | Yes |
| Purpose | Growth/repair | Gamete production |

**N21 Q2:** Venn diagram separating mitosis and meiosis features — "produces haploid cells" belongs only to meiosis; "produces 2 daughter cells" belongs only to mitosis; "involves DNA replication" belongs in the shared (middle) section.

**N24 Q1c:** Meiosis produces haploid sperm. After fertilisation, diploid zygote is restored. Mark schemes require use of terms "haploid" and "diploid."

### Microscopy

- **Light microscope:** Uses light; max magnification ~1000×; can view living cells; resolution ~200 nm.
- **Electron microscope:** Uses electrons; max magnification ~500,000×; cannot view living cells; resolution ~0.2 nm.
- Can only reveal organelles like mitochondria and ribosomes with electron microscopes.

**Magnification formula:**
> Magnification = Image size ÷ Actual size

## 3. Mark Scheme Insights

- **Mitochondria function:** MUST include "ATP" or "aerobic respiration." "Energy production" alone — **NOT accepted** (M19).
- **Prokaryote definition:** Must say "no membrane-bound nucleus" — NOT just "no nucleus."
- **SA:V explanation:** Must link ratio decrease to reduced diffusion rate. Ratio alone — **NOT accepted** (M24).
- **Meiosis outputs:** Must say "haploid" — "half the chromosomes" without the term may only get partial credit.
- **Cell specialisation:** Link structure to function explicitly — e.g. "long tail for swimming" is only half a mark; need to add "allows sperm to reach the egg."

## 4. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| "Mitochondria stores energy" | Mitochondria is the site of aerobic respiration / produces ATP |
| "Prokaryotes have no nucleus" | Say "no membrane-bound nucleus" |
| Confusing mitosis (2 cells) with meiosis (4 cells) | Meiosis = 4 haploid cells; mitosis = 2 diploid cells |
| Forgetting SA:V significance | Always link to: as size increases, SA:V decreases, diffusion becomes less efficient |
| "Sperm has half DNA" | Say "sperm is haploid / contains n chromosomes" |
| Confusing tissue and organ | Tissue = similar cells; organ = multiple tissues working together |
