# Enzymes

---

## 1. Key Concepts

### What is an enzyme?

**Definition:** A biological **catalyst** — a protein that speeds up chemical reactions in living organisms without being used up or permanently changed.

**Two points always needed for "Outline the importance of enzymes" (M23 Q3a, 2 marks):**
1. Speed up reactions / biological catalyst
2. Build up molecules (anabolic) **or** break down molecules (catabolic) — accept specific examples

**Key properties:**
- Proteins — made of amino acids
- Specific — each enzyme works on one type of substrate only
- Reusable — not consumed in the reaction
- Affected by temperature and pH

---

### Lock-and-Key Model

- Each enzyme has an **active site** — a specific region with a complementary shape to its substrate
- The **substrate** binds to the active site → forms an **enzyme-substrate complex** → reaction occurs → **products** released → enzyme unchanged and ready to reuse
- Specificity: only the correct substrate fits the active site (like a key into a lock)

**Terminology the mark scheme explicitly awards marks for (M22 Q7b, N21 Q3c):**
> catalyse / catalyst / active site / substrate / product / lock-and-key / induced fit / catabolic

Always drop at least one of these terms into any enzyme answer — a dedicated terminology mark exists in multiple papers.

## 2. Effect of Temperature

| Temperature range | Effect on rate | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Below optimum | Rate increases | More kinetic energy → more frequent enzyme-substrate collisions |
| At optimum (~37°C humans / ~30°C plants) | Maximum rate | Most enzyme-substrate complexes forming per second |
| Above optimum | Rate drops sharply | Enzyme begins to **denature** |
| Well above optimum | Rate → zero | Full denaturation — permanent, irreversible |

**Chain of reasoning required for full marks (N21 Q3c, M25 Q5):**
```
High temperature
  → enzyme denatures
    → active site changes shape        ← must say this
      → substrate cannot bind
        → reaction rate falls to zero
          → irreversible / permanent
```

**Critical mark scheme note:** Never write "enzyme is killed / destroyed / dies" → **rejected** in all sessions (N21, M25).  
Must write: **active site changes shape**.

**Human body context (N21 Q3c — 5 marks):**
- Enzymes have an optimum temperature (~37°C)
- Too cold: rate slows (fewer collisions)
- Too hot: active site changes shape → substrate cannot bind → reaction stops
- Criterion D bonus mark: use any of `active site`, `denature`, `catalyze`, `lock and key`, `substrate`

## 3. Effect of pH

- Each enzyme has an **optimum pH** at which it works fastest
- Extreme pH (too acidic or too alkaline) → changes the shape of the active site → enzyme denatures → substrate cannot bind

**Named examples tested in past papers:**

| Enzyme | Optimum pH | Location |
|---|---|---|
| Pepsin | ~pH 2 | Stomach |
| Salivary amylase | ~pH 7 | Mouth |
| Trypsin | ~pH 8 | Small intestine |
| Alkaline phosphatase | ~pH 9–10 | Small intestine |

**M18 Q4 (enzyme location drag-and-drop):** Given a graph of % maximum activity vs pH for five enzymes, match each enzyme to its location in the digestive system using the pH optimum.

**M18 Q4d — "Why do the pH values chosen not give sufficient data?"**
- Accepted answer: the range tested (pH 1, 7, 14) skips the optimum pH for lactase (~pH 6–7) AND doesn't include enough intermediate values to identify the true optimum; the range should include more pH values around the optimum

---

## 4. Effect of Substrate Concentration

- As substrate concentration increases → more enzyme-substrate complexes form → rate increases
- Rate **plateaus** when all active sites are occupied — adding more substrate has no effect

**Exact mark scheme phrasing for the plateau (M18 Q5, M21 Q7):**
> "All (active sites of the) enzyme molecules are being used" / "all active sites are occupied"

**M18 Q5 — hypothesis evaluation:**
- Hypothesis: "if lactose concentration increases, time to colour change will be shorter"
- Mark scheme: hypothesis is **partially valid** — holds at low concentrations; does not hold at high concentrations because all active sites become occupied → rate plateaus → time no longer decreases

---

## 5. Effect of Enzyme Concentration

- More enzyme → more active sites available → faster rate (if substrate is not limiting)
- M18 Q5g: "change the concentration of the enzyme / change the volume of the enzyme solution (as this gives more active sites)"
- Do NOT accept: "change the enzyme" or "add water" unless explicitly linked to changing concentration

---

## 6. Enzyme Inhibitors

**Competitive inhibitor:**
- Same shape as substrate → fits into the active site → blocks substrate from binding
- Example: **statins** block the enzyme that converts substance A into cholesterol in the liver (N21 Q7b)
- Mark scheme: "(statins) block the enzyme / block the active site / prevent conversion of substance A into cholesterol"
- Do NOT accept: "reflects the active site" (N21 MS — explicitly rejected)

You do not need to know non-competitive inhibitors in detail for MYP.

## 8. Enzymes in Digestion

Enzymes break down large, insoluble food molecules into small, soluble ones that can be absorbed.

| Nutrient | Enzyme(s) | Products |
|---|---|---|
| Carbohydrates (starch) | Amylase | Sugars (maltose/glucose) |
| Proteins | Pepsin, trypsin, proteases | Amino acids |
| Fats (lipids) | Lipase | Fatty acids + glycerol |
| Lactose | Lactase | Glucose + galactose |

**M22 Q2b:** "State what is produced when carbohydrates are chemically digested" → **sugars / glucose / simple sugars**

**Why physical digestion matters for enzymes (M22 Q2a):**
- Chewing / churning increases surface area of food
- More surface area → more contact with enzymes → faster digestion
- Mark scheme: "allow enzymes to work faster / increase the rate of chemical digestion"

## 9. Criterion B — Designing an Enzyme Investigation

**Standard design context:** Effect of temperature (or pH, or substrate concentration) on enzyme activity using lactase + lactose + glucose test strips.

**IV options tested in past papers:**
- Temperature (M18 Q6) — most common
- pH (M18 Q4)
- Substrate (lactose) concentration (M18 Q5)
- Enzyme concentration

**DV:** Time for colour change of glucose test strip / rate of glucose production
- Do NOT accept "rate of reaction" alone — must specify what changes colour or is measured

**CVs accepted in mark schemes (M18 Q4c):**
- Amount of lactose / substrate
- Surface area of substrate
- Amount / concentration of enzyme
- Volume of water
- Temperature (if IV is pH)
- Type of enzyme

**Top-band hypothesis template (temperature as IV):**
> "If temperature is increased from 10°C to 60°C, then the rate of lactose breakdown will increase up to approximately 37°C then decrease, because enzymes have an optimum temperature; above this, the active site changes shape (denatures) and substrate can no longer bind."

## 11. Mark Scheme Rules & Common Traps

| Wrong answer | Correct answer | Source |
|---|---|---|
| "Enzyme is killed / destroyed / dies" | Active site **changes shape** → substrate cannot bind | N21, M25 |
| "Rate of reaction" as DV | Specify: "time for colour change (s)" or "rate of glucose production" | M18 |
| "Reflects the active site" (re: statins) | Statins **block** the active site | N21 |
| "Add water" to change enzyme concentration | "Change the concentration/volume of enzyme solution (to give more active sites)" | M18 |
| Plateau = "no more substrate" | Plateau = **all active sites are occupied** | M18, M21 |
| Only stating "speeds up reactions" for importance | Need both: catalyst AND build up/break down molecules | M23 |

## 12. Revision Priorities

1. Memorise the **lock-and-key model** — active site, substrate, enzyme-substrate complex, product released
2. Know the **denaturation chain**: high temp → active site changes shape → substrate cannot bind → rate → zero (irreversible)
3. Know the **pH optimum** for pepsin, amylase, trypsin — be ready for a drag-and-drop graph question
4. Know the **plateau explanation**: all active sites occupied — not "no more substrate"
5. Memorise all **named enzymes** and their substrates/products
6. Always include a **terminology mark word** in enzyme answers: active site / substrate / catalyse / lock-and-key
7. For Criterion B: DV must be a specific measurable outcome, not "rate of reaction" alone
