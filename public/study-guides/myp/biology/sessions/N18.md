### Question 1 — Diet and Exercise (11 marks)

**Context:** "This question is about diet and exercise. Lifestyle choices are important for health."

---

#### Q1a (2 marks) — Select

**Stimulus:** Images of rice, olive oil, milk, and a plate of fish.

**Question:** For each item, select the main nutrient this food contains. Options: Fats, Proteins, Carbohydrates.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- Rice: carbohydrates
- Olive oil: fats
- Milk: fat **or** protein
- Fish: protein

*Any two correct for each mark.* Criterion A.

---

#### Q1b (1 mark) — State

**Question:** State why protein is needed as part of a balanced diet.

**Mark scheme answer:** needed for growth **or** repair **or** structure **or** enzymes

Criterion A.

---

#### Q1c (2 marks) — Calculate

**Context:** Before going to school, a student eats a cheese sandwich and drinks a glass of orange juice. The cheese sandwich contained 10 g of protein which is equivalent to 20% of the recommended daily intake.

**Question:** Calculate the total mass of protein needed daily.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- 50 (g) — 1 mark
- grams / g — 1 mark (unit mark, Criterion D)

*Working: 10 g = 20%, so 100% = 10 × 5 = 50 g*

---

#### Q1d (4 marks) — Analyse

**Stimulus:** Nutrition facts tables for cheese sandwich (per 100 g) and orange juice (per 240 cm³):

| Nutrient | Cheese sandwich (Daily % per 100g) | Orange juice (Daily % per 240 cm³) |
|---|---|---|
| Fat | 18 | 0 |
| Carbohydrates | 9 | 9 |
| Protein | 20 | 0 |
| Calcium | 10 | 2 |
| Vitamin C | 0 | 120 |

**Question:** Analyse the information in the tables to discuss whether or not the breakfast is part of a balanced diet.

**Mark scheme answers (4 marks, any four):**
- (yes) because it contains all of the nutrient groups in the table
- all of the daily vitamin C is included
- calcium is low so should be supplemented
- (so) you would need to know what else was eaten during the day

Criterion A.

---

#### Q1e (2 marks) — Outline

**Context:** Obesity is a condition where a person's body mass is too high.

**Question:** Outline **two** causes of obesity.

**Mark scheme answers — any two from the list:**
- an unbalanced diet can lead to a high energy intake **or** low energy usage if activity levels are low
- energy intake is higher than energy used
- genetic factors **or** low metabolic rate

Criterion A.

### Question 2 — Disease and Immunity (12 marks)

**Context:** "Some interactions between organisms are harmful. Yearly, thousands of people die because of infection with the bacterium *Vibrio cholerae*, which causes the disease cholera."

---

#### Q2a (1 mark) — State

**Context:** *Vibrio cholerae* is a pathogen.

**Question:** State the meaning of the term *pathogen*.

**Mark scheme answer:** an organism **or** virus that causes a disease **or** an infection

WTTE (Words To That Effect). Criterion A.

---

#### Q2b (2 marks) — List

**Question:** List **two** life processes that a bacterium has in common with other living organisms.

**Mark scheme answers — any two from:**
- movement
- respiration
- response to stimulus
- growth
- reproduction
- excretion
- digestion or nutrition

WTTE. Criterion A.

---

#### Q2c (2 marks) — Suggest

**Context:** *Vibrio cholerae* is transmitted through drinking contaminated water.

**Question:** Suggest why cholera outbreaks often happen after natural disasters, such as hurricanes or floods.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- drinking water supply chains **or** sewage facilities are broken (1 mark)
- Any additional reasonable point, e.g.:
  - people do not have access to bottled or clean water
  - people do not have access to antibiotics/cures/medical care

Criterion D.

---

#### Q2d (2 marks) — Outline

**Context:** The human body has natural defences which protect it from infection.

**Question:** Outline how the skin and white blood cells protect the body from infection.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- Skin: physical barrier **or** pH **or** oils **or** sweat (1 mark)
- White blood cells: destroy/deactivate/eat pathogen **or** produce antibodies (in the bloodstream) (1 mark)

Criterion A.

---

#### Q2e (5 marks) — Explain

**Context:** Vaccinations have been developed against many deadly diseases such as measles and polio. Vaccines work with the body's own immune system.

**Question:** Explain how vaccinations lead to immunity.

**Mark scheme answers:**

**Basic explanation (1 mark):**
- you do not get the (symptoms of) disease **or** subsequent infection will lead to quicker response

**Additional points (up to 4 more marks):**
- vaccine is a weakened/inactive form of the pathogen **or** parts of the pathogen
- vaccine triggers the specific white blood cells/lymphocytes
- immune system **or** white blood cells/lymphocytes produce specific antibodies
- memory cells are present **or** the selection of specific white blood cells/lymphocytes has already been done

Total 5 marks. Criterion A.

### Question 3 — Leech Phototaxis Investigation (14 marks)

**Context:** "Plants and animals can both respond to a stimulus in their environment. When a plant responds to a stimulus, it is called a tropism and when an animal responds to a stimulus, it is called a taxis. A group of students had just returned from a trip to a stream and had observed leeches hiding under rocks or in the muddy water. The students wanted to learn more about leech behaviour. They knew that leeches did not have true eyes, but they could detect light and dark and movement. The students decided to investigate how leeches responded to light. This would be an example of a phototaxis."

**Stimulus:** Animation/image showing a tank split into light and dark halves with a lamp illuminating one side. A timer shows 06:41. Leeches are visible in the water.

---

#### Q3a (1 mark) — Select

- Nervous
- Endocrine
- Respiratory
- Digestive

**Mark scheme answer:** nervous

Criterion A.

**Mark scheme answers:**

  - temperature
  - movement around leeches

Criterion B.

---

### Question 8 — Biofortified Crops Extended Response (14 marks)

**Context (extensive stimulus provided):**
- Getting the right nutrition is essential for health and well-being.
- Food scarcity and poor nutrition are global problems that affect societies worldwide.
- Changes in the environment are causing more extreme weather leading to severe floods and droughts.
- Biofortified crops are genetically modified to contain additional nutritional value.
- The most common consequence of vitamin A deficiency is blindness; other conditions include night blindness, weakened immune function, cancer, and birth defects.
- Golden rice is a genetically modified form of rice which contains beta-carotene in its grains.
- Beta-carotene is needed by the human body to produce vitamin A.
- Non-modified rice grains do not contain beta-carotene.
- In 2009, results of a clinical trial of golden rice carried out with adult volunteers found that beta-carotene from golden rice is effectively converted to vitamin A in humans.
- As of March 2018, golden rice has not yet been grown commercially.
- Vitamin A deficiency map shows: Mild/Moderate/Severe/No data across countries worldwide.
- Some groups are against introduction of golden rice: they claim people will eat it without having a choice.
- Another concern is possibility of cross-pollinating with non-modified rice.
- Research continues in biofortified field of fortified foods; in 2016 World Food Prize went to biofortified sweet potatoes.

**Question:** Using the information provided in this question and your wider MYP knowledge, **discuss** and **evaluate** the possible implications of introducing biofortified food crops. In your answer, you should include:
- health impacts of introducing a new biofortified food on an individual
- environmental impacts of introducing biofortified food crops in areas where the species has never been cultivated before
- ethical impacts
- economic impacts for the local community
- a concluding recommendation based on the arguments you have discussed

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Description |
|---|---|
| **WTTE** | "Words To That Effect" — accept synonyms and paraphrases, not just verbatim answers |
| **or / OR** | Multiple acceptable alternatives; any one acceptable |
| **any two / any four** | Choose from a bullet list; order does not matter |
| **Criterion A** | Knowledge and Understanding — factual recall and application |
| **Criterion B** | Inquiring and Designing — variables, hypotheses, experimental design |
| **Criterion C** | Processing and Evaluating — data handling, limitations, improvements, validity |
| **Criterion D** | Reflecting on the Impacts of Science — scientific language, communication, societal impact |
| **Linked justification** | For multi-part answers (e.g. 3g), the second mark requires the justification to explicitly connect to the suggested improvement |
| **Markband rubrics (Q5, Q8)** | Holistic scoring across named strands; higher bands require multiple points AND reasoning/examples |
| **Unit marks** | Q1c awards a separate mark for the correct unit (g); axis labels in Q4d require units |
| **First mark / Second mark** | In Q7e, partial credit given for any two items in the correct sequence; full credit only for all correct |
| **Measurable prediction** | Q3d second mark specifically requires a measurable/quantifiable prediction, not just direction |
| **Accept ORA** | "Or Reverse Argument" — accept the logically opposite valid answer |
