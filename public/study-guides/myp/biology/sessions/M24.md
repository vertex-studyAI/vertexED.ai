### Question 1 (11 marks) — Characteristics of Living Things / Adaptations / Cell Biology

**Stimulus:** Thermal image of an elephant (temperature range 14°C–34°C) showing heat distribution. Images of maple leaves (broad, large) and cactus spines (modified leaves, small surface area). Table of cube surface area, volume, and SA:V ratios (1×1×1 → SA 6, V 1, ratio 6; 3×3×3 → SA 54, V 27, ratio 2; 5×5×5 → SA 150, V 125, ratio 1.2).

---

#### 1a — Select (2 marks) | Criterion A
**Question:** Select the function described by each definition below.
- Production of offspring → **Reproduction**
- Permanent increase in size → **Growth**
- Reaction to an internal or external stimulus → **Response**

**Mark scheme:**
- One correct = 1 mark; all three correct = 2 marks.

---

#### 1b — Outline (2 marks) | Criterion A
**Question:** Outline how an elephant's ears help to maintain its body temperature.

**Mark scheme:**
- One reasonable feature [max 1]: large size/surface area; can flap ears; thin; increased blood flow/can vasodilate
- Link to heat loss [max 1]: large area for heat loss or evaporative cooling; can fan itself/create a breeze; smaller distance for heat exchange; increased heat exchange as blood carries heat from body to ears
- **Do NOT accept:** "to balance body temperature"

---

#### 1c — Outline (2 marks) | Criterion A
**Question:** Outline one possible advantage of maple leaves having a large surface area.

**Mark scheme:**
- Feature: larger surface area means more light can be absorbed **or** more chlorophyll present **or** more stomata may be present [max 1]
- Justification: more photosynthesis/glucose produced; more transpiration; more gas exchange [max 1]
- **Allow** absorbance of CO₂; **ignore** reference to O₂

---

#### 1d — Outline (2 marks) | Criterion A
**Question:** Outline one possible advantage of cactus leaves having a small surface area.

**Mark scheme:**
- Feature: smaller surface area minimises water loss **or** cacti can survive in areas with limited water [max 1]
- Justification: due to less evaporation or transpiration [max 1]
- **Do NOT accept:** protection against being eaten

---

#### 1e — Explain (3 marks) | Criterion A
**Question:** Explain why having a high surface area to volume ratio is beneficial to a cell.

**Mark scheme:**
- Large surface area allows for more efficient exchange of (named) materials **or** heat [mp1]
- Small volume means fewer metabolic processes **or** less material required **or** shorter distance for materials to travel [mp2]
- High SA to V ratio allows exchange rates to meet metabolic needs [mp3]
- **Do NOT** award mp1 when the ratio is merely stated (no explanation)

### Question 5 (10 marks) — Helicopter Seed / Samara Investigation

Data: 0.50 m → 0.56 s, 0.67 s; 0.75 m → 1.15 s, 1.07 s; 2.00 m → 2.16 s, 2.38 s.

**Mark scheme:**

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Detail |
|---|---|
| WTTE | "Words to that effect" — accept equivalent phrasing, not just exact wording |
| ORA | "Or reverse argument" — accept the logically reversed statement |
| Do NOT accept ratio alone | For SA:V questions, stating the ratio is insufficient — must explain significance |
| Mp2 gains mp1 | When a higher-level mark point is achieved it implies the lower one |
| Command term precision | "Outline" = brief account; "Explain" = give reason; "Justify" = provide evidence/reasoning; "Discuss" = give balanced account; "Evaluate" = make a judgement |
| Criterion D extended responses | Marked by markband rubric, not point-by-point; require structure (step → justification → appraisal) |
| Design question (Q6) | Six separate assessment rows, each marked 1–4 or 1 mark; total 16 marks; all variables must be qualified |
| Unit requirement | Unit of measurement matters for calculations (Q3b, Q3c) — circumference must be in cm, not mm or m |
| Specific examples required | For ecosystem impacts, named examples score higher; generic answers score lower |
| No credit for protection from predation | Q1d cactus advantage — do not accept protection against being eaten |
| Sub-species vs type | Q2f — accept sub-species or ecotype; do not accept "type" |
| No linear/proportional | Q4f growth rate — do not accept "linear" or "proportional" to describe sycamore growth |
| Safety comment must be specific | Q6 (6.S) — only accept safety concerns genuinely relevant to this investigation, not generic lab safety |
