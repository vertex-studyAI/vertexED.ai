**Mark scheme answer:** Mammal
**Criterion:** A

### Question 7 (12 marks) — Health / Atherosclerosis & Interventions

**Stimulus:** Changes in human lifestyles (diet, activity) have led to increased levels of cardiovascular diseases. Cardiovascular disease describes diseases of the heart and blood vessels — accounts for 31% of global deaths annually. Advances in medicine have reduced the death rate from cardiovascular disease.

Cholesterol: found in all body cells — important in cell membranes and as a precursor to hormones. Body makes the cholesterol it needs but it is also found in some foods (eggs, milk, meat, cheese). Too much cholesterol in the body leads to atherosclerosis, e.g., coronary heart disease. Most common cause of high cholesterol: unhealthy diet (salty, sugary, processed foods), physical inactivity, and smoking. Cholesterol levels also rise naturally with age. Cholesterol levels can be easily monitored with simple blood tests. High levels managed by adopting a healthier lifestyle or medical and surgical interventions. Detecting and treating high cholesterol early significantly reduces risk of further complications.

**Surgical intervention — angioplasty:** A tube called a stent is placed into an artery to keep it open. (Image shows blocked artery before and after stent placement.)

**Medical intervention — statins:** Enzymes catalyse the conversion of substance A into cholesterol in the liver. Statins block the action of these enzymes. Statins are a form of medication commonly taken in tablet form.

#### Q7a (3 marks) — Suggest
> Suggest how atherosclerosis may lead to an increased chance of coronary heart disease.

**MS answers (WTTE):**
- fatty (artery) plaque/narrowing of the artery is obstructed / less blood / tissue reaches the heart muscle / tissue at making blood flow more difficult
- inflating the balloon (inside a stent) in the artery or squeezed the plaque against the artery walls
- allowing more blood to flow or making it easier for blood to flow

*Note: the heart (muscle) does not need to pump as hard (to supply the same volume of blood).*
**Criterion:** D

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

1. **ORA (Own Response Accepted):** Used where later questions build on student's earlier answers (e.g., Q3b depends on Q3a). Examiners mark consistently with whatever systems the student named.

2. **WTTE (Words To That Effect):** Correct biological meaning accepted even if exact wording differs. Used widely in Q3c, Q7a–c.

3. **Do NOT accept lists:** Several answers explicitly prohibit vague or incomplete responses:
   - Q1b: Do not accept a description of pollen moving alone
   - Q1c: Do not accept "tree" alone
   - Q3c: Do not accept "die" for enzyme denaturation
   - Q5a: Do not accept references to immunity

4. **Markband rubrics (Questions 6 and 8):** Extended response questions use multi-strand rubrics. Each strand is marked 1–4 independently then combined. Maximum marks are 17 (Q6, Criterion B) and 15 (Q8, Criterion D).

5. **Scientific terminology bonus (Q3c):** Criterion D mark awarded for correct use of any of: active site, denature, catalyze, lock and key, substrate.

6. **Measurement tolerance:** Q4g and Q5b both allow ±1 mm and ±0.1 percentage points respectively, with partial credit for getting some but not all values correct.

7. **Criterion distribution:**
   - Criterion A (Knowing & Understanding): Q1, Q2, Q3a–b, Q4a, Q5a
   - Criterion B (Inquiring & Designing): Q4d–f, Q6
   - Criterion C (Processing & Evaluating): Q4b–c, Q4g–h, Q5b–e
   - Criterion D (Reflecting on Impacts): Q3c (partial), Q7, Q8
