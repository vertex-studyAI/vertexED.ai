### Question 1 — Water and Transpiration (7 marks)

**Stimulus / Context:**
> Water is needed to sustain plant life. Water supports many different functions within a plant. Plants have evolved many different adaptations to collect and conserve water depending upon their environment.

#### Q1a — Select (1 mark) — Criterion A

**Question:** Select the correct diagram showing the movement of water through a plant (transpiration). Diagrams A, B, C and D shown.

**Mark scheme answer:** Diagram A (water taken up by roots, moves up stem, exits via leaves).

---

#### Q1b — State (1 mark) — Criterion A

**Context:** Cells filled with enough water are turgid; if a cell has lost too much water it may become flaccid or plasmolysed (hypotonic / isotonic / hypertonic diagrams shown).

**Question:** State the name of the structure in the cell that prevents it from bursting when it is turgid.

**Mark scheme answer:** Cell wall

---

#### Q1c — Suggest and justify (1 mark) — Criterion A

**Question:** Suggest and justify a possible effect of plasmolysed cells on the plant's structure.

**Mark scheme answer (WTTE — Worth The Thought Expressed):**
- The plant will no longer be able to stand upright
- The plant will wilt or go floppy

---

#### Q1d — Explain (4 marks) — Criterion A

**Context:** Marram grass grows in dry and windy conditions. The image shows cross-section of the leaf with hairy surface, stomata on inner/sunken surface.

**Question:** The image shows that the leaves of marram grass are curled inward. The outer surface of the leaf is quite thick and the inner surface is folded so that stomata are sunken. They are also surrounded by hairs. Explain how one adaptation of the marram leaf reduces water loss by transpiration. You should include scientific knowledge and understanding in your answer.

**Mark scheme answers — either pathway:**

*Pathway 1 (curled/sunken stomata/hairs):*
- The leaves are curled / the stomata are sunken / there are hairs surrounding the stomata
- This allows transpired water to become trapped in enclosed spaces
- Humidity increases
- So evaporation or transpiration is reduced in humid/moist conditions

*Pathway 2 (waxy cuticle):*
- Leaf has a waxy cuticle
- Leaf surface is impermeable to water
- This reduces area of leaf where water can be lost
- Prevents evaporation or transpiration through that surface

**Key MS note:** Either structural adaptation pathway is valid; the answer must chain structure → mechanism → effect.

### Question 2 — Tropisms (16 marks)

**Stimulus / Context:**
> Living organisms have the ability to respond to a stimulus. When a plant responds to a stimulus, it is called a tropism. There are many examples of tropisms in plants — for example, gravity or touch may act as a stimulus and cause a response in plants. If the tropism is positive, the stimulus causes growth of the plant toward the stimulus; if negative, growth away. In this example, the growth of a plant is responding to touch.

*(Image shows a climbing plant shoot coiling around a support — thigmotropism.)*

---

#### Q2a — State (2 marks) — Criterion A

**Question:** State two stimuli, other than gravity or touch, that cause tropisms in plants.

**Mark scheme answers — any two from:**
- Light / sunlight
- Water
- Heat / high temperature
- Nutrients
- Pheromones

---

#### Q2b — Suggest (4 marks) — Criterion A

**Context:** A plant shows both positive and negative tropisms (image of plant with shoot growing up and roots growing down).

**Question:** Suggest how the growth of a plant shows both positive and negative tropisms towards gravity.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Positive tropism shown by stem growing upwards
- So that the leaves are exposed to light
- Negative tropism shown by roots growing downward
- So that roots can absorb water/nutrients **or** a stable root structure is formed

---

#### Q2c — Suggest (3 marks) — Criterion A

**Context:** *Mimosa pudica* is a low-growing plant that responds to touch by closing its leaves.

**Question:** Suggest how this type of tropism might improve chances of survival for this plant.

**Mark scheme answers — any three reasonable suggestions, for example:**
- Touch causes leaves to close so leaves cannot be eaten
- Gives wilted, unappealing appearance
- Predators are confused as food disappears
- (Hence) improved chance of survival with more photosynthetic tissue

---

#### Q2d — Describe (3 marks) — Criterion C

**Context:** In 1880, Charles Darwin and his son Francis Darwin carried out a famous experiment on plant tropism. Seedlings set up in conditions: Control / Tip removed / Tip covered by opaque cap / Tip covered by transparent cap / Base covered by opaque sheet. Light source from one side. Results showed: control bends toward light; tip removed — no bending; opaque cap on tip — no bending; transparent cap — bends toward light; opaque cap on base — bends toward light.

**Question:** Describe what the results in the picture indicate about the response of plants to light.

**Mark scheme answer (WTTE; ORA — or reverse argument):**
- The stalk or plant will bend or move toward the light
- Only if the tip is exposed to the light

---

#### Q2e — Justify (4 marks) — Criterion C

**Context:** Other researchers separated two plant tips with a thin sheet of mica (non-permeable). In one set-up mica was on the shaded side; in the other, on the lit side.

**Question:** Based on these results, the researchers concluded that the response was the result of the movement of a substance from the tip of the plant toward the base on the shaded side of the stalk. Use scientific knowledge and understanding to justify how these results support the conclusion.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- On the shaded side, the mica blocked the substance moving down from the tip
- (And therefore) the plant did not bend
- When the mica was on the lit side, the bending was not affected
- (So) the plant grew toward the light

### Question 5 — Osmosis and Temperature (Potato Investigation) (21 marks)

**Stimulus / Context:**
> Osmosis is the movement of water molecules across a semi-permeable membrane, to maintain equilibrium from a region of low concentration of dissolved substances to a region of high concentration of dissolved substances. The movement of water usually occurs until equilibrium is reached.

> The students wanted to investigate whether the rate of osmosis was related to temperature. The students thought that as more water molecules moved into the tube, the mass of the tube would increase, so they decided to use the mass of the tubes to determine the rate of osmosis. They planned to use 10 cm³ of starch solution in the tubing and to measure the mass change after 10 minutes. The start of the experiment is shown below.

*(Setup: dialysis tubes in beakers at different temperatures — 5°C, 10°C, 15°C, 20°C, 35°C.)*

---

#### Q5a — State (1 mark) — Criterion B

**Question:** State the problem being studied in this investigation.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Whether temperature affects the rate of movement across a membrane
- (Measurement is made for a fixed time period)

---

#### Q5b — Outline and formulate (3 marks) — Criterion B

**Question:** Outline how the rate of osmosis is determined and formulate the unit for the rate of osmosis.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Change in mass over a fixed time period used to determine rate
- g min⁻¹ **or** g s⁻¹
- Accept g/min or g/s

---

#### Q5c — Calculate (2 marks) — Criterion C

**Question:** Complete the table by calculating the missing value. (Negligible: very small and not important.)

Data table: Temperature / °C | Average initial mass / g | Average final mass / g | Increase in mass after 10 min / g | Rate of osmosis (unit formulated in part b)

Missing value at 15°C: rate of osmosis = 1.01 / 10 = **0.10(1)** g min⁻¹

**Mark scheme answer:** 0.10(1) — accept incorrect precision for first mark; correctly stated as 0.10 with correct sig figs for full marks.

---

#### Q5d — Present (4 marks) — Criterion C

**Question:** Present the data from the table in a graph.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Increments evenly spaced
- Y-axis scale appropriate to give good visual differentiation of data trend
- Two points plotted
- All points plotted correctly (ECF from part c)
- Ignore point (5,0) if plotted

---

#### Q5e — Compare and contrast (4 marks) — Criterion C

**Context:** After conducting their investigation, the students researched other investigations to see if they matched similar conclusions. They found a similar graph from a local university. The university graph shows rate of osmosis increasing steeply to ~35°C then plateauing/constant above 35°C. Student graph only goes to 35°C.

**Question:** Scroll back to your graph in part (d) and compare and contrast the trend in the students' data in part (d) with the graph of the university data.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Both graphs show similar trend until 35°C
- Both graphs increase at a similar rate **or** rate of increase slows at a similar rate
- The university graph shows a plateau **or** reaches a constant value above 35°C
- The student graph has no data above 35°C

---

#### Q5f — Explain (4 marks) — Criterion C

**Question:** Explain one scientific reason for one trend shown in the university data.

**Mark scheme answers — either pathway:**

*Pathway 1 (increasing rate below 35°C):*
- As temperature increases, (kinetic) energy increases
- Particles move more quickly
- So the rate of movement across the membrane increases
- Term "kinetic energy" used correctly

*Pathway 2 (plateau above 35°C):*
- At a temperature above 35°C, the movement of water is equilibrium is reached (for this system)
- So the rate of (net) movement becomes constant
- Because particles are moving in both directions at the same rate
- Term "equilibrium" **or** "osmotic pressure" used correctly

**Key MS note:** Accept any other correctly used terminology associated with osmosis e.g. isotonic etc.

---

#### Q5g — Outline (1 mark) — Criterion C

**Question:** Outline whether the students' method is valid based on comparison of their data with the university data.

**Mark scheme answer:**
- Valid because the trends match (below 35°C) **or** valid because the data/results were similar
- **or** Not valid because there are no measurements above 35°C

**Key MS note:** Do not accept "valid" or "not valid" alone unless a correct reason is given.

*Improvement, for example:*

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Description |
|---|---|
| WTTE | "Worth The Thought Expressed" — any reasonable answer conveying the same meaning is accepted |
| ORA | "Or Reverse Argument" — accept equivalent answers stated from the opposite perspective |
| ECF | "Error Carried Forward" — marks awarded in subsequent parts if method is correct even when applied to an earlier wrong answer |
| Criterion A | Knowledge and understanding — recall, select, state |
| Criterion B | Inquiry/experimental design — identifying variables, hypothesis, prediction, safety, method design |
| Criterion C | Processing and evaluating data — calculations, graphs, compare/contrast, validity, improvements |
| Criterion D | Reflecting on impacts — compare/contrast written responses, discuss/evaluate extended responses with markbands |
| Markband questions | Q3c, Q6b, Q7c, Q8 use level-descriptor rubrics (levels 1–4) rather than point-per-mark schemes |
| "Do not accept alone" | Answers that are too vague without a mechanism or reason are not credited (e.g. "DNA has genetic information" for Q3b) |
| Command term precision | "Suggest" requires a plausible idea; "Justify" requires a reason; "Explain" requires mechanism; "Outline" requires brief account; "Identify" requires naming |
