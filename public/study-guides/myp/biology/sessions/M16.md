## Question 1 — Living Cells: Exchange, Transport, and Coordination (10 marks)

**Stimulus:** Images showing four biological transport scenarios: (1) root hair cells absorbing water, (2) oxygen uptake in lungs into bloodstream, (3) white blood cell taking in a bacterium, (4) leaf cells releasing oxygen. Labels offered: Osmosis, Diffusion, Neither.

### Q1a (2 marks) — Select
**Select** the option describing the transport mechanism in each diagram.

- Root hair cells absorbing water → **Osmosis**
- Oxygen taken into bloodstream in lungs → **Diffusion**
- White blood cell taking in bacteria → **Neither**
- Oxygen given out by leaf cells → **Neither**

**MS answers:** osmosis – neither – diffusion – diffusion (two correct = 1 mark; all correct = 2 marks)

---

### Q1b (1 mark) — State
Oxygen is carried by red blood cells. **State** the name of the organ system which carries blood through the human body.

**MS answer:** circulatory system / blood system / transport system / cardio-vascular system
**MS note:** Do **not** accept "cardiac system" as it refers to the heart only.

---

### Q1c (2 marks) — Outline
Using scientific terms, **outline** why the uptake of oxygen from the lungs into the bloodstream does not require energy.

**MS answers (any 2):**
- There is a higher oxygen concentration in the air (than in the blood)
- Oxygen moves/diffuses / passive transport / passive movement from area of higher concentration to area of lower concentration
- **or** oxygen moves/diffuses down the (oxygen) concentration gradient

---

### Q1d (1 mark) — State
Besides oxygen, the blood also carries hormones. **State** the name of another system that transmits signals in the human body.

**MS answer:** nervous / nerve / neurological system

---

### Q1e (4 marks) — Select + Explain
**Select** one of the senses (sight / hearing / taste / smell / touch). **Explain** how this sense and the transmission of signals inside the human body leads to responses which help us to survive. You should refer to an example of a survival situation.

**MS answers:** Up to 4 marks:
- Suitable stimulus linked to sense selected
- Response linked to the stimulus
- **Any two additional valid points [2 max]**, for example:
  - Route of signal transmission e.g. receptor to CNS
  - Processing in brain or central nervous system
  - Hormonal response e.g. adrenalin
  - Signal from CNS to the effector

**MS notes:**
- Allow touch to include pain, pressure, heat and corresponding stimulus
- Accept brain in examples of reflexes which involve CNS; reflex alone is not enough to score this mark

## Question 2 — Cell Division: Mitosis and Meiosis (12 marks)

**Stimulus:** Circular diagram showing the five phases of mitosis: Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase → Cytokinesis.

### Q2a (1 mark) — State
**State** the name of the molecule that stores genetic information in living organisms.

**MS answer:** deoxyribonucleic acid / DNA

---

### Q2b (3 marks) — Outline
**Outline** how the events in anaphase are crucial for the formation of two identical cells.

**MS answers (3 points, WTTE):**
- Identical DNA molecules / genes / chromosomes / (sister) chromatids are separated
- (and) are moved to opposite poles of the cell
- Two separate genetically identical cells are formed

---

### Q2c (3 marks) — Outline
Mitosis leads to growth and repair; meiosis is required for sexual reproduction. **Outline three** differences between the products of mitosis and meiosis.

**MS answers** (answer must focus on **products**, not processes; any 3):
- Meiosis produces four cells **and** mitosis produces two cells
- Meiosis produces haploid/1n cells **and** mitosis produces diploid/2n cells
- **or** in meiosis the chromosome number gets halved, **and** in mitosis the chromosome number remains the same
- Meiosis produces genetically non-identical cells **and** mitosis produces genetically identical cells

**MS note:** Answer must focus on the products of meiosis and mitosis rather than the processes.

---

### Q2d (2 marks) — Describe
**Describe one** problem that can occur to the genetic molecule during meiosis.

**MS answers — either:**

Option 1 (mutation):
- mutation (which results in…)
- translocation **or** addition **or** deletion **or** loss of a part of a chromosome

Option 2 (non-disjunction):
- non-disjunction (which is caused by…)
- failure of homologous pairs to separate in anaphase **or** failure of sister chromatids to separate during anaphase
- **or** non-disjunction occurs / resulting in an extra chromosome or a reduced number of chromosomes

**MS note:** Accept incorrect references to changes in replication for the first mark. Ignore incorrect phase.

---

### Q2e (3 marks) — Explain (Criterion D)
**Explain** how **one** process that occurs in meiosis can lead to genetic differences in children.

**MS answers:**
- *First mark:* One correct use of the term "chromosome"
- *Second and third mark, either:*
  - Genes are exchanged between (homologous) chromosomes (because of) crossing over
  - **or** independent assortment **or** separation of (unlinked) genes (resulting from) independent separation of (homologous) chromosomes

**MS notes:** WTTE — technical terms not essential if meaning is clear for marks 2 and 3. Ignore incorrect phase.

## Question 6 — Evaluating a Biomass Investigation (14 marks)

**Stimulus — Hypothesis:** If a plant receives more water then the biomass of 20 plants of the same species and same type of squares will increase more than the plants with less water added.

**Overview of method:**
- Step 1: Find 20 plants of the same species; cut, weigh and record leaf samples
- Step 2: Put 10 plants in environment with dilute acid and temperature; weigh leaf samples
- Step 3: Place one leaf every other day and weigh 60 cm² of plants
- Step 4: After 3 weeks remove 1 leaf from each; weigh all and record mass

**Results:** Bar chart showing initial vs. final mean average biomass (g/m²) for two groups:
- Both groups showed increased biomass after 3 weeks
- Group with more water had a higher final biomass

### Q6a (1 mark — Criterion C) — State
**State** one trend in the data.

**MS answers (ORA):**
- Average mass increased in both groups after three weeks
- **or** biomass after three weeks increased more for plants that received more water
- **or** percentage increase is greater for plants that received more water

---

### Q6b (3 marks — Criterion C) — Explain
**Explain** one scientific reason for the difference in final and initial mean average biomass for the two groups.

**MS answers:**
- In both groups of samples/plants the average mass increased
- Because plants continued to grow/photosynthesise (over the three-week period)
- (which led to the) formation of more biomass/products of photosynthesis stored on leaves
- **or** with more water present photosynthesis could take place at a higher rate
- A higher rate of photosynthesis produced more glucose/sugars
- More glucose/sugars produced creates a higher biomass

---

### Q6c (1 mark — Criterion C) — Comment
**Comment** on the validity of the hypothesis based on the data.

**MS answers:**
- The data supports the hypothesis
- **or** the hypothesis refers to plants not samples so the data is inconclusive

**MS note:** Accept hypothesis is correct/valid only if linked to the data.

---

### Q6d (4 marks — Criterion C) — Describe
Based on the context and apparatus presented, **describe** one strength and one weakness related to the method's design.

**MS answers:**

*Strength of method, for example:*
- Any of the controls — same size leaves, temperature, soil, light; ten plants used for each volume of water; ten trials; different conditions gave measurable difference in outcome

*Description of strength, for example:*
- (so) data is reliable / less variation in data / average used to remove individual variation

*Weakness, for example:*
- Range of volumes of water / only two volumes used; two different leaves used; was plant damaged during leaf sampling; were leaves taken from same location of plant

*Description of weakness, for example:*
- Insufficient range of water data to see true trend; two data sets are not sufficient to plot a graph; if the thickness/composition of the leaves were not similar the biomass would change

---

### Q6e (4 marks — Criterion C) — Outline
**Outline** two improvements to this investigation. **Justify** your answers.

**MS answers (any two, 2 marks each):**
- Use a larger range of volumes
- Use similar sized leaf
- Use similar colour of leaf
- Use leaf from similar location

*Clearly linked explanation for each improvement, for example:*
- (Larger range of volume) sufficient relevant data for a graph / more accurate data
- (Similar leaves) better control — less variation in data / more precise data

---

### Q6f (1 mark — Criterion C) — State
Other than changing the temperature or light, **state** one extension to this method that would benefit this scientific investigation.

**MS answers:**
- Any reasonable modification to this investigation or change in the independent variable, for example:
  - Change humidity of surroundings
  - Change soil
  - Change water e.g. type, pH etc.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **WTTE** (Words To That Effect) | Accept answers using different wording if the scientific meaning is clear |
| **ORA** (Or Reverse Argument) | Accept the logically equivalent reverse argument |
| **ecf** (error carried forward) | Award marks in subsequent parts even if an earlier answer was wrong, provided the method is correct |
| **"either/or" answers** | Many questions accept two separate valid pathways — both awarded full marks |
| **Scientific term required** | Some marks explicitly require specific terminology (e.g., "enzyme" in Q4c, "chromosome" in Q2e) |
| **Do not accept** | Certain common incorrect answers explicitly excluded (e.g., "cardiac system" for Q1b; "food chain" for Q9a) |
| **Criterion A** | Knowledge and understanding — short-answer, factual recall |
| **Criterion B** | Inquiry — hypothesis, variables, experimental design |
| **Criterion C** | Processing — data tables, calculations, graphs, evaluation of data |
| **Criterion D** | Reflection — extended writing, scientific discussion, evaluation of implications |
| **Extended response markbands** | Q7 (16 marks) and Q10c (17 marks) use holistic markbands, not point-by-point marking |
