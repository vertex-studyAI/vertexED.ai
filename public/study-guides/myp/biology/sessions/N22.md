### Question 9 — Personalized Medicine (14 marks)

**Context passage provided:**
- Most standard drug combinations and doses are based on evidence gained from clinical trials — the drug and dose are effective for most patients with a specific disease.
- However, differences in genetic material mean that not all patients respond in the same way to standard treatments.
- In personalized medicine, DNA sequencing is used to determine which version of a disease a patient has, to predict how individual patients will respond to different drugs and doses.
- Personalized medicine has the potential to reduce incorrect treatment and save valuable resources; improvement of DNA sequencing methods will speed up the process and reduce the cost.
- The move from standard treatments to personalized medicine is a huge task.
- Decisions need to be made about how to manipulate, measure, or monitor an individual's genetic information: Who owns the sequencing data and who should have access?
- Should we use data to confirm diagnoses, or could we use it to look for potential issues before a person gets ill?

> **Discuss** and **evaluate** the use of personalized healthcare in the treatment of diseases. In your answer, you should include:
> - medical benefits of personalized healthcare for an individual and wider society
> - economic considerations in moving to personalized healthcare
> - ethical considerations
> - a concluding appraisal with justification

**Mark Scheme (level-based, markband rubric):**

| Criterion | 1 mark | 2 marks | 3 marks | 4 marks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Medical** | One statement of a medical benefit | Two statements of a medical benefit **or** one statement with further development | Two statements of a medical benefit, one with further development | Two statements of a medical benefit, both with further development |
| **Economic** | One statement of an economic consideration | Two statements of an economic consideration **or** one statement with further development | Two statements of economic consideration, one with further development | Two statements of economic consideration, both with further development |
| **Ethical** | One statement of an ethical consideration | Two statements of ethical consideration **or** one statement with further development | Two statements of ethical consideration, one with further development | Two statements of ethical consideration, both with further development |
| **Conclusion** | A simple conclusion is stated | A conclusion is stated with further development | — | — |

**Total: 14 marks. Criterion D.**

**Key content for a full-mark response:**
- Medical benefits: correct treatment reduces side effects; prevents incorrect treatments; benefits wider society by reducing healthcare costs from misdiagnosis; early detection possible
- Economic: DNA sequencing costs are falling; significant upfront investment required; could reduce long-term healthcare costs; inequality in access between countries/individuals
- Ethical: data ownership and privacy; consent for genetic information storage; insurance/employer discrimination risk; use of predictive data before illness manifests
- Conclusion: personalized medicine is beneficial but requires careful regulation of data use, investment in technology, and equitable access policies

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Detail |
|---|---|
| **WTTE** | "Words to that effect" — accept reasonable paraphrases; applies to Q1b, Q5b, Q7e and others |
| **CON (contradictory answer)** | A correct answer paired with a contradictory one scores 0 (e.g. Q2a: saying both carbohydrates and protein) |
| **No ecf (error carried forward)** | Q2c: first mark must be correct before second is awarded |
| **ORA** | "Or reverse argument" — accept opposite direction if internally consistent (Q4c hypothesis) |
| **Max marks within bullet lists** | Many sub-parts award from a list with a stated max (e.g. [max 2]) — do not double-award |
| **Level-based marking** | Q6 (Design) and Q9 (Evaluate): assessed via markband rubric across multiple criteria; not point-for-point |
| **Data reference required** | Q5d requires specific data values (0.10% CO₂ plateau point) to be cited |
| **Calculation: correct answer only** | Q4d: 261 — full 2 marks for correct rounded answer only; no working marks stated |
| **Criteria codes** | A = Knowledge/Understanding; B = Inquiry (planning); C = Inquiry (processing/evaluating); D = Thinking critically |
