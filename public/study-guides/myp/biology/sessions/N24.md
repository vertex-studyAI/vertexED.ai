## Question 1 — Cell Biology and Respiration (10 marks)

**Context / Stimulus:** Questions explore cell organelle functions, specialised blood cells (red and white), sperm cell structure (haploid nucleus labelled A), and differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.

---

### Q1a (2 marks) — Select / Drag

**Question:** For a system to be successful, every part of it must perform its specific function. An image of an animal cell is shown. Select the organelle responsible for each function.

| Function | Correct Organelle |
|---|---|
| Stores genetic material | Nucleus |
| Controls what goes in and out of the cell | Cell membrane |
| Makes proteins | Ribosome |

Two correct = 1 mark. All correct = 2 marks.

**MS notes:** Options given: Cell membrane, Cytoplasm, Mitochondria, Nucleus, Ribosome.

---

### Q1b (2 marks) — State

**Question:** Specialized cells such as red blood cells and white blood cells develop from stem cells found in bone marrow. State the function of these two types of cell.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- Red blood cell: Transports oxygen **or** CO₂
- White blood cell: Produces antibodies **or** immune system defence **or** fights infection **or** fights disease

**MS notes:** WTTE (words to that effect). Award 1 mark per correct function.

---

### Q1c (3 marks) — Describe

**Question:** Sperm cells have a high number of mitochondria to carry out cellular respiration and release the energy needed to move towards an egg. Use the image below to **describe** how structure A (the nucleus/head region) is specialized to conserve an organism's **chromosome number** when fertilizing an egg. You must use scientific terminology in your answer.

**Stimulus:** Labelled diagram of a sperm cell showing part A as the (haploid) nucleus/head region.

**Mark scheme answers:**
- Nucleus is haploid **or** contains 23 chromosomes **or** half the genetic material required
- When sperm fertilizes an egg, the zygote will be diploid **or** have 46 chromosomes **or** full set of chromosomes
- Any correct use of a term from the list: haploid, diploid, gamete, zygote

**MS notes:** Accept to avoid polyploidy. Awarded on criterion D.

---

### Q1d (3 marks) — Discuss

**Question:** Cells can carry out aerobic and anaerobic cellular respiration. **Discuss** the differences between the two types of cellular respiration in animal cells.

**Mark scheme answers (any 3):**
- Aerobic requires oxygen and anaerobic does not
- Aerobic releases more energy **or** ATP than anaerobic
- Accept any further point from the list [max 1]:
  - Aerobic produces water (and carbon dioxide) **and** anaerobic produces lactic acid
  - Aerobic respiration occurs in the mitochondria (and cytoplasm) **and** anaerobic respiration occurs in the cytoplasm

**MS notes:** Awarded on criterion A.

## Question 8 — Forest Regeneration Methods (16 marks)

**Context / Stimulus:** Forests can be regenerated for different purposes using a range of methods. Three main methods shown via interactive media:

1. **Natural regeneration** — healthiest trees identified and selected as seed trees before forest is cut down. Just before they release seeds, the rest of the trees are cut down. Care must be taken to ensure seed trees are not damaged by machinery. Soil prepared by adding nutrients. Weeds removed, herbicides applied. Site managed to optimize growth. After 1–2 years, seed trees are removed and growth of new trees is monitored. Takes ~9 years.

2. **Direct seeding** — landowner applies a known quantity of seeds directly to the land. Soil prepared by adding nutrients. Weeds removed, herbicides applied. Seeds stored below 2°C. Number of seeds needed varies by species and site conditions. Seeds treated to increase germination and survival rate. Different seeds planted at different times of year (Spring/Autumn). Large or inaccessible areas: seeds planted by airplane. Smaller plots: planted by hand. Takes ~8 years.

3. **Planting seedlings** — seedlings grown in tree nurseries and later planted at the site. Soil prepared by adding nutrients. Weeds removed, herbicides applied. Genetically improved seedlings can be selected. 1–2-year-old seedlings grow best if planted directly after harvesting. Transport to regeneration site must be in closed or refrigerated vehicle. Trees planted in rows with even spaces. Hand (2 people, 1,000–2,000 trees/day) or machine (2 people, 8,000–10,000 trees/day). Seedlings require more care than seeds, including regular watering. Takes ~5 years.

**Question:** Using information from this task and your wider MYP studies, **discuss** and **evaluate** the different methods for forest regeneration. In your answer, you must include:
- two reasons to regenerate forests
- a scientific explanation of why planning for regeneration is important
- a discussion of different environmental consequences for two methods
- a discussion of different economic considerations for two methods
- a concluding appraisal justifying your opinion of which is the best method of regeneration.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

1. **WTTE (Words To That Effect):** Applied throughout; synonyms and paraphrases accepted where meaning is clear.

2. **ECF (Error Carried Forward):** Explicitly used in Q4b/4c; if wrong mean calculated, use that value for the bar graph and award marks.

3. **Max lists:** Several questions cap the number of acceptable answers from a list (e.g., Q1d "accept any further point [max 1]", Q5b "accept two CVs [max 2]"). Students should not list more than required.

4. **Criterion codes:** Questions are awarded under criteria A (knowledge), B (scientific method/inquiry), C (processing data), or D (reflecting on implications). Q5 (method design) = B/C; Q8 (discuss/evaluate regeneration) = D.

5. **Holistic marking (Q6, Q8):** These questions use markbands rather than point-scoring. Award the band that best fits overall quality — do not total individual sub-points.

6. **"Do not accept" rules:**
   - Q2a: Do not accept "start to menstruate" or "start to produce sperm" (given in question stem)
   - Q3b: Only award mark 3 if another mark is awarded first
   - Q5d: Do not award mark 3 if mark 2 is not awarded
   - Q6: Do not accept oven/incubator as temperature control equipment

7. **Calculation questions (Q4b):** Show working is required; award 2 marks if correct answer (5.36) is seen, even if working is not shown.

8. **Graph questions (Q4c):** Axes must be labelled, intervals equal, bars within ± half a square of grid lines. ECF from calculated mean.

9. **Q8 structured essay:** Must address all five bullet points. The 16 marks are split 2+4+4+4+2 across the five sub-criteria. A response missing any section is capped accordingly.
