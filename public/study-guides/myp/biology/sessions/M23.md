## Question 2 — Active Food Packaging (8 marks)

### Stimulus
Active packaging changes conditions inside packaging to extend food shelf life. As bananas ripen they produce a gas that speeds ripening; active packaging contains a gas absorber (red dots) and a coating to prevent microbial growth (blue dots). Diagram compares standard vs. active packaging over time.

### Sub-questions

#### 2a (2 marks) — Suggest
> **Suggest** two benefits of using active packaging when transporting fruit.

**Mark scheme — accept any two reasonable benefits:**
- Slows ripening
- Prevents microbe growth **or** keeps food safe to eat for longer
- Reduces waste **or** extends shelf-life

**Notes:** WTTE (words to that effect)

---

#### 2b (3 marks) — Describe

## Question 3 — Enzymes, Lactose Tolerance & Genetics (10 marks)

### Stimulus
Lactase is the major energy source for infants and young mammals; it is found in milk and broken down by the enzyme lactase. An organism able to produce lactase is "lactose tolerant". The dominant allele T must be inherited; the recessive allele has the symbol t. Graph shows lactase production over time after birth in pigs and rats. World infographic shows lactose tolerance distribution.

### Sub-questions

#### 3a (2 marks) — Outline
> **Outline** the importance of enzymes in the body.

**Mark scheme (both points needed):**
1. Speed up reactions **or** biological catalyst
2. Build up molecules **or** break down molecules — accept specific examples

---

#### 3b (1 mark) — State (table completion)
> **State** whether each genotype below is lactose tolerant.

| Genotype | Lactose tolerant? |
|---|---|
| TT | Yes |
| Tt | Yes |
| tt | No |

**Mark scheme:** All correct — a selection must be made in **all** boxes  
**Notes:** Incorrect use of "gene" is a CON, award 0

---

#### 3c (1 mark) — State
> **State** the meaning of the term phenotype.

**Mark scheme:** The (observable) characteristics of an organism (resulting from the expression of genes)  
**Notes:** Incorrect use of "gene" is a CON, award 0

---

#### 3d (4 marks) — Identify and justify
> The amount of milk consumed by young mammals is usually related to the amount of lactase they produce. Graph shows lactase production after birth in pigs and rats.  
> Using scientific knowledge, **identify** and **justify** one similarity and one difference in lactase production after birth in pigs and rats.

**Mark scheme:**
- *Similarity* [max 1]: highest levels directly after birth; both decrease (after birth)
- *Similarity justification* [max 1]: maternal milk is primary food source; decrease as transition to other food sources
- *Difference* [max 1]: production is much higher in pigs; pigs level off later than rats; production decreases at a faster rate or earlier in pigs
- *Difference justification* [max 1]: consume more lactose **or** pigs are bigger than rats; rats transition to other foods earlier than pigs; pigs and rats mature at different rates

---

#### 3e (1 mark) — State
> Using the data in the infographic, **state** the relationship between lactose tolerance and milk consumption.

**Mark scheme:** The higher the % lactose tolerance, the higher the milk consumption  
**Notes:** ORA (or reverse argument); do **not** accept linear or proportional

---

#### 3f (1 mark) — Suggest

**Mark scheme — accept any reasonable suggestion:**
- Data not available for all countries
- Correlation does not guarantee causation
- People choose not to drink milk for reasons other than lactose intolerance

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

1. **WTTE (Words To That Effect):** Applied throughout — markers accept equivalently phrased answers. Exact wording not required.
2. **CON (Contradiction):** Use of scientifically incorrect terminology (e.g. "gene" instead of "allele") in Q3b/3c invalidates the answer and scores 0.
3. **ECF (Error Carried Forward):** Applied in Q5b — if Q5a measurement is incorrect, the percentage calculation still scores if method is correct.
4. **ORA (Or Reverse Argument):** Applied in Q3e — the relationship can be stated in either direction.
5. **"Do not accept 'kills'":** In Q2b, microorganism activity being *reduced* is correct, not that they are killed.
6. **"Do not accept ethanol":** In Q1d, ethanol is not a human anaerobic product — only lactic acid is acceptable.
7. **Chain-of-reasoning questions:** Q8b (pollinators → biodiversity) and Q8c require logically linked steps, not just isolated facts.
8. **Extended response markbands (Q7, Q8c, Q9):** Marks awarded by category (RQ, Variables, Equipment, Method, Data, Safety for Q7; Environmental, Economic, Biosolar, Location, Conclusion for Q9). Each category is assessed independently — a weak method does not penalise a good variables answer.
9. **Graph plotting (Q4f):** Y-axis must have equal increments; mp 4 (key matching) only awarded if at least 3 points correctly plotted.
10. **"Do not accept strength" (Q4b):** The dependent variable must be stated as "mass needed to break the fibre", not "strength".
