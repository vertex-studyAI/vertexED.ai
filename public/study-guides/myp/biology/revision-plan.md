# 2-Week Biology Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of Biology per day**, with other subjects filling the rest. Adjust the daily slot to fit your schedule.

---

## Week 1 — Content Consolidation

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 1** | Criteria B & D strategy | Read [scientific-inquiry.md](topics/scientific-inquiry.md) top to bottom. Write one full Criterion B answer from scratch (any past paper). Self-mark against the markband. |
| **Day 2** | Ecosystems + Food webs | Read [ecology-and-ecosystems.md](topics/ecology-and-ecosystems.md). Do all ecosystem/food web questions from the question bank (they appear in every paper — guaranteed marks). |
| **Day 3** | Cells + Genetics | Read [cells-and-organisation.md](topics/cells-and-organisation.md) and [genetics-and-evolution.md](topics/genetics-and-evolution.md). Practice Punnett square questions from M18, N22, N23, N24. |
| **Day 4** | Photosynthesis + Respiration | Read [photosynthesis-and-respiration.md](topics/photosynthesis-and-respiration.md). Memorise both equations. Practice graph interpretation (limiting factors). |
| **Day 5** | Human body systems | Read [health-and-disease.md](topics/health-and-disease.md) and [homeostasis.md](topics/homeostasis.md). Focus on digestion, circulation, immunity, blood glucose. |
| **Day 6** | Transport + Enzymes | Read [transport-systems.md](topics/transport-systems.md). Skim enzyme questions in [question-bank.md](question-bank.md) — they appear in 12/18 sessions. |
| **Day 7** | Rest / light review | Skim [overview.md](overview.md) section 2 (topic frequency table). Identify any gaps. |

---

## Week 2 — Past Papers + Criterion D

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 8** | Criterion D essays | Read the Criterion D markband in [overview.md](overview.md) §3. Write one practice essay on GMOs or antibiotic resistance. Aim for 3 stakeholder perspectives + specific evidence. |
| **Day 9** | Full past paper (timed) | Do [M24](sessions/M24.md) or [N24](sessions/N24.md) under timed conditions. Mark yourself strictly. Note every question you dropped marks on. |
| **Day 10** | Fix weaknesses | Go back to the topics you dropped marks on in Day 9. Re-read those topic pages, then redo just those question types. |
| **Day 11** | Criterion C skills | Read [data-processing.md](topics/data-processing.md). Practice 3–4 evaluation questions (weakness + specific improvement pairs). |
| **Day 12** | Second full past paper | Do [M25](sessions/M25.md) (most recent, 100-mark format — closest to what you'll sit). Mark it. |
| **Day 13** | Criterion B drill | Write one full design answer cold. Check: IV with values, DV with units, 5 CVs with control methods, numbered method, data table, safety, ethics. |
| **Day 14** | Command terms + light review | Read [command-terms.md](command-terms.md). Focus on "Explain" vs "Describe" vs "Outline" — these are where marks are lost. Final skim of weakest topic. |

---

## Priority if time is tight

If Biology time gets squeezed by other subjects, protect these three:

1. **Criterion B design** — 13–21 marks, entirely formulaic, learnable
2. **Ecosystems / food webs** — appears in every single paper, Criterion A marks
3. **Criterion D essay structure** — one well-practised template covers any topic
