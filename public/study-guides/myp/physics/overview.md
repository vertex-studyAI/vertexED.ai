# MYP Physics e-Assessment — Exam Overview

## Exam Format

MYP Physics is assessed through an **online e-assessment** sat at the end of the MYP programme (typically Year 10). The paper is entirely computer-based and interactive.

**Session duration:** approximately 2 hours  
**Total marks:** varies by session (72–116); most sessions fall in the 80–100 range  
**Sessions available:** May (M) and November (N), 2016–2025 (M20 does not exist — May 2020 was cancelled)

### Question types used in the e-assessment

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Multiple-choice / select | Choose one or more correct options from a list |
| Drag-and-drop | Match, sort, or label items by dragging |
| Text-entry (numeric) | Type a calculated value, often with a unit mark awarded separately |
| Short written response | 1–4 sentences answering a describe/explain/suggest prompt |
| Extended written response | Paragraph-length answers for evaluate/discuss/justify questions |
| Table completion | Fill missing values in a data table (interpolate, calculate, unit conversion) |
| Graph / plot | Plot points on a pre-drawn axes, draw a line of best fit, read off values |
| Simulation interaction | Read and record values from an on-screen simulation |

### Assessment criteria

| Criterion | What it tests | Typical mark range |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **A — Knowledge and Understanding** | Recall, definitions, calculations, explaining physics concepts | 20–40 marks |
| **B — Inquiring and Designing** | Writing a research question, hypothesis, identifying variables, designing a valid method | 13–25 marks |
| **C — Processing and Evaluating** | Organising/presenting data, calculations, graph work, evaluating results | 15–30 marks |
| **D — Reflecting on the Impacts of Science** | Extended response: social, ethical, environmental, economic impact of science and technology | 8–20 marks |

All four criteria appear in every session. Criterion A carries the most marks in most sessions.

## Top 10 Most-Tested Concepts (Criterion A)

These specific knowledge points recur most often as short-answer or calculation questions:

1. **Ohm's law (V = IR)** and series/parallel circuit calculations — appears in almost every session
2. **Half-life calculations** — given a decay graph or table, find half-life or remaining activity
3. **Nuclear equations** — balancing alpha, beta, gamma decay; identifying daughter nuclides
4. **Types of radiation** — properties of alpha, beta, gamma; penetration, ionisation, uses
5. **Wave equation (v = fλ)** — calculate speed, frequency, or wavelength
6. **Energy transformations** — identify and describe energy changes in a given scenario
7. **Efficiency** — calculate as useful output / total input × 100%; suggest improvements
8. **Newton's laws** — explain motion, balanced/unbalanced forces, terminal velocity
9. **Heat transfer mechanisms** — distinguish conduction, convection, radiation; explain in context
10. **Electromagnetic spectrum** — order by frequency/wavelength; properties and uses of each region

## Command Terms by Criterion

### Criterion A
Calculate, State, Identify, Describe, Explain, Outline, Define, Label, Select, Classify, Deduce, Predict, Determine, Find, Estimate, Verify, Construct, Apply, List, Interpret

### Criterion B
Formulate (research question), State (hypothesis in If/Then/Because form), Identify (variables), Design (method), Outline (steps), Justify (method choices), Select (equipment), Describe (safety/ethical considerations)

### Criterion C
Organise / Present, Plot, Draw (line of best fit), Calculate, Determine (from graph), Evaluate (conclusion), Suggest (improvements/modifications), Comment, Compare, Interpret, Measure

### Criterion D
Discuss, Evaluate, Justify, Suggest, Explain, Compare, Assess, Consider

### Trends and patterns across years

**Mark totals:** Early May sessions (M16, M17) had the highest mark totals (100–116). Later sessions and all November sessions tend to sit in the 72–100 range. November sessions are consistently shorter than May sessions.

**Topic rotation:** No topic is absent from more than a few consecutive sessions. Electricity, waves, and energy are tested every year without exception.

**Radioactivity:** Appears in ~14 of 18 sessions. When it appears, it almost always involves a half-life calculation and/or a nuclear equation. Applications (medicine, industry, safety) feature in Criterion D.

**Space and astronomy:** Appears in ~13 of 18 sessions. Topics cycle between stars/stellar evolution, telescopes, satellites, and cosmology (Big Bang, Hubble). JWST and space missions feature in recent sessions (M24, M25).

**Investigation topics (Criterion B):** Investigations focus on mechanics (ramps, bouncing, motion) and heat/energy (insulation, thermoelectrics) most often. Resistance investigations (Criterion B/C) appear in N21, M24, N24.

**Criterion D themes:** Sustainability and climate change, medical ethics (imaging, radioactive tracers), transport choices, and technology access (developed vs developing world) are the most common extended-response themes.

**Increasing difficulty (complexity):** The most recent sessions (M24, M25, N24) involve more multi-step calculations and multi-part explanations than earlier sessions, suggesting the marking scheme has become more demanding over time.

## Mark Scheme Conventions

Understanding these conventions is essential for writing answers that gain full credit.

| Convention | What it means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **WTTE** | "Words to that effect" — accept any phrasing with the correct meaning; you don't need to use the exact wording in the mark scheme |
| **ORA** | "Or reverse argument" — if the question can be answered by describing the opposite scenario, that also earns the mark |
| **ECF** | "Error carried forward" — if you make an error in part (a), you can still earn marks in part (b) if you use your wrong answer consistently |
| **"seen or implied"** | The mark may be awarded even if the word/value isn't written explicitly, as long as the working makes it clear you know it |
| **[max N]** | The total available exceeds N marks but you can only earn up to N; write your best points first |
| **Dependent mark** | A later mark requires the earlier mark to be earned; flagged in the mark scheme |
| **All correct for full marks** | Drag-drop or select-all questions: you must get every item right to earn the marks (no partial credit) |
| **Unit mark** | The numerical value and the unit are marked separately; you can earn the number mark even with the wrong unit (and vice versa) |
| **"judge by eye"** | Diagram/graph answers are assessed holistically rather than with a ruler; reasonable accuracy is sufficient |
