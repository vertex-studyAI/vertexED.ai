# 2-Week Physics Revision Plan

Assumes roughly **1 hour of Physics per day**, with other subjects filling the rest. Adjust the daily slot to fit your schedule.

---

## Week 1 — Content Consolidation

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 1** | Electricity & circuits (Criterion A core) | Read the Electricity topic page. Drill Ohm's law (V = IR), series/parallel resistance, power (P = IV), and transformer calculations. Do M24 Q3 (wire resistance) and N21 Q4 (Ohm's law / resistance data). |
| **Day 2** | Waves & EM spectrum | Read the Waves topic page. Memorise the wave equation (v = fλ), the EM spectrum order, and key properties of each region. Attempt N16 Q2 (transverse/longitudinal/infrasound) and M23 Q2 (dispersion/light). |
| **Day 3** | Energy: transformations, efficiency, power | Read the Energy topic page. Practise Sankey diagrams, efficiency = (useful output / total input) × 100%, and GPE/KE calculations. Attempt M18 Q2 (ski slope/efficiency) and N23 Q3 (LED vs filament). |
| **Day 4** | Forces & motion (Newton's laws, velocity, acceleration) | Read the Forces & Motion topic page. Cover balanced/unbalanced forces, terminal velocity, and momentum. Attempt M22 Q1 (bullet train forces/motion) and N19 Q1 (momentum/Newton's laws train). |
| **Day 5** | Radioactivity & nuclear physics | Read the Radioactivity topic page. Practise half-life calculations from graphs, balancing alpha/beta/gamma decay equations, and identifying radiation types. Attempt N16 Q8 (half-life/Sr-90/Cs-137) and M24 Q2 (nuclear fission/U-235 decay). |
| **Day 6** | Space & astrophysics | Read the Space & Astrophysics topic page. Cover stars, telescopes, satellites, orbital mechanics, Hubble's law, and Big Bang evidence. Attempt N23 Q1 (space/astronomy/units) and M24 Q6 (telescopes/Hubble). |
| **Day 7** | Rest / light review | Skim the overview.md topic frequency table and year-by-year patterns. Identify any gaps from Days 1–6. Note which past-paper contexts you found hardest. |

## Week 2 — Past Papers + Criterion B/C/D

| Day | Focus | What to do |
|-----|-------|------------|
| **Day 8** | Criterion B — inquiry design | Read the Scientific Inquiry topic page. Write one full Criterion B answer cold: state a research question, hypothesis (If/Then/Because), IV with values, DV with units, at least 5 CVs with control methods, numbered method, sufficient-data statement. Self-mark against the markband. Use M21 Q5 (parachutes/air resistance) as a model. |
| **Day 9** | Full past paper (timed) | Attempt M25 (93 marks, most recent May session) under timed conditions. Mark yourself strictly. Note every question where you dropped marks. |
| **Day 10** | Fix weaknesses from Day 9 | Return to the topics you dropped marks on. Re-read those topic pages, then redo just those question types from a different session. |
| **Day 11** | Criterion C — data processing & graphing | Practise organising data tables, plotting points, drawing lines of best fit, calculating gradients, and evaluating whether a hypothesis is supported. Attempt M22 Q4 (pan flute/frequency graph) and N24 Q5 (thermistor/temperature). Focus on pairing weaknesses with specific improvements. |
| **Day 12** | Thermal physics + Heat transfer | Read the Thermal Physics topic page. Cover conduction, convection, radiation, specific heat capacity, states of matter, and gas laws. Attempt M19 Q1 (Dewar flask) and N23 Q7 (passive houses/kinetic theory). |
| **Day 13** | Criterion D — extended writing | Read the Criterion D markband in overview.md. Write one timed practice essay (~15 min) on a recurring theme: climate change and energy choices, medical ethics of radioactive tracers, or technology access (developed vs developing world). Aim for 3 distinct perspectives, specific evidence, and a concluding appraisal. Use M24 Q7 (JWST) or N22 Q8 (ultrasound/X-ray ethics) as a model structure. |
| **Day 14** | Command terms + final paper | Read the Command Terms section in overview.md — focus on "Explain" vs "Describe" vs "Outline" vs "Evaluate". Attempt N24 (75 marks) as a final timed test. Review any mark scheme conventions (ECF, WTTE, unit marks) you are still uncertain about. |

## Priority if time is tight

If Physics time gets squeezed by other subjects, protect these five:

1. **Electricity & Ohm's law** — tested in every single session; Criterion A marks are highly predictable
2. **Criterion B design** — 13–25 marks per paper, entirely formulaic once you know the template
3. **Radioactivity (half-life + decay equations)** — appears in 14/18 sessions; almost always includes a calculation and a nuclear equation
4. **Wave equation and EM spectrum** — tested in every session; questions are short and mark-efficient
5. **Criterion D essay structure** — one well-practised template (perspectives + evidence + appraisal) covers any theme
6. **Energy transformations and efficiency** — every session; Sankey diagrams and efficiency calculations appear across Criteria A and C
7. **Criterion C graphing** — plotting, LOBF, and gradient calculations appear in almost every processing block; practice pays off quickly
