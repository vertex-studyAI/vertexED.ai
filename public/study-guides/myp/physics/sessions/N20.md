## Question 1 — Thermoregulation / Heat Transfer (8 marks)

**Stimulus:** Mammals need to keep their body temperature within a given range — a concept called thermoregulation. This question is about the physics of heat transfer used in thermoregulation. Four animals are shown: Pig (smooth, light coloured skin), Zebra (short, smooth black and white fur), Polar bear (thick, long white fur), Buffalo (short, grey fur).

**Criterion: A**

### Q1a (1 mark) — Select

Select which of these animals is better adapted to living in cold conditions.

**MS answers:**
- C (Polar bear)

---

### Q1b (2 marks) — Outline

Reindeer have fur with hollow hairs. Outline how this feature enables reindeer to keep warm in cold weather.

**MS answers:**
- hollow fibres help to trap air
- air is a poor conductor / good insulator **or** heat cannot be lost through convection

**MS note:** WTTE

---

### Q1c (2 marks) — Outline

The Arctic fox is another animal adapted to living in cold conditions while its distant cousin, the fennec fox, has evolved to live in a hotter desert environment. Outline how the colour of the Arctic fox's fur and the size of the fennec fox's ears help with thermoregulation. You should use scientific language in your answer.

**MS answers:**
- white fur **and** reduces (heat lost through infra-red) radiation
- large surface area **and** increases (heat lost through) conduction **or** convection **or** radiation

---

### Q1d (3 marks) — Explain

One method of thermoregulation in dogs is evaporation from their wet tongue and nose. Explain how the process of evaporation will lead to a cooling effect in a dog.

**MS answers:**
- Heat is required for evaporation or evaporation removes heat
- high energy particles (in the liquid) escape the surface
- leaving lower energy particles behind

**MS note:** Accept "fast moving" for "high energy"

height above the liquid surface = capillary constant × 1/diameter

The capillary constant is the constant of proportionality for the relationship. From this, the student makes the following prediction: As the diameter of the tube decreases, the height that the water travels up the tube will increase. In order to function, the maglev train uses magnetic forces to control three systems: the levitation system allows the train to stay suspended about 1 cm above the tracks; the guidance system keeps the train centred over the tracks; the propulsion system drives the train forward.

**Criterion: A (Q8a), D (Q8b–d)**

### Q8d (3 marks) — Explain

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| WTTE | Will Take The Equivalent — any phrasing conveying the same meaning is accepted |
| ECF | Error Carried Forward — if an earlier calculation is wrong but subsequent working is correct, marks are still awarded |
| ORA | Or Reverse Argument — accept the logically equivalent converse argument |
| "Seen or implied" | The mark can be awarded even if not written explicitly, if it is clear from context |
| Two correct = 1 mark; all correct = 2 marks | Partial credit scheme used for classification/matching tasks |
| Markband tables (Q7, Q9) | Extended response scored holistically against descriptors for each criterion row; take the lowest row where all descriptors are met |
| Unit must match number given | When a numerical answer is expressed in a particular unit, the unit label must be consistent with that magnitude |
| Do not accept MJs⁻¹ or kJs⁻¹ | Power unit must match the scale of the numerical answer given |
| Accept fast moving for high energy | Everyday language equivalent accepted for particle energy descriptor |
| Do not accept j (lowercase) | Joule must be capitalised as J or written in full |
| Criterion D on unit mark (Q2b) | The unit mark in a calculation can be assessed under Criterion D (Reflection) not just A |
