## Question 1 — Star Colours and Temperature (4 marks)

**Stimulus:** Two photographs of stars — one red/orange star and one blue star. Context: colours of visible light emitted from a star are related to the star's temperature. Young, hot stars appear more blue; older stars appear more red.

A table of surface temperatures and corresponding star colours is provided:

| Surface temperature / °C | Colour of star |
|---|---|
| < 3200 | red |
| 3200 – 4700 | orange-yellow |
| 4700 – 6000 | yellow |
| 6000 – 7500 | yellow-white |
| 7500 – 10 000 | white |
| 10 000 – 30 000 | white-blue |
| 30 000 – 60 000 | blue |

### Q1a (2 marks) — Select

Select the correct statements to complete the comparisons between red and blue light.

The speed of red light is [Select] the speed of blue light.

The wavelength of red light is [Select] the wavelength of blue light.

The frequency of red light is [Select] the frequency of blue light.

**MS answers:**
- The speed of red light is **equal to** the speed of blue light. (1)
- The wavelength of red light is **greater than** the wavelength of blue light. (1)
- The frequency of red light is **less than** the frequency of blue light. (1)

**MS note:** Any one correct scores one mark; all three correct scores two marks.

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| WTTE | Will Take The Equivalent — accept answers in equivalent wording |
| ORA | Or Reverse Argument — accept the converse statement |
| ECF | Error Carried Forward — allow follow-through marks from earlier errors |
| Do not accept "proportional" | For trend descriptions, "linear" is required; "proportional" implies passing through origin which cannot be verified from data alone |
| Do not accept "orbit" for "axis" | In Q3d, the key concept is Earth's rotational axis, not orbital path |
| Units not essential | Where noted, the numerical answer alone earns the mark without units |
| Accept Nm or joules | For energy calculations, either unit form is acceptable |
| Award only if prior points earned | Q3b point 3 (arrow direction) only awarded if points 1 and 2 are also correct |
