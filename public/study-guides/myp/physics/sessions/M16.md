## Question 2 — Gravitational Field Strength on Planets (11 marks)

**Stimulus:** A robotic probe is sent into space to collect data from three of the four inner planets: Mercury, Mars and Venus. While passing close by each planet the probe drops a test object from a height of 100.0 m; frictional forces are negligible. An interactive simulation allows the student to measure the fall time for each planet. Results shown:
- Mars: time = 7.3 s
- Mercury: time = 7.4 s
- Venus: time = 4.8 s
- Earth: time = 4.5 s

Additional context: The gravitational field strength (g) is proportional to a planet's density (ρ) and its radius (r): g ~ ρ × r. The radius of Mercury is about two thirds of the radius of Mars.

Further: The probe visits Jupiter's moons Io and Ganymede. g on Io = 1.8 N kg⁻¹; g on Ganymede = 1.4 N kg⁻¹. Test object mass = 20 kg. All gravitational potential energy transforms to kinetic energy before hitting the moons' surfaces.

### Q2a (3 marks) — Calculate
Using the data you collected above, **calculate** the acceleration due to gravity on Mercury and Mars. You may wish to use the formula sheet.

**MS answers:**
- Mercury = 3.7 (ms⁻²)
- Mars = 3.8 (ms⁻²)
- m s⁻² (unit)

**MS note:** Maximum 2 if more than two significant figures are recorded. Accept m/s². Maximum 2 if more than two significant figures are recorded.

---

### Q2b (2 marks) — Explain
**Explain** why Mars and Mercury can have almost the same value of g, if Mars has a much larger radius.

**MS answers:**
- a reference to the relationship between g and density
- a reference to the relationship between r and density
- (So) Mars must have a much lower density

**MS note:** Accept mass per unit volume in place of density.

---

### Q2c (3 marks) — Calculate
Use information from the formula sheet to **calculate** the gravitational potential energy (Ep) of the test object before it is dropped over Io.

**MS answers:**

- unit correct as J or kJ as appropriate

**MS note:** Seen or implied by correct substitution. Award 1 mark only for 3600(J)/3.6(kJ) if no working is shown. Accept Nm or kNm.

---

### Q2d (3 marks) — Explain

**Stimulus:** Look at the two very different images of feet. Image 1 shows a normal photograph of a foot. Image 2 is by the artist Hugh Turvey who uses X-rays in his work. This piece of art is called *Femme Fatale* and shows the foot of a woman wearing a high-heeled shoe. The artist has used an X-ray, normally used in science, medicine or industry to create this artistic image.

**Outline** what the use of science can reveal that a photograph does not. Refer to the image and apply the ideas that you have been introduced to in this task in your answer.

**MS answers:**
- Any relevant scientific point e.g. high detail in x-ray (first point must refer to the image)
- Two further reasonable points clearly linked to the image, for example:
  - the x-ray shows the foot is in an unnatural/distorted position
  - information about the construction of the shoe is revealed

## Question 13 — XRFS and Rembrandt's Paintings (4 marks)

**Stimulus:** A link gives similar information about the use of XRFS and Rembrandt's work. Three images are shown:
- Image A: a painting of an old man by Rembrandt
- Image B: an X-ray fluorescence scan (XRFS) of the canvas beneath — showing a ghostly face shape
- Image C: Rembrandt's self-portrait showing a face similar to that revealed in image B

Some art historians suggest that Rembrandt reused the canvas shown in Image B.

### Q13a (2 marks) — Outline
**Outline** the evidence in these three images that supports the suggestion that the canvas was reused.

**MS answers:**
- XRFS reveals image B not visible in image A
- image B resembles image C

| ECF | Error Carried Forward — award marks if working method is correct despite earlier error |
