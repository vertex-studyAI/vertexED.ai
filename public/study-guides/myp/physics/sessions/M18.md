## Question 1 — Electromagnetism and Transformers (10 marks)

**Stimulus:** The key concept is **change**; criterion A (Knowing and understanding). Magnets can be used to induce a current in a wire. The context covers Faraday's experiments on electromagnetic induction and the development of transformers.

### Q1a (1 mark) — Select

Select the diagram that correctly shows the magnetic field lines around a bar magnet.

Options A–D show four different representations of field lines around a bar magnet (N on top, S on bottom). Option B shows field lines looping from N to S outside the magnet and through the magnet from S to N — the conventional correct pattern.

**MS answers:**
- B

**MS note:** No additional notes.

---

### Q1b (2 marks) — Suggest

Faraday performed an experiment in which he moved the bar magnet through the coil of wire. He noticed at certain times that there was an induced current flowing in the coil of wire. He noted the following observations:
- the induced current only flowed for a short time
- if the magnet was entering the coil, the current would flow in the opposite direction compared to when the magnet was leaving the coil
- the current was greater when the number of turns on the coils was increased.

Suggest two additional ways that the size of the current could be increased.

**MS answers:**
Any two of the following [max 2]:
- stronger magnet
- faster movement of magnet
- use a coil of wire with lower resistance
- coils closer together

**MS note:** Do **not** accept "increase the number of turns" as this is given in the question. Accept "area" only if accompanied by discussion of flux density.

---

### Q1c (1 mark) — Predict

The bar magnet is turned so that the south pole enters the coil first. Predict the change on the induced current.

**MS answers:**
- current flows in the opposite direction

**MS note:** None.

---

### Q1d (2 marks) — Calculate

In another experiment, Faraday found that a current could be induced in a coil of wire near to a coil carrying an electric current. This led to the development of a device called a transformer.

[Diagram shows: Power supply 110 V connected to primary coil P with 10 turns; secondary coil S has 20 turns; both wound on a soft iron ring.]

Assume that the transformer is 100 % efficient and calculate the value of the voltage on the secondary coil.

**MS answers:**

### Q1f (2 marks) — Suggest

  **or** direct current produces a change in the magnetic field only when it is switched on **or** off

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| WTTE | Worth The Text Equivalent — accept any answer that conveys the same meaning |
| ORA | Or Reverse Argument — accept logically equivalent converse statements |
| ECF | Error Carried Forward — if an earlier wrong answer is used correctly, award the mark |
| Do not accept X alone | The answer must include additional scientific detail or context beyond X |
| Award 2 marks for correct answer only | No partial marks if the final answer is wrong without working |
| Any two/three suitable | A list is provided; student chooses any acceptable pair/triple |
| Seen or implied | The mark can be awarded if the concept is clearly embedded in the answer |
| Accept values in the range ... | Numerical tolerance is given for graph reading questions |
| No ECF | Error carry-forward is explicitly not permitted for this sub-part |
