## Question 1 — Light and Sound Waves (8 marks)

**Stimulus:** A photograph of a lightning strike at night. This question is about light and sound waves and the different speeds at which they move through the air.

**Criterion: A (Knowledge)**

### Q1a (1 mark) — Select

Select the correct response from the choices below.

The speed of sound in air is [Select]
The speed of light in air is [Select]

Options given: 330 ms⁻¹ / 300 000 000 ms⁻¹ / 55 000 ms⁻¹

**MS answers:**
- speed of sound in air = 330 ms⁻¹ **and** speed of light = 300 000 000 ms⁻¹

---

### Q1b (3 marks) — Outline

If you watch lightning from a storm and listen until you hear the thunder, it is possible to find the distance to the lightning strike.

Outline what quantity you would measure and the calculation you would perform to calculate the distance.

**MS answers:**
- time between lightning and thunder
- use speed = distance / time to find distance
- using the speed of sound in air

**MS note:** Accept velocity, displacement. Can be implied; award separately.

---

### Q1c (2 marks) — Describe

A student is watching a storm. He calculates the distance to the storm every five minutes and draws the following graph of his results. [Graph shows Distance/km on y-axis (0–4.0) vs Time of day on x-axis (07:55–08:25); the storm approaches from 4.0 km at 07:55 to ~1.0 km at 08:15, then recedes to ~3.0 km at 08:25.]

Use the graph to describe the journey of the storm as it passes the student.

**MS answers:**
- moving towards and then away from student (or equivalent)
- any additional point, for example:
  - moving towards and then away from student
  - it passes at a distance of 1 km
  - it passes overhead

**MS note:** Any additional correct detail for 2nd mark.

---

### Q1d (2 marks) — Calculate

Use information from the graph to calculate the speed of the storm between 08:00 and 08:10. Give your answer in km h⁻¹.

**MS answers:**
- values from graph: distance changes from ~3.0 km to ~1.0 km in 10 minutes → Δd = 2.0 km

## Question 2 — Lightning and Electricity (9 marks)

**Stimulus:** This question is about lightning and electricity.

**Criterion: A (Knowledge)**

### Q2a (1 mark) — Select

Select the sentence that best describes the process by which the water and crystals become charged.

Options:
- Electrons are transferred from the small upward moving crystals to the water drops.
- Electrons are transferred from the water drops to the small upward moving crystals.
- Protons are transferred from the small upward moving crystals to the water drops.
- Neutrons are transferred from the water drops to the small upward moving crystals.

**MS answers:**
- Electrons are transferred from the small upward moving crystals to the water drops.

**MS note:** Seen or implied.

---

### Q2b (3 marks) — Calculate

A build-up of charge in a thunderstorm may discharge as a huge spark of lightning between the bottom of a cloud and the ground. When lightning strikes, the air is heated to a plasma (super high temperature gas) which has a low resistance so that a current can flow.

[Image shows: Cloud → Ground with 500 000 V and 20 000 A labelled]

Using information from the picture, calculate the resistance of the plasma during the lightning strike.

**MS answers:**
- R = 500 000 / 20 000
- R = 25
- correct unit Ω

**MS note:** Seen or implied.

---

### Q2c (2 marks) — Calculate

A bolt of lightning has a duration of 30 μs. Calculate the charge transferred during this time.

**MS answers:**
- ΔQ = I × t = 20 000 × 30 × 10⁻⁶
- ΔQ = 0.6 (C)

**MS note:** Seen or implied from previous. Award 1 mark for 600 000 (ΔQ if time not converted).

---

### Q2d (3 marks) — Explain

Explain how lightning conductors prevent buildings from being damaged.

**MS answers:**

- current/charge from lightning will flow through the conductor

## Question 3 — Decay and Half-life (9 marks)

**Stimulus:** This question is about decay and half-life.

**Criterion: A (Knowledge)**

### Q3a (1 mark) — Select

Draggable options: ⁰₋₁β and ⁴₂α

- ²⁴¹₉₅Am → ²³⁷₉₃Np + [   ]
- ²¹⁰₈₄Po → ²⁰⁶₈₂Pb + [   ]
- ²⁴⁰₉₃Np → ²⁴⁰₉₄Pu + [   ]

**MS answers (all three correct for 1 mark):**
- ²⁴¹₉₅Am → ²³⁷₉₃Np + ⁴₂α
- ²¹⁰₈₄Po → ²⁰⁶₈₂Pb + ⁴₂α
- ²⁴⁰₉₃Np → ²⁴⁰₉₄Pu + ⁰₋₁β

---

### Q3b (3 marks) — Identify

Meitnerium-274 emits an alpha particle when it decays.

Identify the atomic number, mass number and name of the element formed when an atom of meitnerium-274 decays. You may refer to the periodic table.

- mass number = 270
- bohrium or Bh

**MS note:** Award all marks independently.

---

### Q3c (2 marks) — Outline

After decay has taken place, the daughter nucleus emits gamma radiation. Outline the nature of gamma radiation.

**MS answers:**

- electromagnetic waves or radiation or photon

**MS note:** Do not accept "particle".

---

### Q3d (3 marks) — Evaluate

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| WTTE | Will/Would/Accept The Term/Expression Equivalent — accept any reasonable phrasing with the same meaning |
| ORA | Or Reverse Argument — accept the inverse reasoning |
| ECF | Error Carried Forward — award follow-on marks even if an earlier answer was wrong |
| Do not accept | Specific terms or approaches explicitly excluded from credit |
| Seen or implied | Mark is awarded even if the point is not stated explicitly but is clear from working |
| Award all marks independently | Each mark point is assessed separately; getting one wrong does not preclude others |
| [max N] | Maximum of N marks for that particular sub-category |
| Consider both response boxes | Where a question has two text boxes, look across both when awarding marks |
