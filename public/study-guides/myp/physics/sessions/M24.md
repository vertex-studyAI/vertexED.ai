## Question 1 — Coal Power and Climate Change (12 marks) — Criterion A

**Stimulus:** Coal-fired power stations are systems that change chemical potential energy into electricity, beginning with the burning of coal to heat water. A coal-fired power station involves three states of matter. An annotated diagram of a coal power station is shown (boiler with burning coal, steam turbine, generator, cooling tower with water).

### Q1a (1 mark) — Identify

A coal-fired power station involves three states of matter. **Identify** the location of each of the substances below.

[Drag-drop: Water → cooling tower/condenser; Steam → turbine area; Coal → boiler/furnace]

**MS answers:**
- All three correct: Water in cooling tower/condenser, Steam at turbine, Coal at boiler — 1 mark for all correct

---

### Q1b (1 mark) — State

Absorbing heat can affect the kinetic energy of water molecules. **State** how water molecules are affected by this increase in kinetic energy.

**MS answers:**
- Molecules move faster

**MS note:** Accept "move more" or "vibrate more"

---

### Q1c (2 marks) — Outline

As water boils into steam, the heat energy absorbed does not affect the kinetic energy of the molecules. **Outline** what happens to this heat energy referring to the movement and arrangement of particles.

**MS answers:** Accept any two points from the list [max 2]:
- Speed of movement of particles does not increase
- Energy is being used to break bonds
- Energy is used to separate molecules/particles
- Potential energy of particles is increased

---

### Q1d (2 marks) — Calculate

One kilogram of coal contains approximately 2.5 × 10⁷ J of chemical potential energy. For a coal-fired power station that burns 4.5 × 10⁵ kg of coal per hour, **calculate** the energy released in one hour. You should give your answer to two significant figures.

**MS answers:**
- 1.125 × 10¹³ (J)
- Rounded correctly to 1.1 × 10¹³ (J)

**MS note:** Accept 11 × 10¹² or 11 TJ. Award 2 marks for a correct answer rounded to 2 sf.

---

### Q1e (2 marks) — Determine

Another coal-fired power station burns enough coal per hour to generate a power of 26 700 MW. If the efficiency of this power station is 28 %, **determine** the useful power output.

**MS answers:**
- Recognition/application of efficiency formula (seen/implied)
- 7476 MW or 7.476 × 10⁹ W

**MS note:** Award 2 marks to correct answers given with units of kW, W or MW to 2 or more significant figures.

## Question 2 — Nuclear Fission and Radioactive Decay (12 marks) — Criteria A, C, D

**Stimulus:** A video/animation shows the fission of uranium-235. A neutron strikes a uranium-235 nucleus, which splits into caesium-140 (Cs-140) and rubidium-94 (Rb-94), releasing two free neutrons and energy.

### Q2a (2 marks) — State

Using the periodic table, **state** the number of protons and the number of neutrons present in the nucleus of one atom of uranium-235.

**MS answers:**
- Protons = 92
- Neutrons = 143

---

### Q2b (2 marks) — Outline

**Outline** how the protons and neutrons are affected by the fission of uranium-235.

**MS answers:** Any correct statements from the list [maximum two]:
- Protons or neutrons or protons and neutrons are divided (between the daughter nuclei)
- The total number of protons in the daughter nuclei is the same as in U-235
- Two free neutrons are released
- Total number of neutrons and protons is the same after the reaction as before

---

### Q2c (2 marks) — Suggest

A neutron is needed to start this fission reaction. Neutrons are also produced during the reaction. This means that a chain reaction can occur with the fission of uranium-235. **Suggest** the meaning of the term chain reaction.

**MS answers:**
- One reaction causes another reaction to occur
- The released neutrons cause the other reactions

**MS note:** WTTE

---

### Q2d (3 marks) — Describe

In nuclear power plants, fission reactions are used to supply energy but these chain reactions have to be carefully controlled. **Describe** a possible danger of an uncontrolled chain reaction in a nuclear power plant.

**MS answers:**

More reactions mean more energy is released, **or** each reaction in a chain reaction releases energy, **or** too much energy is released — [1 mark]

Results in an atomic explosion **or** a nuclear melt down — [1 mark]

Emission of radioactive material into the surrounding environment, **or** nuclear fallout, **or** harmful effects of radiation on living things, **or** effects of fire/explosion on the surrounding environment — [1 mark]

**MS note:** Accept reference to heat in place of energy.

---

---

### Q2f (2 marks) — Suggest

**Suggest** why the long decay time of uranium-235 poses a significant challenge for the safe disposal of fuel rods.

**MS answers:**
