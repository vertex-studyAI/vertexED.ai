A map of the city is shown at 06:00 morning with a key showing power off and power on regions. The animation shows traffic at a city intersection with traffic lights. A solar panel and battery station are shown positioned to capture the energy of the sun. Load shedding results in the electric power supply to different parts of a city being interrupted.

### Q10a (1 mark) — Suggest

Apart from traffic lights, **suggest** a different application where a constant source of electricity is necessary.

**MS answers:**
- any reasonable application requiring a constant source of electricity

---

### Q10b (15 marks) — Discuss / Evaluate

Using information from the animation and your wider MYP studies, **discuss** and **evaluate** the use of a small-scale solar electrical power supply to solve the problem identified in part (a). In your answer you should consider:
- why a constant source of electricity is important in your application
- the advantage of using a small-scale power supply in this situation
- the disadvantage of using a small-scale power supply in this situation
- economic factors
- social factors

**MS answers — markband table:**

| Marks | Descriptors |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | incomplete statement of reason for constant source of electricity with no reference to application in 10a; advantage or disadvantage stated with no reference to 10a |
| 3–5 | reason for a constant source of electricity related to application in 10a is stated; advantage and disadvantage related to 10a is stated; an impact on society or an economic impact |
| 6–9 | reason for a constant source of electricity in application in 10a is clearly explained; advantage outlined; disadvantage outlined; an impact on society and an economic impact |
| 10–15 | reason for a constant source of electricity in application in 10a is clearly explained and justified; advantage explained in detail; disadvantage explained in detail; a clearly reasoned impact on society; a clearly reasoned economic impact; a concluding appraisal |

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| ECF | Error carried forward — subsequent marks awarded even if earlier answer incorrect, provided method is correct |
| ORA | Or reverse argument — accept the converse/opposite reasoning |
| WTTE | Words to that effect — exact phrasing not required; accept equivalent meaning |
| Unit required | Calculation marks where unit must be given to earn final mark |
| Do not accept | Specific wrong answers explicitly excluded (e.g. "dangerous" for Q9a, "gloves or goggles" for Q7g) |
| Award only one mark | Where two linked answers are needed but only one is correct (e.g. Q8d: 4 half-lives AND 8 years; "four years" alone = 1 mark) |
| Sig fig note | Q8a averages — award sig fig mark for any number correctly expressed |
| Accept "negative charge" | Q4b — "negative charge" accepted in place of "electron" for marks 2 and 3 |
| Markband (extended response) | Q10b uses a 0–15 markband with four descriptors rather than point marking |
