## Question 1 — Radio Waves and Communication (8 marks)

**Stimulus:** Context set in the late 19th century when physicists considered radio waves to be an invisible form of light. Marconi was trying to find a way to send messages without the need for wires. One advantage of radio waves is that they can bend around obstacles (diffraction). In 1897, Marconi sent the first ever radio message across open sea — "are you ready" — over a distance of 6 km. Speed of radio waves in air = 3.0 × 10⁸ ms⁻¹.

### Q1a (2 marks) — Select

Select the correct option to complete the statements below.

Radio waves travel [Select] light waves in a vacuum.
Radio waves have [Select] light waves.

Options shown: faster than / slower than / at the same speed as; a longer wavelength than / a shorter wavelength than / the same wavelength as.

**MS answers:**
- Radio waves travel **at the same speed as** light waves in a vacuum.
- Radio waves have **a longer wavelength than** light waves.

**MS note:** 1 mark per correct selection. Criterion A.
