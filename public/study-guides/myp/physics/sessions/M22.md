## Question 1 — Shinkansen Bullet Train (11 marks)

**Stimulus:** The Shinkansen (bullet train) uses magnetic levitation — repelling magnets raise the train above the tracks, reducing friction. When entering tunnels, the bullet-shaped nose caused air to build up, producing a loud boom on exit. Scientists redesigned the nose modelled on a kingfisher's beak. The redesigned train cuts through air, reduces air build-up, reduces the boom, and increases efficiency. Context is presented as a video with an infographic showing forces on the train and a speed-time graph.

### Q1a (1 mark) — Select

Select the unit for measuring force.

Options: joule / kilogram / newton / watt

**MS answers:**
- Newton

---

### Q1b (2 marks) — Label

The diagram below shows the forces acting on the 1964 train travelling at a constant maximum speed of 220 km/h. Label the forces in the diagram.

Draggable items: Weight / Normal force / Magnetic force / Drag force

**MS answers:**
- Normal force — upward (or magnetic force acceptable for vertical component)
- Magnetic force — upward (or normal force acceptable for vertical component)
- Drag force — horizontal, opposing motion
- Weight — downward

**MS note:** Accept magnetic force or normal force for the vertical component. 2 marks for 2 correct; full marks for all correct.

---

### Q1c (3 marks) — Explain

The redesigned train is described as more efficient. Explain why, referring to the forces acting on the train.

**MS answers:**
- Less than (drag force is less than for the original design) — WTTE
- The drag force or air resistance is reduced — WTTE
- The forces are balanced or resultant force is zero (at constant speed)

**MS note:** WTTE throughout.

---

The speed-time graph shows the motion of the 1964 train on its journey through a tunnel. Use the graph to calculate the length of the tunnel.

(Graph: train enters at ~61 ms⁻¹, decelerates through tunnel, speed varies — trapezoidal shape over ~62 s)

**MS answers:**

**Stimulus:** Transformers are electrical devices that reduce or increase voltage of alternating current. They consist of primary and secondary coils wrapped around an iron core. Use information from the formula sheet to calculate the primary supply voltage. Assume 100% efficient.

Given: Primary coil current = 1.2 A; Secondary coil voltage = 60 V; Secondary coil current = (from diagram)

## Question 3 — Melting Ice in Pure and Salt Water (9 marks)

**Stimulus:** Video shows ice cubes made from pure water melting in glasses of pure and salt water (ice cubes coloured for visibility). The ice cube in pure water melts faster than in salt water. Tiny water droplets form on the outside of each glass — mostly all around the pure water glass but only at the top of the salt water glass.

### Q3a (1 mark) — Match

There are three methods of heat transfer. Select the most appropriate description for each method. (Drag-drop)

- Radiation → Transfer of heat by electromagnetic radiation
- Conduction → Transfer of heat by direct contact
- Convection → Transfer of heat by moving fluids

**MS answers:**
- All correct (1 mark for all three correct)

---

### Q3b (1 mark) — Select

Select the diagram that best represents the process of heat energy transfer between the ice cube and the water.

**MS answers:**
- Correct diagram selected (convection currents from warmer water to cold ice)

---

### Q3c (1 mark) — State

In the video, tiny water droplets begin to form on the outside of each glass. State the physical change that is shown by the formation of these water droplets.

**MS answers:**
- Condensation

**MS note:** Accept "it condenses".

---

### Q3d (1 mark) — Suggest

Tiny water droplets form around most of the outside of the glass containing pure water but only form at the top of the glass containing salt water. Suggest why the pattern of water droplets is different.

**MS answers:**
- Condensation occurs on cold surfaces, OR
- Salt water is only cold at the top of the cup

---

### Q3e (3 marks) — Explain

Using your knowledge of heat transfer processes, explain why the ice cube melts more slowly in the salt water.

**MS answers (any 2 points from list, max 2, plus conclusion):**
- Salt water is more dense (than pure water)
- Melted cold water remains near surface
- Convection of salt water does not occur or less convection in salt water than in pure water
- Conclusion: (so) heat is transferred less quickly (in the salt water) — WTTE

**MS note:** Do not award the final mark unless the first and second marks are awarded. WTTE for all points.

---

### Q3f (2 marks) — Predict

**MS answers:**
- First marking point: Melted cold water would be distributed, OR stirring would increase (heat transfer by) convection — WTTE

## Key Mark Scheme Patterns

| Pattern | Explanation |
|---|---|
| WTTE | Will To The Effect — accept equivalent wording, not verbatim |
| ORA | Or Reverse Argument — accept the converse reasoning |
| [max N] | Only award N marks from the bulleted list, even if more are stated |
| Award 0 if... | Some questions void all marks for specific errors (e.g., selecting tension as IV in Q5e; hypothesis relating tension and frequency in Q5f) |
| Markband (1–4) | Extended-response questions (Q6d, Q8) scored using a 4-level markband across multiple criteria |
| Seen or implied | Intermediate step credited even if not explicitly written out, provided final answer is consistent |
| Consistent with LOBF | Gradient/calculation marked consistent with the candidate's own drawn line, not a fixed value |
| Do not accept "to take an average" | For reliability questions, this phrasing alone is insufficient — must explain why |
