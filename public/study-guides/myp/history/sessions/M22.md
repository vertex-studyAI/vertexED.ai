> 3. Keep your body quiet and calm, and do not breathe deeply.

## Key Patterns

1. **Two distinct thematic halves**: Questions 1–3 focus on social/cultural/artistic movements with a hip-hop music case study and open-ended student-chosen research; Questions 4–7 pivot to an entirely different topic (Black Death / bubonic plague / scientific innovation). This is unusual — two unrelated topic areas in one paper.

2. **Heavy research-skills emphasis**: Q2 (18 marks, 7 sub-parts) is almost entirely about *process* — how to plan an investigation, formulate RQs, sub-questions, choose sources and interview subjects — rather than testing content knowledge. Students are rewarded for methodology, not factual recall.

3. **Student autonomy in open questions**: Q2 and Q3 both allow students to choose their own movement. The mark scheme uses the Renaissance as the model example throughout, but explicitly states any valid movement can score full marks. This creates very diverse responses and examiners must apply best-fit across many contexts.

4. **Source analysis skills tested rigorously in Q5**: Both value and limitation must reference **origin and/or purpose** explicitly to reach 3 marks — a higher bar than simply noting what the source says.

5. **Persuasive speech format (Q3)**: Unusually, the extended writing task is a speech, not an essay. This is marked on format, communication style (emotive language, rhetorical devices) and organizational structure separately from content. The worked example (Nazi Germany / 1932 Berlin) dominates the mark scheme, though any valid movement is accepted.

6. **Q7 essay carries the most marks (24)**: It is marked across four criteria (Criterion D content/thinking 8 marks; C1 format 2 marks; C2 communication 6 marks; C3 organization 8 marks — note the high weighting on structure and communication relative to content knowledge). The "to what extent" command term requires students to argue a nuanced position, not simply list positives.

7. **Command terms used**: Evaluate (Q1), Outline (Q2a, Q4), Explain (Q2b, Q2f, Q3, Q6), Formulate (Q2c, Q2e), Justify (Q2d), Identify (Q2g), Describe (Q5a, Q5b), To what extent (Q7). This is a broad spread covering all cognitive levels from identify through evaluate and synthesis.

8. **"Best-fit" marking model**: The mark scheme opens with an explicit note that examiners apply a "best fit" approach — if a student matches the top two criteria out of three in a markband, the mark is awarded. This means partial achievement across multiple bands is resolved in favour of the better-fitting overall band.

9. **No choice questions**: All questions are compulsory; there is no option to select alternative questions within any part of the paper.
