## Context / Scenario

The paper has three major inquiry sections:

1. **Section 1 (Q1a–Q1j) — Investigating**: Your school has asked you to participate in a conference for MYP students from around the world. The conference topic this year is *"improving cooperation between different ideologies around the world."* Your task is to investigate the impacts of a supranational organization or alliance. The statement of inquiry context runs through all Q1 sub-questions.

2. **Section 2 (Q2) — Evaluating an investigative process**: Your MYP history teacher has given another team the task of investigating the impacts of empires throughout history, to help prepare for a different conference on international systems. The teacher has asked them to present their investigative process for your feedback.

3. **Section 3 (Q3) — Communicating**: You have been asked to help organize an exhibition at your school library on independence movements in history. Your part of the exhibition will focus on how independence movements can help create a sense of identity. You must design a **promotional poster** for a chosen independence movement, plus write **library website text** explaining how the independence movement formed a sense of identity.

4. **Section 4 (Q4a–Q4b) — Thinking Critically (Source analysis)**: Source A — a text by a historian studying the key concept of *change* in history.

5. **Section 5 (Q5) — To what extent**: Evaluate the statement *"Throughout history innovation has come from individuals responding to crises."* (Use one example from MYP history studies to support your answer.)

6. **Section 6 (Q6) — Compare and Contrast**: Source B and Source C on the significance of technological innovation for society.

7. **Section 7 (Q7) — Discuss essay**: *"Pioneers, innovators and developers are influenced most by the period in which they live."* Discuss this statement using examples from MYP history.

### Mark scheme insights
- Q1a has a strict **not acceptable** list — NGOs and political parties are excluded; only supranational intergovernmental organizations count.
- Q1b–Q1d form an integrated research design chain: identification → explanation of purpose → research question → justification of relevance. Each step penalised if a preceding step is wrong.
- Q1e and Q1f are unusual: sub-questions must reference the **concept** (ideology/cooperation) but do **not** need to use the exact word.
- **Evaluate** (Q2, 12 marks) is assessed on values, limitations, AND a concluding statement as separate strands contributing to the overall mark.
- Q3 is a **creative communication task** — the student produces an actual poster design and website text. Criterion C1 (format) assesses the poster; C2/C3 assess the written website text.
- **To what extent** (Q5, 8 marks) requires an explicit position, a supporting example, AND a counter-claim to reach the top band (7–8).
- **Compare and contrast** (Q6, 8 marks): to reach 7–8, the student must do both comparing AND contrasting with detail — one-sided analysis is capped lower.
- Q7 essay is marked on four criteria simultaneously (A + C1 + C2 + C3 + D). The mark scheme example throughout is **Henry Ford** and the assembly line.
- The statement of inquiry underlying Q1 is: *"Improving cooperation between different ideologies around the world."*
- The key concept underpinning Q3 and Q7 is **identity** (Q3) and **change** (Q4a/Q4b).
