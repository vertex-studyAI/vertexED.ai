## Key patterns

1. **Theme coherence across the paper**: The entire paper is structured around a single global theme — *Globalization and Innovation*. Questions 1–3 focus on globalization and cultural sharing; Questions 4–6 transition to exploration/colonization and identity; Questions 7–8 centre on scientific innovation and its consequences. This tight thematic unity is a distinctive feature of N22.

2. **Sport as the vehicle for Q2**: The investigation stimulus in Q2 is unusually specific — a marketing department video presentation about the role of sport in cultural sharing, with named real sources (National Geographic, YouTube, a book by Michael Llewellyn Smith). Students must evaluate the actual sources cited, not just evaluate an abstract process.

3. **Q3 is entirely open-ended research planning**: Unusually, Q3a–3f ask students to design their own investigation from scratch, making all six sub-answers dependent on the student's own chosen research question. Marks in 3c–3f therefore follow from the student's answer in 3a.

4. **Q4 uses a presentation format**: Rather than a conventional essay, Q4 asks for a class presentation with required structural features (heading + sub-headings + concluding statement + informal tone). Criterion C2 explicitly rewards presentation-appropriate language over essay style. This is the most structurally constrained writing task on the paper.

5. **Q7 OPCVL-style analysis**: Questions 7a–7c follow an origin → purpose → value/limitation sequence on Source E (NOAA infographic on the Exxon Valdez oil spill). The source is an infographic rather than a text, requiring students to analyse a visual data source. The context — a US government agency both producing the infographic AND having led the original investigation — creates an obvious potential bias point for limitation answers.

6. **Q8 requires balanced perspectives explicitly**: The mark scheme for Criterion D makes explicit that at 7–8 marks, perspectives must be *equally considered/balanced*. Students who only argue one side are capped at 3–4 for Criterion D. The question is deliberately open in allowing any pioneer, innovator or developer from before 2010.

7. **"Best fit" marking model throughout**: The mark scheme repeatedly emphasises that examiners choose the level that *overall* best matches the work. If a student matches two of three strands in a band but one is seriously lacking, they are awarded the lower end of that band. Quantity of points mentioned does not determine the mark — quality and depth do.

8. **Spelling errors explicitly do not affect marks**: The mark scheme includes a note that spelling errors should not impact marks awarded in any part of the examination.
