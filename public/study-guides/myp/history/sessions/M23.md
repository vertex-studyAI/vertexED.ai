## General examiner notes

- **Best-fit model**: The examiner chooses the achievement level that overall best matches the work. It is not necessary for every detailed aspect of a level to be met. If work spans multiple markbands, compensation depends on performance in higher-order skills.
- **Mark positively**: Award marks for what is correct; do not deduct for errors.
- **Spelling errors**: Must not impact marks awarded in any part of the examination.
- **Quality over quantity**: For most questions, the quality of the response is rewarded — not the number of strengths, limitations, or examples mentioned.

### Q4a (2 marks) — Formulate — Historical inquiry / National identity

**Question** (reconstructed from MS): You must now plan a new investigation on how national identity has been used in history. Your teacher has provided you with three statements of inquiry. Choose **one** of the following statements of inquiry for your investigation:
- National identity has often been used as a tool of propaganda in history.
- Leaders have used national identity as a tool to increase power.
- National identity has been used in history to bring society together.

Formulate a clear and focused research question to investigate your chosen statement of inquiry.

| Marks | Descriptor | Notes / Example answers |
|-------|------------|------------------------|
| 0 | Does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. **NOTE**: If the student simply rephrases the Statement of Inquiry then 0 marks should be awarded. E.g. "To what extent have leaders used national identity as a tool to increase power?" (which merely restates the SoI) = 0 marks. | |
| 1 | Student formulates a research question that is either **clear** or **focused** and connected to the statement of inquiry. | "To what extent have totalitarian leaders used national identity successfully to gain power?" — Clear (intention is understandable, connected to SoI) but lacks focus (no specific leader or location). / "Did the Indian national identity succeed in causing the independence in 1947?" — Focused (specific time and place) but lacks clarity ("succeed" is too vague). |
| 2 | Student formulates a research question that is both **clear and focused** and connected to the statement of inquiry. | "To what extent was Hitler's use of nationalism significant in his rise to power?" / "To what extent did Gandhi make use of Indian national identity when protesting against the British?" — Both are clear (intention is understandable) and focused (specific person, place or time). |

### Q5 (14 marks) — Explain / Extended writing — Biography / Historical change and daily life

**Question** (reconstructed from MS): You have been asked to contribute to a similar type of biography that looks at how the daily life of one unknown individual was impacted by a change. You will explain how the daily life of this individual was changed by one significant event in history.

You must:
- choose a significant event or time period that you have studied
- decide on the individual's identity, such as their name, nationality, age and profession.

Your biography must include:
- A title that informs the reader of the significant event and/or time period chosen
- Accurate historical information about daily life at the time
- Details about the changes made to the individual's daily life.

> **Criterion A — Knowledge and understanding (0–6 marks)**

> **NOTE**: If a student uses an actual historical figure the maximum marks they can receive is **3 marks**.

| Marks | Descriptor | Notes / Example answers |
|-------|------------|------------------------|
| 0 | Does not achieve a standard described by any of the descriptors given below. | |
| 1 | Student demonstrates **limited** knowledge and understanding by **outlining** how the daily life of an individual was changed by one significant event in history, using **limited** examples and **limited** relevant terminology. | Excerpt (Italian soldier after WW1): "When World War 1 ended, Luigi was only 22 and was unemployed. He was traumatized by the war. Luigi participated in the March on Rome, and helped Mussolini come to power." — Responses likely to be brief with little detail. Terminology may be limited in terms of accuracy and/or frequency. |
| 2–3 | Student demonstrates **adequate** knowledge and understanding by **describing** how the daily life of an individual was changed by one significant event in history, using **satisfactory** examples, and **appropriate** relevant terminology. | Excerpt (3-mark level): "When World War 1 ended, Luigi was only 22 and was unemployed. Mussolini said that violence was good and that they were going to destroy the communists. Like hundreds of other ex-servicemen in his city, Luigi was traumatized by the war. They were all angry at the government of Italy that had so poorly managed the war. Luigi was impressed by Mussolini's 'Blackshirts', who said they would restore Italy's power and get the land Italy was promised but never received in the Treaty of Versailles. Luigi agreed because after 2 years spent in the trenches, violence was all he knew. In 1922, Luigi participated in the March on Rome, and helped Mussolini come to power." — The response should be very detailed and provide clear reasoning, making use of examples. Comprehensive understanding of the significant event should be included. |

## Key patterns

### Topic coverage
1. **Latin American independence** (Q1–Q3): Source A focuses on Spanish colonial rule; Napoleon's invasion of Spain and its effect on the colonies; Argentina's Independence Day. Students need knowledge of independence movements globally (Q3 explicitly excludes Argentina, steering toward US, French Revolution, Indian independence, etc.).
2. **National identity and power** (Q4a–Q4d): Investigation planning around the SoIs: propaganda, consolidation of power, or social cohesion. Sample answers use Hitler/Germany and Gandhi/India as model examples.
3. **WWI and German imperialism** (Q6a–Q6c): Source B = primary source by von Bülow on German expansion; Source C = secondary source. The argument to develop is Germany's responsibility for WWI via expansionism → threat → alliances.
4. **International organisations** (Q7): Source D is a cartoon about the UN (nations "playing different games with different rules") — lack of unity and difficulty of cooperation.
5. **Superpowers and military conflict** (Q8): Broad essay requiring multiple perspectives — whether tensions always result in military conflict. "Superpowers" to be considered in a broad sense.

### Command term hierarchy (recurring across questions)
The mark scheme repeatedly demonstrates a clear progression:
- **State** (1 mark) → one sentence, hints at a characteristic/process/impact
- **Outline** (2 marks) → single sentence with brief elaboration / two clauses
- **Describe** (3 marks) → short paragraph, some detail, limited reasoning
- **Explain** (4 marks) → reasoning included, cause-and-effect clear
- **Justify** (4 marks) → argument + reasoning for why the argument holds
- **Evaluate** (8 marks) → strengths + limitations + overall appraisal

### Essay criteria structure (Q8 = 24 marks)
Q8 is the most heavily weighted question. Students must hit **four** separate criteria:
- **Criterion A** (8 marks): Contextual/conceptual knowledge — four levels (outline → describe → explain → thorough explanation)
- **C1 Format** (2 marks): Introduction + body + conclusion as separate paragraphs (0 marks if not separated)
- **C2 Style** (3 marks): Formal, clear, consistent essay language
- **C3 Organisation** (3 marks): Logical paragraph flow, effective transitions
- **Criterion D** (8 marks): Critical thinking — two balanced perspectives + synthesis → conclusion on extent

### Biography criteria structure (Q5 = 18 marks)
- **Criterion A** (6 marks): Accuracy, detail, reasoning about historical change on individual's daily life
- **IMPORTANT cap**: Actual historical figures max out at 3 marks (Criterion A); students must use a fictional/unknown individual to access full 6 marks
- **C1 Format** (2 marks): Must have title + focus on unknown individual + third-person narration (all three = 2 marks)
- **C2 Communication** (6 marks): Biography-appropriate style (not diary-like); 5–6 = consistently biographical
- **C3 Organisation** (4 marks): Logical structure, effective transitions; 4 = consistently effective

### Investigation planning (Q4 series — 12 marks)
- Q4a: RQ must be both **clear AND focused** — not just a restatement of the SoI (= 0 marks). Specificity about person/place/time earns the second mark.
- Q4b: Justifying RQ relevance to SoI escalates from stating a connection → describing factors → providing reasoning for why it is relevant.
- Q4c–Q4d: Source identification + explanation of utility follows the same state → outline → describe → explain progression.

### Source work principles
- **Q6b**: Verbatim quotes from the source cannot count as elaboration/additional detail.
- **Q6c**: Responses must refer only to the sources provided, not own knowledge. Referencing both sources is required for 3–4 marks.
- **Q7**: A description of Source D's content is explicitly not required — examiners should not award marks for description; the message/meaning is what matters.

### Best-fit marking
- The mark scheme applies a **best-fit model**: if a student meets 2 of 3 requirements in a markband, they are awarded that band (at the lower end).
- **Compensation across markbands**: if a student spans two markbands, the higher-order skills performance is decisive.
- **Quality over quantity**: stated throughout — one strong example/strength/limitation beats three weak ones.
