# Extended Writing (Criterion C)

Every paper includes a major extended-writing task worth 18 marks (Criterion A + C) and a final essay worth 24 marks (Criterion A + C + D). Criterion C has three sub-criteria: **C1 Format** (structural elements present), **C2 Communicating** (style and register match the format), and **C3 Organizational structure** (logical flow and transitions). Non-essay formats — speeches, podcast scripts, journal entries, social media responses — require fundamentally different writing styles from essays; defaulting to essay prose is one of the most common and costly errors.

---

## Key concepts

- **Criterion A (Knowing and Understanding)**: marks historical content — accuracy, use of named examples, depth of explanation; runs to 6 marks (18-mark tasks) or 8 marks (24-mark essays); at 6/8 marks, requires thorough explanation with accurate examples and reasoning throughout.
- **Criterion C1 (Format)**: element-counted (0, 1, or 2 marks), not quality-based — marks whether required structural features are present; for essays, all three (introduction, body, conclusion) must be in **separate paragraphs** — a wall of text with no paragraph breaks = C1 0 marks automatically.
- **Criterion C2 (Communicating)**: marks whether style and register match the format — writing an essay when the task demands a speech, podcast, or journal entry is capped at 3 marks; emotive language, rhetorical questions, and direct address in speeches earn 5–6 marks.
- **Criterion C3 (Organizational structure)**: marks logical flow — ideas must build on each other with transitions that show the *relationship* between arguments, not just introduce the next point; 4/4 requires this to be consistent throughout.
- **Criterion D (Thinking Critically)**: marks argument quality in essays only — runs to 8 marks; requires genuinely different perspectives developed with substantial evidence and a synthesis conclusion that states a specific position on "to what extent."
- **Register**: the style appropriate to a format — a persuasive speech needs direct address ("Dear..."), rhetorical questions, emotive language, and a call to action; a journal entry needs first-person and colloquial, reflective language; writing academic essay prose in any of these = C2 capped at 3 marks.
- **Synthesis**: combining perspectives into a conclusion that takes a position — required for Criterion D top band (7–8); "both sides have good points" scores nothing; must weigh which factors matter most and why.

### C2 register requirements — what each format demands

| Format | Required features for C2 5–6 marks | What caps you at 3/6 |
|--------|-------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Persuasive speech | Direct address ("Dear..."), emotive language, rhetorical questions, metaphor, call to action | Essay-style prose |
| Political commentary | Emotive language, hyperbole, rhetorical questions, first person throughout | Essay-style prose |
| Journal entries | First-person, colloquial, reflective, rhetorical questions, emotive vocabulary | Too formal; addressing an audience |
| Tour guide script | Direct address to group, references to artefacts, rhetorical questions, transitional language | Essay-style prose |
| Formal email | Formal, respectful tone, appropriate greeting and sign-off | Too casual or personal |
| Podcast script | Rhetorical questions, analogies, concise sentences, conversational engagement | Essay-style prose |
| Social media response | Formal/professional tone (official account, not personal), relevant hashtag | Too informal/personal |
| Biography | Third-person throughout, references to what the individual did/thought/felt | First-person; using a real historical figure |

### Criterion D — what synthesis looks like at the top band

At 7–8 marks, the mark scheme consistently requires:
- Two genuinely different perspectives, each with substantial development and evidence
- A conclusion that weighs which argument is stronger — not "both have merit"
- A specific statement of the degree of agreement: "to a large extent," "only partially," "not significantly"
- Synthesis can appear in the body or the conclusion — but it must exist somewhere

**What does NOT score at 7–8 marks:** presenting perspective A, then perspective B, then saying "in conclusion, both sides have good points." This is narrative, not synthesis.

## Mark scheme insights

### What examiners reward

- For essays: clear separation of introduction, body, and conclusion into distinct paragraphs (C1 requirement)
- For non-essay formats: consistent use of the required register throughout the entire response — not just at the start
- Specific historical examples with accurate details, causal reasoning, and relevant terminology throughout
- A synthesis conclusion that takes a clear position on the "to what extent" question with reasoning
- Criterion A at full marks: thorough explanations with accurate examples and reasoning throughout — not just in one paragraph

### What examiners penalise

- No paragraph breaks in essays — entire response as one block of text = C1 0 marks
- Essay prose in speeches, podcasts, or journal entries — caps C2 at 3/6 regardless of content quality
- Using a real historical figure in M23 biography — caps Criterion A at 3/6
- Missing required structural elements: no hashtag (M25 Q5), no sub-headings (N22 Q4), no visual aid (M24B Q5), no dates in journal entries (N23 Q5)
- One-sided essays on "to what extent" questions — second perspective must be developed, not just mentioned
- Visual aid in M24B/N20 that is never referred to in the written script — creates or refers to; must do both

## Common question types

1. **18-mark alternative format (C1 + C2 + C3 + A)** — always check: what format is required? What are the C1 structural elements? What register does C2 demand?
2. **24-mark essay (A + C1 + C2 + C3 + D)** — introduction + body + conclusion in separate paragraphs; two perspectives; synthesis conclusion with a clear position
3. **Format-specific tasks** — persuasive speech, podcast, social media response, journal entries; these are NOT essays and require fundamentally different prose
4. **Biography with fictional individual (M23 Q5)** — must invent a fictional person living through a real event; real historical figures cap Criterion A
5. **Visual aid + written explanation (M24B Q5, N20 Q5)** — must create the visual aid AND explicitly reference it in the written script

---

## Revision priorities

1. **C1 paragraph rule** — essay must have three separate paragraphs; one wall of text = 0 on C1; this is the most costly avoidable error
2. **Register for non-essay formats** — learn the defining features of each format; essay prose in a speech = 3/6 cap on C2
3. **Synthesis for Criterion D** — must take a clear position in the conclusion; "both sides have merit" does not score at the top band
4. **Check C1 elements before finishing** — essays: intro + body + conclusion; presentations: heading + 3 sub-headings; social media: greeting + hashtag; podcast: title + intro/ending
5. **Criterion A hard caps** — M23 biography with real person: A capped at 3/6; N22 Q8 one-sided (no negative): D capped; N21 Q7 missing conflict or movement: D capped
6. **Visual aid** — must be created AND referred to in the script; neither alone gets C1 marks
