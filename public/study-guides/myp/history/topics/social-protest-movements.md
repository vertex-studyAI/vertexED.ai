# Social Protest Movements

Social protest movements appear across multiple sessions through essays, source analysis, and persuasive extended writing. The topic covers: factors that make protest movements successful, methods of protest (violent vs. non-violent), the WTO Seattle protests (1999), civil rights, anti-apartheid, hip-hop as cultural protest, and the ideological movement/protest distinction. The M21 Q8 essay is the core assessment, asking whether gaining diverse support is the *most important* factor — which requires two fully developed counter-perspectives.

---

## Key concepts

- **Civil disobedience**: deliberate, non-violent refusal to obey unjust laws to force moral pressure on governments — Gandhi's Salt March 1930 and the Montgomery Bus Boycott 1955 are the model examples; their strategic power comes from making violent government responses politically costly.
- **WTO Seattle protests (1999)**: tens of thousands of protesters from labour rights, environmental, and human rights groups converged on Seattle in November 1999; the four-day disruption collapsed the WTO ministerial trade talks — Source B (M21) is an ABC News retrospective article written 20 years later by Gene Johnson, making it a secondary source.
- **Civil Rights Movement**: the US campaign for racial equality, 1954–1968; achieved the Civil Rights Act 1964 and Voting Rights Act 1965 through non-violent marches, boycotts, and sit-ins — the March on Washington 1963 photograph (Source D, M21) shows peaceful protest with placards; Source C (M21) shows the violent window-smashing at Seattle 1999, contrasting two protest methods.
- **Satyagraha**: Gandhi's concept of "truth-force" — the idea that non-violent resistance generates moral authority that armed force cannot — it influenced Martin Luther King Jr.'s approach and is the basis for the argument that non-violence is the most important factor in a movement's success.
- **Anti-apartheid movement**: the South African campaign against apartheid 1948–1994, combining ANC political resistance, international economic sanctions, and Mandela's cross-racial leadership; apartheid ended in 1994 and Mandela was elected the first Black president.
- **Hip-hop as cultural protest**: originating in African-American communities in New York in the 1970s, pioneered by DJ Kool Herc (a Jamaican musician who invented the breakbeat); "The Message" (1982) by Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five was the first major Conscious Rap song, critiquing urban poverty and racial inequality through music rather than marching (M22 Q1 evaluation task).
- **Gaining support from diverse groups**: the M21 Q8 essay question is specifically whether this is the *most important* factor — the Seattle protests succeeded partly because diverse causes united; the counter-argument is that Gandhi succeeded through discipline and moral authority, not breadth of support.

## Case studies

### WTO Seattle protests 1999 (M21)

- November 1999, Seattle, Washington; WTO ministerial conference aimed to create new global trade agreements
- Diverse groups united: labour rights (sweatshop conditions), environmentalists (sea turtles / trade rules), education privatization concerns, human rights groups
- Four days of protests disrupted the conference; talks collapsed
- Long-term legacy: raised global awareness of WTO implications; influenced later anti-globalization movements; cited by politicians including Bernie Sanders
- Source B (M21): ABC News retrospective article by Gene Johnson (Associated Press), written November 2019 — secondary source, 20 years after the event; value: long-term perspective; limitation: journalist was not a participant, cannot provide first-hand experience

### M21 Q7 — Sources C and D compared

| Feature | Source C (Kiro 7 TV, Seattle 1999) | Source D (March on Washington, 1963) |
|---------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Type | Primary — eyewitness news broadcast transcript | Primary — photograph |
| Methods shown | Violence, window-smashing, police clashes, tear gas | Peaceful march with placards |
| Tone | Emotive ("the smell of tear gas", "Seattle under siege") | Visual only; peaceful atmosphere |
| Context | WTO anti-globalization protest | US Civil Rights Movement |

**Cap rule**: Responses covering only similarities OR only differences = maximum 4 out of 6 marks.

### Hip-hop as cultural protest (M22 Q1)

- Origins: New York, South Bronx, 1970s–1980s; emerged from African-American communities
- DJ Kool Herc (Jamaican-American): invented the breakbeat technique in New York; pioneered hip-hop
- "The Message" (1982) by Grandmaster Flash and the Furious Five: first major Conscious Rap song; lyrics about urban poverty, crime, racial inequality
- Resources in M22 Q1: "The Message" song (primary), music history book (secondary), DJ Kool Herc interview (primary), New York street photos (primary)
- **Key strength**: mix of primary and secondary sources
- **Key limitation**: the history book (Robert P. Morgan, *Twentieth-Century Music*) covers all of European and American 20th-century music — will have very little detail on hip-hop specifically

### Factors making protest movements successful

| Factor | Example | Counter-argument |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Gaining diverse support | WTO Seattle: labour + environment + human rights united | Gandhi succeeded with disciplined movement, not breadth |
| Methods used (non-violence) | Gandhi's satyagraha; MLK's marches gave moral authority | Seattle's window-smashing damaged reputation but still succeeded |
| Time period / political climate | Civil Rights Movement benefited from Cold War pressure on US | Timing is external; not something movements control |
| Justice of the cause | Public sympathy for civil rights created momentum | Gaining support may follow naturally from a just cause |

- M21 Q8 without a specific named protest movement — generic discussion cannot reach top marks

- M21 Q7: only similarities or only differences — capped at 4/6
- M22 Q3 speech written like an essay — C2 capped at 3/6
- Confusing protest movement with ideological movement in M22 Q3 — task requires an intellectual or ideological movement (Nazism, Communism, the Enlightenment), not a protest movement

## Key arguments

### M21 Q8 — Is gaining diverse support the most important factor in making a protest movement successful?

**YES — diverse support is most important:**
- The WTO Seattle protests succeeded in collapsing the conference precisely because tens of thousands from unrelated causes (labour, environment, human rights) united around a common target
- Mandela's anti-apartheid movement gained international support across political and racial lines — without external economic pressure (sanctions), internal resistance alone might not have succeeded
- A movement without broad support lacks the numbers and moral authority to force change

**NO — other factors matter more:**
- Gandhi's independence movement succeeded not through breadth of support but through the strategic coherence of satyagraha — discipline and moral authority made British violent responses counterproductive
- The US Civil Rights Movement succeeded in the 1960s partly because Cold War politics made US racial segregation internationally embarrassing — the time period and political climate were decisive
- The justice of the cause creates support; gaining support is the consequence of a just cause, not the cause itself

**Model conclusion:** Gaining diverse support is a necessary condition for large-scale protest movements, but it is not sufficient on its own — and it is not always the most important factor. Gandhi's Indian independence movement demonstrated that strategic coherence and moral authority can achieve more than mass numbers. The WTO protests demonstrated that mass numbers can achieve disruption. The most successful movements combine both: the civil rights movement had millions of supporters AND the strategic discipline of non-violence AND the political timing of Cold War pressure. To identify any one of these as the single most important factor misunderstands how successful protest movements actually work.
