# History — 2-Week Revision Plan

**Exam**: MYP History on-screen examination (~70–82 marks, ~13 questions)
**Constraint**: Shared revision time across multiple subjects — prioritise high-frequency patterns

---

## Strategy

Every single paper has:
1. **Criterion B cluster** (research question + justification + sources + evaluation) — ~12–20 marks
2. **Source analysis** (origin, purpose, value/limitation, compare/contrast) — ~6–12 marks
3. **Extended creative writing** (essay, speech, article, journal, podcast…) — ~18 marks
4. **Big essay** ("To what extent" or "Discuss") — 24 marks

These four components account for **~75% of your total marks**. Master the formulas before memorising content.

---

## Week 1 — Skills and high-frequency topics

### Day 1 — Criterion B formula
- Read: [research-methods.md](topics/research-methods.md)
- Practise writing **3 research questions** — one must be clear AND focused (name a person, place, or time period). Reject any that just rephrase the Statement of Inquiry.
- Practise writing a **4-mark justification**: state relevance → outline connection → describe why it matters → justify with reasoning.
- Memorise the Evaluate structure: **Strengths (0–3) + Limitations (0–3) + Appraisal (0–2) = 8 marks**. Strengths-only or limitations-only caps at 3.

### Day 2 — Source analysis (Criterion D)
- Read: [source-analysis.md](topics/source-analysis.md)
- For any source: practise **Origin** (who made it, when, where) + **Purpose** (what it is trying to do — *never* write "to inform an audience") + **Value** (how origin/purpose makes it useful) + **Limitation** (what it can't tell us because of origin/purpose).
- Practise compare/contrast: always cover **both** similarities AND differences. One-sided = capped at 4/6 or 4/8.
- Do one full source pair from the question bank (e.g. N22 NOAA/Exxon or M25 washing machine/frozen dinner).

### Day 3 — Extended writing formats (Criterion C)
- Read: [extended-writing.md](topics/extended-writing.md)
- Learn the **format requirements** for: formal essay, persuasive speech, journal entries (×3), podcast script, biography, social media response. One structural feature missing = 1 mark lost on C1.
- For every format: write one opening paragraph practising appropriate register (emotive for speech, reflective for journal, conversational for podcast, professional for social media).
- C3 tip: always include transitional phrases between paragraphs ("This demonstrates…", "In contrast…", "Building on this…").

### Day 4 — Scientific and technical innovation (Tier 1 topic)
- Read: [scientific-innovation.md](topics/scientific-innovation.md)
- Prepare **3 case studies** in depth (e.g. wristwatch + WWI, washing machine + women's liberation, penicillin/Black Death, printing press). For each: context, the innovation, short-term impact, long-term impact.
- These appear in 12 of 13 sessions — you will almost certainly get a question on this.

### Day 5 — Independence movements and national identity (Tier 2 topic)
- Read: [independence-movements.md](topics/independence-movements.md)
- Know **2 movements in depth**: Jallianwala Bagh / Indian independence and Ghana (Nkrumah) are the most examined. For each: causes, key event, outcome, how it built national identity.
- Practise a "discuss" paragraph: argument for → counter-argument → mini-conclusion linking back to the question.

### Day 6 — Industrialization (Tier 2 topic)
- Read: [industrialization.md](topics/industrialization.md)
- Know the **4 causes** of the Industrial Revolution (economic, technological, political, social) for Britain.
- Know key **human impacts**: child labour (Factory Acts), urbanisation, environmental damage.
- Know the Japan industrialization angle (Meiji era) as a contrast.

### Day 7 — Rest / light review
- Skim [overview.md](overview.md) — topic frequency table and Revision Priorities
- Re-read your command-terms card: State / Outline / Describe / Explain / Evaluate / Compare and contrast. Internalize what each asks for.
- Optional: do Q1–Q4 from one past session (N24 or M25) to feel the rhythm.

## Week 2 — Essay technique, remaining topics, past-paper practice

### Day 8 — Big essay formula (Criterion A + C + D)
- Read: [extended-writing.md](topics/extended-writing.md) — essay section
- Structure for every "To what extent" or "Discuss" question:
  - **Intro**: define the question, briefly state your two perspectives, hint at your conclusion
  - **Para 1**: Perspective A + named example + analytical sentence
  - **Para 2**: Perspective B (counter-argument) + named example + analytical sentence
  - **Conclusion**: synthesis — which perspective outweighs the other and why
- Write one timed intro + conclusion (~15 min) on a question from the question bank.

### Day 9 — Social protest movements + significant individuals (Tier 2–3)
- Read: [social-protest-movements.md](topics/social-protest-movements.md) and [significant-individuals.md](topics/significant-individuals.md)
- Social protest: WTO Seattle 1999, hip-hop as cultural protest. Know: causes → methods (violent/non-violent) → outcomes → what made it succeed or fail.
- Significant individuals: Mandela and Stalin contrast is the highest-yield pair. Know: context, actions, short-term impact, long-term legacy, how history judged them.

### Day 10 — Empires, globalization, supranational organisations (Tier 3)
- Read: [empires-and-colonialism.md](topics/empires-and-colonialism.md), [globalization.md](topics/globalization.md), [supranational-organisations.md](topics/supranational-organisations.md)
- Empires: British Empire in India + Columbus/Spanish colonialism. Know both the "stability and development" argument AND the exploitation/resistance counter.
- Globalization: Silk Road + Atlantic slave trade as historical examples. Benefits (cultural exchange, economic growth) vs. drawbacks (exploitation, loss of identity).
- Supranational orgs: UN (structure + peacekeeping successes and failures), WTO (Seattle protests), NATO.

### Day 11 — Past paper full simulation: N24
- Do the **full N24 paper** under timed conditions (~90 min)
- Check against the mark scheme for:
  - Criterion B: Was your RQ both clear AND focused? Did your evaluation have strengths + limitations + appraisal?
  - Source analysis: Did you reference origin OR purpose in your value/limitation?
  - Essay: Did you cover two perspectives and synthesise?

### Day 12 — Past paper full simulation: M25
- Do the **full M25 paper** under timed conditions
- Focus on command terms you dropped marks on in N24. If you lost marks on "Explain", practise writing "because…" causal chains. If you lost marks on "Evaluate", check your appraisal is present.

### Day 13 — Ideological movements + final weak spots
- Read: [ideological-movements.md](topics/ideological-movements.md)
- Know Fascism, Nazism, Communism, Maoism: key beliefs, rise to power, impact, how propaganda/persuasion was used.
- Use remaining time to re-read any topic page you feel uncertain about.

### Day 14 — Final review
- Re-read [command-terms.md](command-terms.md)
- Re-read the **Mark scheme universal rules** section of overview.md (best-fit model, cap rules, quality over quantity)
- Skim the creative writing format table — make sure you can instantly recall what each format needs
- Rest and sleep well.

## Quick-reference: format checklist

| Format | Must include |
|--------|-------------|
| Essay | Intro + body paragraphs + conclusion; formal language; transitions |
| Persuasive speech | Opening statement; emotive language; rhetorical questions; call to action |
| Journal entries (×3) | Three dated entries; first-person; reflective tone; emotional language |
| Podcast script | Title; conversational greeting; rhetorical questions; sign-off |
| Biography | Title; third-person; chronological; focus on impact and legacy |
| Social media response | Official greeting; original hashtag; professional/appropriate tone |
| Formal email | Greeting (Dear…); body paragraphs; formal sign-off |
| Presentation | Heading + sub-headings; visual reference; concluding statement |

## Critical rules to never forget

- **Criterion B RQ**: must be BOTH clear (reader understands intent) AND focused (specific person/place/time). Rephrasing the SoI = 0 marks.
- **Evaluate**: strengths-only or limitations-only caps at 3/8. You need strengths + limitations + appraisal for 7–8.
- **Compare and contrast**: must cover both similarities AND differences. One-sided caps at 4/6 or 4/8.
- **Source purpose**: "to inform an audience" is always rejected. Be specific — "to persuade British workers to support the strike" or "to celebrate the achievements of the empire for a colonial audience".
- **Essay**: one perspective only caps at 3–4 for Criterion D. Always include counter-argument.
- **Spelling errors are never penalised** (stated explicitly from M21 onwards).
